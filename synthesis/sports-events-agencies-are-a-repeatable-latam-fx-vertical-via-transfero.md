# Sports/events travel agencies are a repeatable LatAm multi-corridor FX vertical

> Absolut Sport's BRZ-rails setup is structurally identical to Etherfuse's own FMCG/freight/PSP FX case studies, making CONMEBOL-scale sports and events packagers a clean repeatable vertical reachable through Etherfuse's existing Transfero investor channel — with the bonus that BRZ earns no yield where Etherfuse's stack does.

## The pattern Etherfuse already sells, sitting unworked in a named live customer

Absolut Sport is the official packager for CONMEBOL Copa Libertadores and Sudamericana finals, running 500+ events and 10,000+ trips per year with offices in Frankfurt, Los Angeles, Mexico City, Bogotá and Buenos Aires, settling five corridors (EUR/USD/MXN/COP/ARS) on Transfero/BRZ rails for ~$600K/yr in FX savings and same-day settlement [shared/partners/transfero-absolut-sport-case-study.md].

Strip the sport away and the shape is exactly Etherfuse's standing FX case-study playbook: a multi-country operator accumulating several local currencies, eliminating correspondent-banking spreads, and converging on a single home-currency settlement. That is the same structure as the Rotterdam freight operator across MENA [shared/bd/case-study-freight-eu-egypt-mena.md], the Dubai FMCG distributor across Angola/Nigeria [shared/bd/case-study-fmcg-dubai-africa.md], and the Hong Kong PSP across SE Asia [shared/bd/case-study-psp-hongkong.md]. Etherfuse has already proven and documented this exact motion repeatedly — it just hasn't named the sports/events segment as a vertical, even though the case study's own Open Questions ask "are there other sports/events verticals in LatAm with similar payment corridor needs?" [shared/partners/transfero-absolut-sport-case-study.md].

## Why this is reachable now, cheaply

- **The channel is warm and pre-existing.** Transfero is an Etherfuse strategic investor and the primary relationship holder on this customer [wiki/entities/companies/Transfero.md]. Absolut Sport sits inside Transfero's B2B payment book; replicating it across other CONMEBOL/event packagers is a referral conversation with an investor, not cold BD.
- **The corridors already map to live product.** EUR→EUROB, USD→USTRY, MXN→CETES/MXNe are all live Etherfuse Stablebonds with native MXN SPEI rails [shared/bond-roadmap.md][shared/overview.md]. Three of the five corridors are immediately serviceable.
- **The yield gap is Etherfuse's edge.** BRZ has no yield distribution — reserves earn for Transfero, not holders [wiki/entities/companies/Transfero.md]. Every one of Etherfuse's analogous case studies leads with yield on idle settlement float (the HK PSP earns ~4.5% APY on float that previously earned 0% [shared/bd/case-study-psp-hongkong.md]). A sports/events packager holding multi-corridor float between vendor payments is precisely that idle-float profile, and Etherfuse's TESOURO (~13% BRL) plus the float-yield products are the differentiator BRZ rails alone cannot offer.

## The gap to close

Two of Absolut Sport's five live corridors — COP (Bogotá) and ARS (Buenos Aires) — have no Etherfuse Stablebond and appear nowhere on the bond roadmap [shared/bond-roadmap.md], reinforcing (with a fresh, named, at-scale customer) the Colombia and Argentina roadmap blind spots flagged elsewhere. A sports/events vertical thesis is the demand-side justification to prioritize COP/ARS issuance: the corridors aren't speculative, they are already moving real B2B volume through a partner's rails today.
