# Allo is the ready-built wrapper + US-access vehicle for the Stablebond index others already requested

> Allo has already drafted the exact diversified Stablebond SPV that NIC NBK independently asked for, but unlike a pure fund-wrapper it bundles in-house administration plus 20,000+ HNW investors — and its fund structure is also the cleanest legal route to give US investors exposure to Reg-S Stablebonds they cannot hold directly.

Three independent threads converge on one product. In the May 26 Kazakhstan meeting, NIC NBK asked about on-chain index funds and Dave proposed "an index of Etherfuse stable bonds = ~6% blended yield, diversified across strong and emerging market currencies" [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Allo independently proposed the *same instrument*, already spec'd to product level: an "Allocations Emerging Markets Yield Fund powered by Etherfuse" — a diversified portfolio across MXNM, BRLM, GBPM, EURM, KRWM targeting 8–10% blended, with Allo handling fund admin and Etherfuse providing the underlying assets [wiki/entities/companies/alloxyz.md].

What makes Allo distinct from a generic wrapper partner is verticalization: it is simultaneously the largest RWA marketplace ($2.2B+, 1,600+ RWAs) AND a fund-administration platform running $2B+ AUM across 1,500+ funds with 20,000+ HNW investors, at 10–70x industry efficiency [wiki/entities/companies/alloxyz.md]. So a single deal supplies the legal wrapper, the admin machinery, AND the distribution channel in one entity — no separate manager-plus-distributor stack required.

The non-obvious kicker is the US-market angle. Etherfuse's Stablebonds are sold under Reg S — non-US persons only [shared/overview.md]. Allo's HNW base and fund roster (a16z, General Catalyst, Khosla, IVP) skew heavily US, meaning those investors legally cannot hold Stablebond tokens directly. A US-domiciled SPV that holds the Stablebonds and issues fund interests is the standard mechanism by which US investors get exposure to offshore Reg-S instruments — so Allo's proposed fund is not just a yield product, it is the compliance vehicle that converts an otherwise-blocked US audience into addressable demand. Verify the exact Reg-S/fund-interest structure with counsel before pitching, but the wrapper is precisely what unlocks the constituency Etherfuse otherwise can't sell to.

Product-design note: Allo's 8–10% blended target is materially above Dave's ~6% framing and is only achievable by weighting toward high-yield EM legs (TESOURO ~13%) [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md]; align the index weights to the marketed yield before the SPV launches. Allo's deal also sits stale — due 2026-04-21 with no documented meeting outcome [wiki/entities/companies/alloxyz.md] — so this is a recoverable, high-ceiling re-engagement, not a cold start.
