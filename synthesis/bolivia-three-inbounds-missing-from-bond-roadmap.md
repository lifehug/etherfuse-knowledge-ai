# Bolivia: three same-day inbounds, zero roadmap presence, a Muru intro already in hand

> Bolivia drew three simultaneous inbound requests for stablecoin access (AirTM, LI.FI, and a third party) plus a penciled Q3 2026 AirTM launch — yet a BOB Stablebond appears nowhere on Etherfuse's official bond roadmap, making it the same unworked easy win as Colombia/Argentina but with the warmest demand signal yet.

The May 29 AirTM sync records that **Bolivia was flagged as a priority market with three simultaneous inbound requests — LI.FI, AirTM, and a third party — on the same day**, and that AirTM is already powering Muru's payments in Bolivia; the team's explicit decision was to "double efforts to unlock Bolivia" [shared/partners/2026-05-29-airtm-etherfuse-sync.md]. Crucially, the same note says **Etherfuse already has a contact at Muru** — the on-the-ground processor AirTM runs through — so the distribution intro is near-zero cost [shared/partners/2026-05-29-airtm-etherfuse-sync.md].

This demand is corroborated and not a one-off: AirTM's own partnership proposal lists Bolivia as a Q3 2026 priority country with a planned new Stablebond issuance backed by AirTM's P2P ramp network [shared/partners/airtm-partnership-proposal.md], and Dave's follow-up email to Ruben names Bolivia first in AirTM's 2026 market focus alongside Argentina, Mexico, Brazil, and Colombia [shared/partners/airtm-followup-email.md].

Yet Bolivia is absent everywhere it should appear. The 2026 expansion pipeline in the master KB lists PHP, MYR, GHS, IDR, VND, KES, HKD, and AED/SAR — **no boliviano (BOB)** [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. This is exactly the scheduling blind spot already flagged for Colombia and Argentina, except Bolivia's signal is sharper: three inbound parties at once, a co-owned JV (AirTM × Etherfuse BDA Ltd.) about to ship under a Bermuda license where new-currency Stablebonds are added by **notification only, no re-application** [shared/partners/2026-05-29-airtm-etherfuse-sync.md], and an existing Muru relationship to source local rails. Bolivia's structural pull — heavy dollarization demand under currency controls — is precisely the underserved-market profile the company optimizes for. The action is to put BOB on the roadmap and route it through the AirTM JV + Muru, activating three partners with one issuance.
