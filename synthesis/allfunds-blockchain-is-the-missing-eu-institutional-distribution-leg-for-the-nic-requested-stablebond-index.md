# Allfunds Blockchain is the missing EU distribution leg for the Stablebond index fund

> The Stablebond index fund NIC NBK asked for has candidate wrappers and allocators being designed but no named European institutional distribution channel — and Allfunds Blockchain, the blockchain arm of one of Europe's largest fund distribution platforms, is exactly that missing rail.

In the May 26 NIC NBK meeting, NIC asked specifically about on-chain index funds, and Dave proposed 'an index of Etherfuse stable bonds = ~6% blended yield, diversified across strong and emerging market currencies' [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Existing findings already map the manufacturing side of that product — regulated wrappers (3iQ/Allo) and LP allocators (Aaro) — but the distribution side into European institutions is unnamed.

Allfunds Blockchain fills that gap. It is the blockchain subsidiary of Allfunds, which 'operates a broad network of asset managers and institutional investors across Europe,' positioned precisely 'at the intersection of traditional fund distribution and blockchain-native infrastructure' [wiki/entities/companies/allfunds-blockchain.md]. That is a distinct role from a fund wrapper or a capital allocator: it is the pipe that puts a fund product in front of the European institutional buyers who would hold it. A blended-yield Stablebond index is a fund-shaped product, which is exactly what Allfunds' rails are built to distribute.

Because the index would combine strong-currency (EUROB, GILTS, USTRY) and EM (CETES, TESOURO, KTB) sleeves [shared/bond-roadmap.md], and Allfunds already sits inside a regulated, familiar fund-distribution framework its buyers trust [wiki/entities/companies/allfunds-blockchain.md], it lets Etherfuse reach institutional demand 'rather than building distribution from scratch.' The action is to route the index-fund workstream (currently a Kazakhstan-origin request) through Allfunds Blockchain as its European retail-of-institutions channel, closing the last unassigned leg of the index thesis.
