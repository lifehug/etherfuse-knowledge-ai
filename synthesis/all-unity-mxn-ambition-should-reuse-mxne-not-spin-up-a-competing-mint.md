# All Unity's MXN stablecoin should wrap existing MXNe, not duplicate it under BaFin

> All Unity wants to issue a CETES-backed MXN stablecoin from Germany, but Etherfuse already co-issues exactly that asset (MXNe via Brale) on the same collateral — so the fast, non-fragmenting play is to distribute MXNe into the EUR–MXN corridor rather than help All Unity stand up a fifth competing MXN coin behind a ~3-month BaFin approval.

All Unity 'confirmed interest in issuing MXN stablecoin from Germany' backed by CETES, targeting EUR-MXN remittance flows that Bitso cannot reach from Europe — but gated by a BaFin-only constraint requiring ~3 months of approval per new currency [wiki/entities/companies/all-unity.md]. Etherfuse, however, already ships the identical product: **MXNe, a peso stablecoin backed by CETES (not fiat reserves)**, co-issued with Brale and live on Solana, Base, Polygon and Stellar [wiki/markets/mexico-cetes.md; shared/partners/2023-12-14-brale-mxne-proposal.md].

Standing up a separate BaFin-licensed MXN coin on the same CETES collateral fragments peso liquidity across yet another wrapper — the exact multiplication risk already visible across Brale, AirTM, Alfred Pay and Bello all minting branded local coins on shared Etherfuse backing. The faster, higher-margin route is to make All Unity a **distribution/redemption channel for MXNe in the EU** (its Clearstream custody and Stripe/Privy embedded-wallet reach are the European distribution Etherfuse lacks), capturing the EUR-MXN corridor immediately rather than after a BaFin cycle. The Brale revenue-share tiers (90% to Etherfuse above $50M market cap) [shared/partners/2023-12-14-brale-mxne-proposal.md] already make incremental MXNe volume strongly accretive, so routing All Unity's EUR-MXN demand through MXNe is better for Etherfuse than helping mint a parallel coin Etherfuse would only partly economically own.

**Action:** reframe the All Unity MXN discussion from 'we'll help you issue an MXN coin' to 'distribute MXNe in the EU now, and reserve BaFin issuance only for cases where a German-domiciled e-money token is legally required.'
