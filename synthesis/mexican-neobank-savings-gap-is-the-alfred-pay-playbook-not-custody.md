# Albo + Plata: the Mexican neobank savings gap is a copy-paste Alfred Pay deal

> Albo and Plata are two Mexican consumer neobanks sitting on file with zero engagement, both structurally missing the savings/yield product their rivals already have — exactly the gap the signed Alfred Pay 'MXN balance/savings account' structure was built to fill, and the AVE bill makes Etherfuse's CETES yield their only legal differentiator.

## The unworked cluster

Albo is a Mexican neobank (debit cards, savings, mobile-first) parked at **Wishlist with zero contacts and 'identify contacts and initiate outreach' as the only next step** [wiki/entities/companies/albo.md]. The Albo page frames the pitch generically as 'CETES-backed Stablebonds as a yield/savings product.' Two other sources in this neighborhood turn that generic frame into a precise, already-proven deal structure.

## The savings gap is documented, and it's competitive

Plata — a CNBV-licensed Mexican digital bank with 2.5M+ customers, $3.1B valuation, and a $500M credit facility — has **'No savings/yield product visible yet despite banking license,'** and the research explicitly flags that **'Nubank Mexico and Klar both have savings features. Plata needs a savings/yield product to compete — notable gap'** [shared/partners/plata-research.md]. Albo names the identical competitor set — 'Klar, Uala, and others in our pipeline' [wiki/entities/companies/albo.md]. This is not one prospect; it is a whole tier of Mexican consumer neobanks where a savings product is a competitive necessity and Etherfuse ships the only regulated peso yield engine.

This is a **distinct archetype** from the consumer-lender thesis (Tala/Addi, idle loan reserves) and the brokerage-custodian thesis (Actinver/Kuspit, existing client books): these are deposit-and-card neobanks that need a *customer-facing* savings feature, not treasury yield on their own balance sheet.

## The structure already exists — and so does the framing

The Alfred Pay deal is the template these neobanks need. Alfred wanted to 'issue a branded MXN balance product (Cetes-backed) using Etherfuse as collateral infrastructure — without building a brokerage from scratch,' with a **100% yield to Alfred if retained internally, 50/50 brokerage split if passed to users**, and 1:1 wrap/unwrap between balance, stablecoin, and yield from day one [shared/partners/alfred-pay-2026-04-22.md]. Crucially, Dave's positioning advice maps exactly onto a neobank like Albo or Plata: **'position as MXN balance / savings account rather than emphasizing stablecoin — customers care about liquidity and yield, not the mechanics'** [shared/partners/alfred-pay-2026-04-22.md]. The Brale MXNe table gives the exact economics to drop in (10%→90% revenue share scaling with market cap) [shared/partners/2023-12-14-brale-mxne-proposal.md], and the Baubap/case-study work shows the SPEI-ramp + Pago46 retail plumbing is already live for any Mexican neobank's banked and unbanked users [shared/partners/baubap-integration.md].

## Why now: the AVE bill makes Etherfuse the only legal yield play

Mexico's AVE framework creates a clean two-track system: payment stablecoins (zero yield) vs. yield-bearing tokenized securities. A neobank can launch its own zero-yield AVE, but **'Etherfuse's moat is yield — which AVEs structurally cannot offer'** [shared/products/2026-05-12-murat-bill-stablebond-mxne-positioning.md]. So the moment these neobanks move on savings, the regulated peso-yield supplier is structurally Etherfuse. The window to lock Albo/Plata as distribution before they pick a competitor or build a bare AVE is open now, and the deal is near-zero-cost: identify a contact and pitch the Alfred 'savings account' structure verbatim, not a 'CETES custody' deal.
