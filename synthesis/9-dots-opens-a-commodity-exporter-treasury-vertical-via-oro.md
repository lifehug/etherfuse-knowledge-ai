# 9 Dots opens a commodity-exporter treasury vertical sourced through ORO

> 9 Dots' gold/silver structure — USD/USDC in from buyers, sovereign-yield treasury in the middle, local-currency payouts to providers — is structurally identical to three existing Etherfuse case studies, making precious-metals and commodity exporters a clean repeatable vertical that the ORO referral channel is already feeding.

**The structure is a known template, not a bespoke deal.** 9 Dots' agreed flow — the Japan entity "receives USDC/USDT from gold/silver buyers and holds CETES/USTRY treasury," while the Mexican entity "receives Stablebonds... liquidates to MXN, and pays local providers" [wiki/entities/companies/9-dots.md] — is the same USD-revenue / yield-bearing-middle / local-currency-payout shape Etherfuse already proves in the Dubai FMCG case (USD HQ, NGN/AoA operations) [shared/bd/case-study-fmcg-dubai-africa.md], the Rotterdam freight case (EUR HQ, EGP/AED/SAR/JOD operations) [shared/bd/case-study-freight-eu-egypt-mena.md], and the corporate-treasury MXN study [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md]. The only new variable is the sector: extractive commodities, where revenue is intrinsically USD-priced and costs are intrinsically local-currency — the cleanest possible fit for the model.

**The vertical is underserved and high-friction.** 9 Dots itself describes precious-metals mining as a sector where "traditional financial rails are particularly friction-heavy," with KYC-driven exclusion from Mexican investment products and a high-risk-jurisdiction perception making bank channels unworkable [wiki/entities/companies/9-dots.md]. That friction is the moat: commodity exporters in Mexico/LatAm have a recurring, structurally identical treasury problem and few alternatives.

**ORO is already the channel.** 9 Dots reached Etherfuse not via outbound BD but through the ORO partnership network — Usman of ORO introduced it via Telegram [wiki/entities/companies/9-dots.md]. A gold-adjacent referral source delivering a gold-mining customer is a signal that ORO can source more of the same template, the way Brogan Law functions as a warm-intro engine elsewhere in the graph. The actionable move: codify the 9 Dots flow as a 'commodity-exporter treasury' one-pager (mirroring the existing FMCG/freight case studies) and explicitly task ORO with sourcing the next mining/metals deals into it, rather than treating 9 Dots as a single won account.
