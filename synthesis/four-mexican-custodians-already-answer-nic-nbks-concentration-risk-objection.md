# Etherfuse's 4 CETES custodians already rebut NIC NBK's concentration-risk objection

> NIC NBK's single live objection to the deal was single-counterparty concentration risk, which Dave defended only on the 'BBVA' model — but Etherfuse already custodies CETES across four diversified Category I Mexican institutions (BBVA, Actinver, Kuspit, Casa de Bolsa BASE), an unused multi-custodian answer that directly neutralizes the objection.

**The objection.** In the May 2026 pitch, NIC NBK — a National Bank of Kazakhstan fund-of-funds with *high* interest whose only stated bottleneck is regulatory/custody infrastructure, not product fit — raised one substantive pushback: they hold Mexican sovereigns via 10–15 large global banks and questioned whether Etherfuse's "single-counterparty model (BBVA in Mexico)" creates concentration risk versus a traditional multi-bank setup. Dave pushed back on liquidity/settlement grounds, and the meeting left an open action item: "Do counterparty risk research: Etherfuse single local partner vs multi-bank traditional setup" [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md].

**The unused answer is already in the custodian roster.** Etherfuse does *not* in fact rely on a single Mexican counterparty. CETES are custodied across **four** Category I Mexican institutions — BBVA, Casa de Bolsa BASE, Kuspit, and Actinver [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. Actinver alone is documented as an active Category I custodian (15.23% capitalization index, no solvency problems per CNBV) holding Etherfuse customer financial assets [wiki/entities/companies/actinver-casa-de-bolsa.md]. So the truthful answer to NIC's concentration question is the opposite of how the objection was framed: the backing is spread across four regulated, independently-rated custodians, each with bankruptcy-remote asset-separation rights under Mexican law [shared/bd/website-asset-protection-rewrite.md].

**Why this matters for revenue/speed.** This is the cleanest, fastest path to convert the single highest-intent institutional prospect in the Kazakhstan neighborhood: the DDQ response and the open counterparty-risk research item can be closed by reframing the Mexican custody story as *multi-custodian diversification* rather than "BBVA." It's also reusable verbatim against every future institutional DD that raises the same concern.

**One dependency.** The customer-facing legal/DD copy currently names only three of the four custodians (BBVA, Actinver, Kuspit), omitting Casa de Bolsa BASE [shared/bd/website-asset-protection-rewrite.md, shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md] — so the documents understate the very diversification that answers NIC's objection. Fix the count before leading with the four-custodian rebuttal, or the strongest counterparty-risk argument is contradicted by Etherfuse's own paperwork.
