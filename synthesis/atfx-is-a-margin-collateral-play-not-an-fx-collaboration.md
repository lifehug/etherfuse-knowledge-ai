# ATFX is a margin-collateral play, not an 'FX collaboration'

> ATFX is an Asia-based global FX broker-dealer whose core business requires holding massive segregated client margin deposits — the exact idle-float-to-yield conversion Etherfuse already ships in the PSP case study — and Shinhan AM just quantified the Asia-originated derivatives collateral market at $100B+, making 'yield-bearing margin collateral' a concrete re-engagement hook for a lead that stalled because 'FX collaboration' was too vague to act on.

## The problem with the current framing

The ATFX wiki page logs the opportunity as a generic 'FX collaboration (specifics TBD)' based on a single October 2025 phone call, with no follow-through in eight months [wiki/entities/companies/atfx.md]. The deal has no priority, no offering, and an overdue next-step of 'resume outreach with Mohammed Khan.' The follow-up framework explicitly warns against exactly this kind of vague re-engagement — 'Never just check in... always provide value or a reason to respond' [shared/bd/followup-framework.md].

## What ATFX actually is

ATFX is a retail/institutional FX broker-dealer with global operations centered on Asia and a Mexico City presence [wiki/entities/companies/atfx.md]. FX broker-dealers hold segregated client margin deposits as a regulatory requirement — capital that sits idle in bank accounts earning minimal yield while backing open trading positions. This is structurally identical to the PSP settlement float described in the Hong Kong case study, where $3M in idle settlement float was converted to USTRY at ~4.5% APY, generating ~$135K/year [shared/bd/case-study-psp-hongkong.md].

## The scale of the opportunity

Shinhan Asset Management's C-level (Heyryung Choi, former CS/UBS quant head) quantified that **$180 billion in stablecoins left Korea last year** specifically to access offshore derivatives markets, with at least **$100B attributable to derivatives collateral** [shared/partners/2026-06-04-shinhan-asset-management-clevel.md]. FX broker-dealers like ATFX are the venues where this collateral gets posted. Etherfuse's own sales objection handler explicitly positions Stablebonds as derivatives and trading collateral: 'Use it as collateral on-chain, plug it into lending protocols... your collateral earns 7–13% while you borrow at 5%' [shared/bd/why-stablebonds-vs-direct-bonds.md].

## The re-engagement pitch

Instead of a vague FX check-in, the outreach to Mohammed Khan should lead with a specific value proposition: **Stablebonds as yield-bearing margin collateral for ATFX's client deposits**. The pitch maps onto three concrete products:

1. **USTRY (~3.2% APY)** for USD-denominated margin held across ATFX's global operations [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]
2. **CETES (~5.5% APY)** for MXN margin held at ATFX's Mexico City operation [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]
3. **Q3 2026 Asian Stablebonds** (JPY, SGD, THB, PHP, IDR, MYR, AUD, CNY) for margin denominated in ATFX's core Asian operating currencies [shared/bond-roadmap.md]

ATFX's Asia headquarters + Mexico City presence maps exactly onto Etherfuse's two strongest market clusters, and the Q3 2026 Asian launches create a natural timing hook — Mohammed can see the Mexico product live today and preview the Asian products his clients will use.

## Broader vertical signal

No Etherfuse case study or competitor analysis currently identifies FX broker-dealers as a distinct customer segment [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md] [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md]. The OTC/liquidity provider segment in the sales guide is close but not the same — OTC desks route flow; FX brokers hold margin [shared/bd/why-stablebonds-vs-direct-bonds.md]. If the ATFX re-engagement converts, it opens a repeatable vertical across the global FX brokerage industry (IG Group, Saxo, CMC Markets, etc.) where margin deposits aggregate into the billions.
