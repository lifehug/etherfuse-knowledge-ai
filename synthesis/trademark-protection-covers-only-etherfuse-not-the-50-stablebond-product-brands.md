# Trademark coverage stops at 'ETHERFUSE' — the 70+ Stablebond product brands are unprotected as the roadmap globalizes

> Etherfuse's only documented trademark asset is the 'ETHERFUSE' wordmark in Mexico, yet the company is deliberately building a branded product line (CETES, TESOURO, USTRY, GILTS, EUROB, KTB, MXNe + 50+ planned tickers) across 50+ jurisdictions — leaving the product names that actually carry commercial recognition exposed to squatting exactly where new markets are being entered.

**The only IP asset on record is a single wordmark.** AN-KI Abogados surfaced Etherfuse's mandatory Declaration of Use for IMPI Registros 2557816/2557817 — both covering the 'ETHERFUSE' mark only, valid in Mexico through June 2033 [wiki/entities/companies/an-ki-abogados.md]. No source in this neighborhood references any registration for the product names.

**Meanwhile the product names are the commercial front line.** The go-to-market motion is built around branded tickers, not the corporate name: the collateral playbook markets 'KRWQ' and 'KTB' and instructs partners to lead with the asset brand, never the custodian [shared/bd/collateral-playbook-ktb-krwq.md]; the currency→product matching table treats CETES/TESOURO/USTRY/GILTS/EUROB/KTB/MXNe as the customer-facing identities [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]; and the Visa card-collateral model replicates market-by-market on these same token brands [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md].

**The roadmap multiplies the exposure by ~70 names in ~50 countries.** The authoritative bond roadmap lists dozens of forthcoming branded tickers — INDTB, KAZTB, INDSUN, CHNTDB, JAPTDB, SAUTB, etc. — each tied to a specific sovereign jurisdiction Etherfuse plans to operate in [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. Etherfuse even commissioned an internal brief to standardize a ticker/brand convention (MEXe/USAe) across the top 30 economies — explicit evidence the company treats these as brand assets worth engineering [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. There is a known naming-collision risk already flagged: TESOURO is both a live BRL product and a proposed candidate name, and Brazil is a market Etherfuse is entering [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

**Why this matters commercially, not just legally.** Etherfuse's differentiation is being the *serial, multi-country issuer* whose product brands (not the corporate name) get integrated into partner apps, DeFi listings (Loopscale/TESOURO, Curve/Uniswap pools) and exchange listings [shared/master-knowledge-base.md, shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md]. A local squatter registering 'INDTB' in India or 'KAZTB' in Kazakhstan ahead of launch would sit directly in the path of the very institutional and distribution deals (Abound/India, Halyk & Freedom/Kazakhstan) the pipeline is chasing.

**The fix is cheap and the channel already exists.** The same relationship that surfaced the Declaration-of-Use deadline routes filings through BGBG, which holds power of attorney via Arturo (attorney-in-fact on the existing marks) and is already engaged for on-demand Mexico legal work at $250–450/hr [wiki/entities/companies/an-ki-abogados.md, wiki/entities/companies/BGBG.md]. A defensive product-name filing program — prioritized to the Q3 2026 launch jurisdictions on the roadmap — is a near-zero-cost, high-leverage protection of the brand equity the entire B2B2C distribution model depends on.

**Open question / caveat:** absence of a product-name registration in these sources is not proof none exists; this should be confirmed before it is treated as a live gap. But the fact that the only trademark touchpoint in the record is the corporate wordmark, combined with the deliberate brand-engineering brief, makes it worth an explicit audit.
