# Aptos: run the Polygon GTM playbook, not a cost-offset grant

> The Aptos page frames its grant program as merely a way to "offset integration costs," but the Polygon GTM precedent in the same neighborhood shows chain foundations deploy multi-million-dollar budgets to bootstrap Stablebond/MXNe liquidity — so the correct ask is a funded TVL/liquidity commitment, and a bounced email is a trivial blocker on a potentially large deal.

## The mismatch

The Aptos Foundation page treats the relationship as an ecosystem-expansion play where "the Aptos grant program could offset integration costs" and the only real obstacle is that BD lead Bashar Lazaar's email bounced (550 5.1.1) [wiki/entities/companies/aptos-foundation.md]. That framing badly undersells the model.

## The Polygon precedent sitting in the same neighborhood

Etherfuse has already closed exactly this kind of deal with another L1/L2 foundation. The Polygon GTM proposal committed a **$7.5M budget over 18 months** — $5M to mint CETES/MXNe, $2.5M for USDC liquidity pools, split 40% lending markets / 60% liquidity pools, targeting $100K trades at <5% slippage with a projected 7.52% year-one yield ($564K) [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. That is not a cost-offset; it is a chain foundation funding Etherfuse's on-chain liquidity to win RWA TVL on its network.

Aptos discussions already "[covered] GTM strategy, grant program eligibility, and technical integration requirements" and Aptos "previously requested specific flows and proposals" [wiki/entities/companies/aptos-foundation.md] — the same shape as the Polygon engagement. The ask should therefore be scoped as a Polygon-style liquidity/TVL commitment, not a small integration reimbursement.

## Why the integration itself is near-zero-cost

Etherfuse already ships Stablebonds across five chains (Solana, Polygon, Base, Stellar, Monad) with a BNB/BSC path under consideration, and the institutional platform is explicitly "multi-chain by default" where an institution "issues once and reaches users on every major chain" [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md, wiki/entities/companies/Etherfuse.md]. Adding Aptos is incremental engineering, so nearly all of a grant is deployable capital, not sunk integration cost — which is precisely why the Polygon-style framing captures far more value.

## Why Aptos wants this

Aptos gains what USD-only RWA issuers can't supply: regulated non-USD sovereign yield assets (CETES ~5.6%, TESOURO ~13%, plus GILTS/USTRY/EUROB/KTB) as native collateral and DeFi building blocks [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md, shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. Solana RWA TVL hitting a $1.82B ATH is the competitive benchmark Aptos is chasing [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]; sovereign Stablebonds are a differentiated way to grow it.
