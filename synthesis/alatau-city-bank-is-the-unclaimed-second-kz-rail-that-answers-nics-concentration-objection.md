# Alatau City Bank is the second KZ banking rail that neutralizes NIC's concentration-risk objection

> An unsolicited, unanswered banking proposal from Alatau City Bank offers exactly the custody + FX/SWIFT relationship that both de-risks Etherfuse's single-Halyk dependency in Kazakhstan and directly rebuts the counterparty-concentration objection NIC NBK raised as its main pushback on the whole model.

## The gap no one connected

Etherfuse's entire Kazakhstan launch currently rests on a single banking counterparty. Halyk Finance is the sole custodian/broker ("nominal holder of KZT state bonds") in the confirmed KZ structure, and the launch timeline is gated on Halyk's account opening plus the NBK letter [shared/partners/2026-05-21-kazakhstan-partnerships.md, shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-meeting.md]. Talgat at AIFC reinforced this single-rail posture, recommending Halyk as *the* fiat/IPS path for KZT and third-party payments [shared/partners/2026-05-25-aifc-talgat-amanbayev.md].

Meanwhile, in the deal that matters most for capital — NIC NBK — the sharpest objection Dave had to defend was **counterparty concentration**: "NIC holds Mexican sovereigns via 10–15 large global banks. They questioned whether Etherfuse's single-counterparty model (BBVA in Mexico) creates concentration risk vs. traditional multi-bank setup" [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Dave pushed back on the merits but the objection stands as the institution's stated risk lens — and in KZ, Etherfuse is about to reproduce exactly the single-counterparty structure NIC flagged, this time with Halyk.

## Why Alatau is the easy answer

Alatau City Bank walked in the door unsolicited on 2026-06-10 offering same-day account opening, FX/SWIFT operations, and custody accounts for asset/project fund storage — the precise service set a regulated digital-asset issuer needs — plus collateral lending up to 500M tenge [teams/cfo/legal/kazakhstan/2026-06-10-alatau-city-bank-proposal.md]. No response has been recorded [wiki/entities/companies/alatau-city-bank.md].

Engaging Alatau turns one inbound email into three wins:

1. **Answers NIC directly.** A second KZ custody/FX partner converts the single-counterparty story NIC questioned into a multi-bank one — the same structure NIC said it prefers for Mexican sovereigns. This is a near-zero-cost item on the open NIC action list (which already includes "counterparty risk research") [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md].
2. **De-risks the launch's single point of failure.** If Halyk's account opening or NBK signal slips, a parallel FX/SWIFT+custody rail keeps the KZT Stablebond launch from being hostage to one institution.
3. **Adds a SWIFT rail for the corridor.** Freedom flagged China→Kazakhstan flows taking a week via correspondent SWIFT, and Talgat said third-party fiat payments (the China→KZ→Mexico corridor) need a banking partner [shared/partners/2026-05-21-freedom-bank-aidos-zhumagulov.md, shared/partners/2026-05-25-aifc-talgat-amanbayev.md]. Alatau's FX/SWIFT offering is a candidate second rail for exactly that flow.

## The action

This is a same-week, low-cost move: assign an owner (the page has none and no Linear lead [wiki/entities/companies/alatau-city-bank.md]), respond to Aitkydyrova Aiym Umirbek's proposal, and run the credit/regulatory-standing diligence the wiki lists as open. The value is not Alatau as a headline partner — it is using a free inbound to close a named institutional objection (NIC) and remove single-vendor fragility from a launch already in its final NBK-review weeks.
