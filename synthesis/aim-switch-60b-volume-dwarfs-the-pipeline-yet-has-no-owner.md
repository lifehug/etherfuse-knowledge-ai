# AIM Switch's $60B volume is a bigger FX/float prize than the whole top-15 — and it's ownerless

> AIM Switch is a ~$60B Mexican payments processor that SDF is co-selling into Etherfuse's home market, yet its wiki page has no contacts, no Linear lead, and a vague 'integration pathway' framing — even a fractional FX/float capture on $60B outsizes every deal in the current top-15 pipeline.

## The number nobody has priced

AIM Switch (formerly In Switch) processes **~$60B+ in annual payment volume** in Mexico and is in active discussions with both Etherfuse and SDF, with a positive first meeting already held [wiki/entities/companies/aim-switch.md]. The page treats this as a generic 'could unlock significant on-chain settlement volume' opportunity and lists **zero contacts and no `linear_lead_id`** [wiki/entities/companies/aim-switch.md].

Put that $60B against Etherfuse's published economics:

- The FX revenue tier bottoms out at **5 bps for ≥$100M/month volume** [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. Even if only a **single-digit percentage** of AIM Switch's $60B touches an FX/settlement leg, the 5 bps tier produces revenue in the **eight figures** — larger than BBVA ($7.0M) or Meta ($6.5M), the two biggest revenue lines in the June pipeline [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md].
- Idle settlement float on a processor of this size maps directly onto the **auto-yield-on-float** and **remittance FX-90%-cut** case studies Etherfuse already ships — where enterprise payout accounts route idle float into the highest-yield Stablebond and FX settles at 5–20 bps vs. 100–300+ bps traditional [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md].

AIM Switch does not even appear in the pipeline valuation snapshot's 481 companies or top-15 [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md] — meaning a deal that could rival BBVA is currently invisible to the investor-facing pipeline number.

## Why this is a fast, defensible win

Three structural advantages compound here:

1. **Home turf.** This is Mexico, where Etherfuse is the *only* CNBV-authorized sovereign-bond tokenizer with native zero-cost SPEI ramps and MXNe/CETES already live [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md, shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. A $60B Mexican processor needs exactly the local-currency yield + FX leg that no USD-only RWA competitor can offer.
2. **SDF is co-selling.** SDF is engaged with AIM Switch *in parallel* [wiki/entities/companies/aim-switch.md], and Etherfuse already ships on Stellar with live RedStone oracle feeds [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. SDF has its own incentive to bring $60B of volume onto Stellar rails — making this a partner-driven, low-CAC channel where Etherfuse supplies the yield/FX layer on top of rails SDF is separately motivated to close.
3. **Proven mechanics.** Every component of the pitch — SPEI mint/burn, float auto-yield, 5 bps FX, monthly proof-of-reserves — is already in production, not a build [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md, shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

## The gap to close

The blocker is not product fit — it is that the relationship has **no named owner, no decision-maker mapped, and no Linear lead** [wiki/entities/companies/aim-switch.md]. The single highest-leverage action is to assign a BD owner, get names from the SDF side of the co-sell, and reframe the pitch from 'settlement integration' to the two revenue engines that actually scale with $60B: **FX volume at the 5 bps tier** and **auto-yield on domestic MXN float**.

## Caveat

$60B is stated as total *payment* volume, not FX volume [wiki/entities/companies/aim-switch.md]; the cross-border/FX share is unknown and domestic card/rail volume would not all carry an FX leg. Even so, the domestic-float auto-yield product applies to idle MXN regardless of FX mix, so the revenue floor remains materially above anything currently in the top-15.
