# Alfred's Argentina VASP + Colombia footprint match Etherfuse's next new-issuance markets

> Alfred Pay already holds a VASP license in Argentina and operates in Colombia — the exact two markets Etherfuse's own roadmap flags for new Stablebond issuance in 2026 — making Alfred a pre-regulated ramp/distribution partner for those launches, not merely the MXN 'Won' deal its wiki page shows.

The Alfred wiki page reduces the relationship to a 'Won' deal with a 'TBD' offering [wiki/entities/companies/alfred-pay.md], and prior synthesis only surfaced Alfred's Fireblocks CAB seat. But the underlying note shows a multi-market operator: "Current operations: Mexico (STP), Argentina (VASP license), Colombia," with explicit intent to replicate "across all their markets" and strong interest in a repeatable model [shared/partners/alfred-pay-2026-04-22.md].

Those markets line up precisely with Etherfuse's stated expansion. The Alfred note itself says Etherfuse is "ready now: Mexico, US, Europe, Brazil. Q3 2026: Colombia, Argentina" with a "~4-week process via local custodians" for new countries [shared/partners/alfred-pay-2026-04-22.md]. The AirTM proposal independently pins the same sequence — Argentina Q2 2026, Colombia Q3 2026 — but flags both as requiring "new issuance" and leans on AirTM's P2P network for local ramps [shared/partners/airtm-partnership-proposal.md].

The connection: a new Stablebond needs both an issuance/custody leg and a compliant local fiat ramp. Alfred supplies the second in exactly the two currencies Etherfuse is about to build for — an Argentine VASP-licensed entity and live Colombian operations — which is institutional-grade regulated coverage rather than P2P. So Alfred is the natural launch ramp for an Argentine-peso and Colombian-peso (COP) Stablebond, closing the demand+distribution side of markets Etherfuse would otherwise open cold.

Ease/speed: Alfred has already committed to launching an MXN balance on Etherfuse collateral 'asap' with a scoping call held, so the integration relationship exists [shared/partners/alfred-pay-2026-04-22.md]; extending it to ARS/COP is incremental. The move is to sequence Alfred into the Argentina/Colombia issuance plan now, rather than treating it as a closed MXN account.
