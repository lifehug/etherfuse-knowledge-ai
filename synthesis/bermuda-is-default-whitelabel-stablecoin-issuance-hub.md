# Bermuda is converging as Etherfuse's default white-label stablecoin issuance hub — license once, issue for all

> Multiple independent partners (1Money, AirTM, Alfred Pay) are all routing branded local-currency stablecoin issuance through Bermuda, and Etherfuse's own in-flight BMA license plus the Section 8 'notification-only' moat means Etherfuse should issue these under one license rather than let each partner stand up its own entity.

Three separate deals point to the same jurisdiction without coordinating:

- **1Money** proposes issuing a MXN or BRL stablecoin via *its own Bermuda entity*, Stablebonds as collateral, yield distributed through its network — explicitly modeled on the Agora white-label approach [wiki/entities/companies/1money.md].
- **AirTM** proposes a co-owned JV regulated out of Bermuda under the BMA DABA framework, with Etherfuse owning asset issuance, custody, mint/burn and the BMA relationship — and Etherfuse has *already initiated the BMA pre-application* [shared/partners/2026-05-13-airtm-jv-proposal.md].
- **Alfred Pay** lists Bermuda as the fallback issuance entity for its CETES-backed MXN balance product (El Salvador primary) [shared/partners/alfred-pay-2026-04-22.md].

The AirTM proposal surfaces the decisive structural fact: under BMA's **Section 8 exemption, each new-currency Stablebond requires only a notification to BMA — no re-application** [shared/partners/2026-05-13-airtm-jv-proposal.md]. That is a one-time license that scales to every currency and every partner.

**The non-obvious conclusion:** 1Money wanting to issue under *its own* Bermuda entity is value leakage. If Etherfuse holds the BMA license (via the AirTM JV path or its own pre-application), it can issue 1Money's MXN/BRL coin, Alfred's MXN balance product, and future partner coins under a single license using cheap notifications — capturing the issuance/compliance economics instead of being a collateral vendor to a partner-owned issuer. The same white-label pattern recurs across Agora, Brale (MXNe), AirTM, 1Money, Alfred and Bloque [shared/partners/2023-12-14-brale-mxne-proposal.md][shared/partners/airtm-partnership-proposal.md][shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bloque-etherfuse-onboarding-kickoff.md], meaning 'Bermuda-issued, Stablebond-collateralized white-label stablecoin' is now a repeatable product, not a bespoke deal — and Etherfuse should package it as one (issuance kit + standard revenue-share + Section 8 currency add-on) and insist on owning the license layer.
