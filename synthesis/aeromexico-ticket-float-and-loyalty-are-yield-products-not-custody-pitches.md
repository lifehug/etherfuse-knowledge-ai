# Aeromexico's ticket-sale float + loyalty liability is a yield product Etherfuse can already package

> Aeromexico sits untouched on the wishlist pitched as a generic 'CETES custody' deal, but its real value is two giant idle-MXN liabilities — advance ticket-sale float and Club Premier points — that map directly onto products Etherfuse already ships (the corporate-treasury yield case study, the MXNe/CETES wrap, and the Visa-card collateral playbook).

## The mispriced opportunity

The Aeromexico page frames the deal as "CETES Stablebonds for corporate treasury; potential loyalty program integration," stage Wishlist, **no outreach initiated**, blocker "identify contacts" [wiki/entities/companies/aeromexico.md]. That framing buries the actual size of the prize. The page itself names the two structural facts that matter: airlines "manage substantial float from advance ticket sales, which could be deployed into CETES Stablebonds," and the loyalty program is "a large pool of stored consumer value that could benefit from yield generation" [wiki/entities/companies/aeromexico.md]. Both are large, persistent, MXN-denominated, and currently earning ~0% — the exact profile Etherfuse monetizes.

## 1. The air-traffic-liability float dwarfs the existing treasury case study

Etherfuse's own packaged proof point is a multinational tech company earning **$600K+/year on $10M of idle MXN** held for local operations, redeemed same-day for payments — a compliance/working-capital buffer turned into a revenue line at ~5.6–5.8% CETES [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md; shared/partners/canton-mexico-infrastructure.md]. A flag carrier's *air-traffic liability* (cash collected for tickets not yet flown) is an order of magnitude larger and structurally stickier — it is deferred revenue that sits for weeks-to-months before recognition. That is the same "idle float between holding windows" pattern Etherfuse already sells to PSPs (parking settlement float in CETES/USTRY and redeeming same-day on the batch cycle) [shared/bd/case-study-psp-hongkong.md]. The instant/T+1 redemption-within-float mechanic (7–10% AUM liquidity buffer) means Aeromexico never trades liquidity for yield [shared/master-knowledge-base.md; shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md]. The pitch should lead with "deploy your air-traffic liability into CETES," not "we can custody your treasury."

## 2. The loyalty program is a yield-bearing balance waiting to be wrapped

Club Premier points are a stored-value liability — exactly what Etherfuse converts into a yield product elsewhere without the holder ever touching crypto mechanics. Two already-built primitives apply:
- **The MXNe / CETES wrap.** Brale + Etherfuse already co-issue a peso balance backed by CETES with tiered revenue share (10%→90% as market cap scales) [shared/partners/2023-12-14-brale-mxne-proposal.md]. A loyalty balance backed by CETES gives Aeromexico's loyalty JV a new yield revenue line on float it already holds.
- **The "savings account, not stablecoin" framing.** Dave's explicit guidance to Alfred Pay was to position the product as an "MXN balance / savings account" because customers care about liquidity and yield, not the mechanics — issued on top of the Etherfuse bond asset so balance/stablecoin/yield wrap 1:1 from day one [shared/partners/alfred-pay-2026-04-22.md]. The same packaging lets a loyalty program offer "points that earn" without a regulatory or UX rebuild.

## 3. The co-brand card closes the loop

Aeromexico runs a co-branded travel card. The Visa local-issuer collateral playbook — live today in Mexico on CETES — lets card balances sit as yield-accruing CETES collateral, settling to Visa in pesos with no FX, while idle balances earn the sovereign rate [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md]. That turns "hold + earn" into "hold + earn + spend" for cardholders and gives Aeromexico a card-program margin it doesn't have today.

## Why this is an easy, fast win

Every component already exists and is live in Mexico under CNBV P090/2024 with BBVA custody — no new product, no new market, no new license [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. The only missing input is the contact, which is the page's sole stated blocker [wiki/entities/companies/aeromexico.md]. A high-profile Mexican brand also delivers the market validation the company explicitly wants. The reframing — from a passive custody wishlist line to "three idle-MXN pools (ticket float, loyalty points, card balances) we can monetize with products already shipping" — is what justifies pulling this off the wishlist now.
