# The grant flywheel is Stellar-only — Solana/Monad promises are slipping deals

> Multiple won/active DeFi integrations were partly sold on Solana ($70K) and Monad omnibus grants that the team has since found don't actually deliver, while Stellar is the only ecosystem whose grant process reliably converts — meaning several integration plans rest on subsidies that won't materialize.

Etherfuse routinely closes integration deals by offering to route ecosystem grants to partners to seed liquidity. P0's won deal lists a 'Solana Foundation offering up to $70K for integrating Etherfuse payments' as a named incentive [teams/sales/2026-04-21-0xyz-p0-won.md]; Assetto was offered a '$50–100K range' Solana omnibus grant [teams/sales/2026-04-21-assetto-precept-labs.md]; and SBC was promised grants 'across Stellar, Solana, and Monad' [teams/sales/2026-04-19-sbc-stablecoinxyz.md], with Dave telling Jeff he could 'probably get you a grant from Stellar, Solana and Monad' [teams/sales/2026-04-13-stablecoin-xyz-grant-liquidity.md].

But by June 4 the reality is documented bluntly: Andy tells Jeff that Solana and Monad 'are being very slow with grants… I stopped offering them,' that Monad 'doesn't have a process per se,' and that he hasn't 'seen them successfully give a grant to any of the referrals' — while Stellar 'move[s] very quick' and has 'a more predetermined road' [teams/sales/2026-06-04-jeff-milewski-stellar-grant.md]. The Solana grant itself is still stuck on an unresolved 'direct application vs distribution model' question that has been open since January [wiki/entities/companies/Solana.md].

Decision-useful implication: P0 (won) and Assetto (won) both have integration economics anchored to Solana grants that the company's own most recent evidence says won't arrive. The team should (a) stop citing Solana/Monad grants as deal sweeteners, (b) re-underwrite those integrations on Etherfuse-funded liquidity, and (c) concentrate the omnibus-grant motion on Stellar, where it works — which also reframes Stellar (the anchor investor) as the only reliable subsidy partner for cheap-to-acquire integrations.
