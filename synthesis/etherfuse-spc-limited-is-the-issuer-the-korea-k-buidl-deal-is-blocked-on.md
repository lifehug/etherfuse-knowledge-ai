# Etherfuse SPC Limited is the bankruptcy-remote issuer K-BUIDL is blocked on

> The AIFC SPC stood up for Kazakhstan's KZT Stablebond is exactly the bankruptcy-remote vehicle the Shinhan K-BUIDL architecture names as a hard requirement, so a Kazakhstan setup already completed resolves the Korea deal's #1 'blocks everything' open item via series ring-fencing.

The K-BUIDL regulatory analysis flags one issue as **HIGH — blocks everything**: "Which Etherfuse entity signs as offshore vehicle? (Inc. vs AIFC SPC)" — and the bankruptcy-remoteness section answers it directly: "Etherfuse insolvency: 'The issuing vehicle is a bankruptcy-remote special-purpose entity, and the fund shares are distributed to token holders.' → Requires AIFC SPC as the issuing entity." [teams/cfo/legal/korea/2026-06-08-kbuidl-regulatory-report-analysis.md]

That entity already exists. **Etherfuse SPC Limited** was registered at the AIFC on June 10, 2026, formed under AIFC SPC Rules with English common law, non-petition clauses, limited recourse, an orphan structure, and — critically — **series ring-fencing**, where "each series of Stablebonds can be legally isolated from other series within the same SPC." [wiki/concepts/aifc-spc.md] It is modeled on Freedom Finance's SPC, which already underpins a USD 1B AIFC bond programme. [teams/cfo/legal/kazakhstan/2026-05-21-nbk-meeting-agenda.md]

**The non-obvious link:** the SPC was built as a Kazakhstan compliance artifact (to ringfence KZT Stablebond assets from NBK's asset-disposal-risk concern), but its series ring-fencing means the *same* legal entity can host a distinct, legally-isolated K-BUIDL series for the Korean ETF tokenization without standing up a new vehicle. A Central Asian regulatory setup therefore unblocks a Korean institutional product — Dave's flagged blocker is already 90% solved.

**Why it matters / speed:** K-BUIDL is a Won-side tokenization of a Shinhan Asset Management bond ETF (Korea's BlackRock-equivalent), riding a no-action letter pending June 2026 → sandbox → formal process. [teams/cfo/legal/korea/2026-06-08-kbuidl-regulatory-report-analysis.md] Confirming Etherfuse SPC Limited as the K-BUIDL issuer is a near-zero-cost decision that removes the gating dependency and lets the Shinhan PoC kick off. It also reinforces the broader thesis that the AIFC SPC is a reusable, globally-deployable institutional wrapper, not a Kazakhstan-only chore.
