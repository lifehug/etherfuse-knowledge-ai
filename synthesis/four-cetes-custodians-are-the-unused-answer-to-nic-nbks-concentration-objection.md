# Actinver + Kuspit + BASE are the unused rebuttal to the single-custodian objection

> Etherfuse already runs a four-custodian CETES structure (BBVA, Actinver, Kuspit, Casa de Bolsa BASE), which is a ready-made answer to the exact counterparty-concentration concern NIC NBK raised — yet Dave defended on a different argument and the open 'counterparty risk research' action item still treats Mexico as single-counterparty.

When NIC NBK pressed Etherfuse on counterparty risk, they framed it precisely as a single-custodian problem: "NIC holds Mexican sovereigns via 10–15 large global banks. They questioned whether Etherfuse's single-counterparty model (BBVA in Mexico) creates concentration risk vs. traditional multi-bank setup" [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Dave pushed back on liquidity/yield grounds (local desks, T+1 settlement, ~6.2% vs ETF ~4.5–5%) and took on an open action item to "Do counterparty risk research: Etherfuse single local partner vs. multi-bank traditional setup" [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md] — implicitly accepting NIC's single-custodian premise.

But the premise is wrong. Etherfuse's CETES backing is already spread across **four** Category I Mexican custodians, not one: BBVA, Casa de Bolsa BASE, Kuspit, and Actinver [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. The Actinver entity page confirms Actinver is a live CETES custody partner carrying a 15.23% capitalization index, classified Category I without solvency problems [wiki/entities/companies/actinver-casa-de-bolsa.md]. The Anchorage strategy doc independently lists "Casa de Bolsa BASE, Kuspit, and Actinver for CETES" alongside BBVA [wiki/entities/companies/Etherfuse.md]. So the diversified multi-custodian structure NIC said they wanted from "10–15 large global banks" already exists in miniature in Mexico — it simply wasn't surfaced in the room.

**Why this matters for revenue/speed/ease:** NIC NBK is a sovereign-wealth-grade institutional allocator with an active conversion in progress [shared/partners/2026-05-21-kazakhstan-partnerships.md], and its own stated bottleneck is risk/custody comfort, not product fit [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. The counterparty-risk action item can be answered today with a one-page diagram showing four independent Category I custodians, each CNBV-classified without solvency problems — no new research, no new partnerships, no product build. The same artifact strengthens every institutional DD package (it is already named in the asset-protection copy: "BBVA México, Actinver Casa de Bolsa, and Kuspit Casa de Bolsa" [shared/bd/website-asset-protection-rewrite.md], and in the Maimone legal response [shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md]).

**Two fixable gaps that blunt this rebuttal:**
1. The asset-protection / DD copy names only three of the four custodians (drops Casa de Bolsa BASE) [shared/bd/website-asset-protection-rewrite.md][shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md], so the strongest version of the diversification story isn't even being told to the audiences most sensitive to concentration risk.
2. Actinver's only on-file metric — 15.23% capitalization, Nov 2024 [wiki/entities/companies/actinver-casa-de-bolsa.md] — is ~18 months stale by the time of the NIC pitch; a refreshed figure is needed before it goes into a counterparty-risk deck.

Net: a high-value institutional close is sitting on an objection Etherfuse can already defeat with facts it owns, but the facts are scattered across the KB and partially omitted from the exact documents where they'd land.
