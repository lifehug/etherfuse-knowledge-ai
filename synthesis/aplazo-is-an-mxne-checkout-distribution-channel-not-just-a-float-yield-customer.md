# Aplazo is an MXNe checkout-distribution partner across 9,000 merchants, not just a float-yield lead

> Aplazo's stated ambitions — stablecoin-powered checkout, digital wallets, and an already-built Privy embedded-wallet stack — map onto Etherfuse's digital-wallet revenue-share case study and make Aplazo a low-friction MXNe distribution channel to 9,000+ merchants, a far larger wedge than the idle-float CETES yield the deal is currently scoped around.

The Aplazo deal is framed almost entirely as CETES yield on idle BNPL float between merchant settlement and consumer repayment [wiki/entities/companies/aplazo.md]. That float-yield motion maps cleanly onto Etherfuse's SMB-lender reserves and corporate-treasury case studies [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md] — but it undersells the relationship.

Two facts on the page point to a bigger, faster wedge. First, Aplazo has already adopted [[Privy]] for embedded wallets, meaning the hardest part of a consumer stablecoin product — non-crypto-native wallet UX — is already solved; Etherfuse only needs to plug its yield/ramp API into an existing stack, a near-zero-integration win [wiki/entities/companies/aplazo.md]. Second, Aplazo explicitly wants stablecoin-powered checkout across its 9,000+ merchant base [wiki/entities/companies/aplazo.md].

Stablecoin checkout in Mexico is the MXNe use case: MXNe is Etherfuse's live CETES-backed peso stablecoin co-issued with [[Brale]], built for payments and merchant settlement [shared/master-knowledge-base.md, shared/partners/2023-12-14-brale-mxne-proposal.md]. Routing merchant settlement through MXNe lets Aplazo (a) settle merchants in a yield-bearing peso instrument and (b) earn a partner revenue share on distributed AUM — the exact model in Etherfuse's digital-wallet case study, where a wallet adds yield-bearing local-currency savings and earns 10–60 bps on all distributed balances [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md, shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md].

**Why this is bigger than float yield:** float yield captures Aplazo's own idle balance once; MXNe checkout distribution turns Aplazo into a B2B2C channel pushing yield-bearing pesos to thousands of merchants and their consumers, with recurring revenue-share economics. The Privy stack makes it the fastest path, not the slowest.

**Bonus channel value:** Aplazo's merchant roster includes named enterprises already elsewhere in Etherfuse's orbit — notably Aeroméxico [wiki/entities/companies/aplazo.md], the subject of separate float/FX-yield theses — making Aplazo a warm conduit into large-merchant treasury conversations.
