# Standing up Circle Mint + Tether institutional mint for EM banks is a repeatable product, not a one-off favor

> In two separate institutional pitches Etherfuse is offering to provision partners' own Circle Mint and Tether institutional-mint accounts as part of a bundled 'stablecoin stack' — a packageable onboarding service for banks in underserved corridors that is distinct from, and a faster on-ramp to, Stablebond revenue.

## The same offer appears in two unrelated deals

The Tajikistan/IBT concept brief proposes Etherfuse stand up IBT's institutional stablecoin stack: MPC/HSM custody, **USDC via Circle Mint** (1:1, no spread), and **USDT via Tether's institutional mint** for the Russia–Central Asia remittance corridor — explicitly noting 'Dave has set this up before' and that minting at par lets the bank hold the float [shared/partners/2026-05-30-etherfuse-ibt-concept-brief.md][shared/partners/2026-05-30-tajikistan-ibt-stablebond-analysis.md].

The Shinhan cover letter makes the same move for Korea: it nudges Shinhan AM toward a KRW bank account so that *'Circle (USDC) and Tether (USDT) Mint have direct banking infrastructure in Korea… which creates a liquidity bridge to send or receive KRW in a compliant way'* [shared/partners/2026-06-05-shinhan-cover-letter.md].

## Why this is a distinct product, not plumbing

The institutional platform overview lists USDC/CCTP as an internal ramp capability [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md], but the IBT and Shinhan docs reveal something more sellable: Etherfuse acting as the **onboarding partner that provisions the bank's own primary mint relationships**. For an EM bank, Circle Mint KYC + sanctions screening 'can take several weeks' [shared/partners/2026-05-30-etherfuse-ibt-concept-brief.md] — friction Etherfuse has already navigated and can compress. That is a discrete deliverable a bank will pay for, and it lands *before* any Stablebond is issued, shortening time-to-first-revenue.

## The strategic wedge

Provisioning the dollar rail first makes Etherfuse the natural issuer of the **local-currency yield leg** that every one of these banks then needs — USTRY/Stablebonds as the productive reserve behind their new stablecoin, exactly the reserve-backing pitch already structured for IBT (hold USTRY at 3.3% instead of zero-yield USDT) [shared/partners/2026-05-26-international-bank-of-tajikistan.md]. The Circle/Tether mint setup is the cheap, fast foot-in-the-door; the Stablebond reserve is the recurring revenue.

**Action:** name and templatize this as a 'Stablecoin Stack' onboarding SKU (custody + Circle Mint + Tether mint + compliance tooling), price it, and lead with it for every bank in a remittance-heavy, under-banked corridor — the Central Asian anchor banks (IBT, Halyk, Freedom) and any partner already routing branded coins through Bermuda are the immediate targets.
