# ARQ's card/credit-lines thread is the ready-made Visa collateral playbook — the deeper wedge Florencia already offered

> Florencia explicitly offered to introduce ARQ's cards/credit-lines team for a Stablebonds-as-collateral discussion, which maps one-to-one onto Etherfuse's fully-documented Visa local-issuer card-collateral model — a structural, multi-country integration far larger than the MXN yield embed the deal is stuck on.

The one concrete expansion ARQ's own side proposed was a **Stablebonds-as-collateral discussion with its cards/credit-lines team**, which Florencia volunteered to broker [wiki/entities/companies/arq-finance.md]. The ARQ page treats this as a vague secondary thread, but Etherfuse already has a complete, Visa-validated playbook for exactly it: local card issuers hold settlement float as yield-bearing local-currency Stablebond collateral (CETES/TESOURO), burn to fiat at day's-end netting, and settle to Visa in local fiat — with Visa confirming no token ever touches its rails and no additional approval needed for the domestic flow [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md].

The model is explicitly replicable in every market where Etherfuse has both a tokenized sovereign asset and a local settlement rail — Mexico/CETES/SPEI and Brazil/TESOURO/PIX are both listed [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md], covering two of ARQ's four markets today. For a card issuer, this converts idle 0%-yielding collateral float into sovereign yield with zero FX risk on domestic spend and no change to the Visa settlement workflow [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md]. That is a far deeper, stickier integration than a savings-feature embed, and it is the thread ARQ itself flagged as worth exploring.

This mirrors the AirTM Visa-seat pattern already recognized elsewhere — deploy the documented card-collateral model through a Visa-connected LATAM wallet — but ARQ arrives with a warm intro to the exact internal team, no JV to stand up, and a live card product across four countries. The reactivation ask is simple: take Florencia up on the cards-team introduction and lead with the Visa collateral playbook rather than the stalled MXN yield brief.
