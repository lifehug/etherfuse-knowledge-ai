# Amazon Mexico: a two-sided float play the orchestration channel unblocks

> Amazon Mexico's wiki blocker is 'enterprise BD is complex,' but its two idle-MXN liabilities map exactly onto two Stablebond case studies Etherfuse already ships, and a treasury-orchestration/PSP partner is the wedge that sidesteps the direct-BD wall.

## The reframe

The Amazon Mexico page treats the account as an undifferentiated 'CETES custody / seller treasury' wishlist with the honest admission that 'Amazon's enterprise structure makes BD complex' and 'no outreach initiated' [wiki/entities/companies/amazon-mexico.md]. But the same page already identifies **two structurally distinct** idle-MXN pools: (1) marketplace **seller-proceeds float** held pending disbursement, and (2) **Amazon Pay consumer stored value** [wiki/entities/companies/amazon-mexico.md]. Those are not one product pitch — they are two products Etherfuse ships today:

- Seller-proceeds float pending scheduled disbursement is the **corporate-treasury / settlement-float** model: idle in-country MXN swept into CETES at ~6–7% APY, redeemable same-day within the liquidity float for payouts, with monthly proof-of-reserves — the exact mechanics of Case Study 4 ($600K+/yr on $10M idle MXN) [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md].
- Amazon Pay balances are the **digital-wallet yield-share** model: consumer stored value earning 0% today, converted to a CETES-backed savings feature with a 10–60 bps partner revenue share to the platform — Case Study 2's playbook verbatim [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md].

## Why the BD-complexity blocker is the wrong frame

The Mancio consultation describes the *identical* pain at Amazon's scale from the other side: multinationals and 'large betting platforms' holding 'millions of dollars on hand that are doing nothing for the business,' stuck on T+7 settlement MSAs and once-a-week wire treasuries, whose 'treasury is not built for' instant on-chain funds [shared/partners/mancio.md]. Mancio's answer is a treasury-orchestration layer that tokenizes in-country funds into domestic-yield assets and calls Etherfuse the 'ideal partner' for the yield leg [shared/partners/mancio.md]. The lesson for Amazon Mexico: rather than fight Amazon's enterprise procurement directly, the faster path is to land the seller-float product **through** an orchestration/PSP partner (Mancio-style, or a BaaS such as Galileo which already runs Mexican fintech distribution [shared/partners/galileo-research.md]) that carries the integration and treasury-workflow burden Etherfuse's B2B2C model is built for.

## The bigger prize: an e-commerce marketplace vertical

Seller-proceeds float is not Amazon-specific — every marketplace holds it (Mercado Libre, delivery/logistics platforms, gig payout platforms). This is the same 'one structure → repeatable vertical' pattern the KB already recognized for commodity exporters via 9 Dots/ORO. A single seller-float template — advance proceeds in MXN, sovereign yield during the disbursement window, SPEI payout — generalizes across the entire Mexican marketplace/e-commerce segment, and the Visa local-issuer card-collateral model extends it to any co-branded card those platforms issue on consumer balances [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md]. Amazon Mexico is worth pursuing less as one hard-to-close logo than as the anchor case study for that vertical.

## Recommended next step

Replace the vague 'research Amazon's fintech team' step with: (a) scope the seller-disbursement-cycle float as the corporate-treasury product and Amazon Pay as the wallet-yield product separately, and (b) pursue entry via a treasury-orchestration/BaaS intermediary rather than cold enterprise BD.
