# Anchorage's 2026 IPO clock is the lever to close an already-built integration

> Anchorage's reported CETES custody integration is technically complete on its side yet stalled on customer-facing routing with zero direct Etherfuse contacts — and Anchorage's $200–400M pre-IPO raise creates internal urgency for exactly the differentiated yield revenue Stablebonds provide, making this a fast, low-cost close if Etherfuse pushes the 'why now' angle.

Two facts in this neighborhood should be connected. First, as of the June 10 BD sync, Clint coordinated with Anchorage, shared mint addresses, and **received confirmation that custody setup is complete on Anchorage's side** — the only open item is whether Anchorage now displays the asset and routes customers to the Etherfuse site, with no direct Anchorage point of contact on file [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage.md]. The technical work is essentially done; the deal is stalled on customer-facing activation and relationship ownership.

Second, Anchorage is **raising $200–400M for a 2026 IPO** and is explicitly 'looking for differentiated yield products for institutional clients' [shared/partners/anchorage-digital.md]. The strategy doc itself names the timing: 'Pre-IPO Growth: Perfect timing for revenue diversification ahead of 2026 IPO' [shared/partners/anchorage-digital-strategy.md].

The synthesis: Etherfuse's revenue-share (30/40/60 bps by yield tier) on CETES/USTRY/TESOURO is precisely the differentiated, compliant yield line Anchorage needs to show in its pre-IPO revenue story [shared/partners/anchorage-digital-strategy.md]. Because the integration is already built, the close is mostly a matter of (a) securing a named owner beyond Clint and (b) pressing the IPO-clock 'why now' with Monica Ramirez de Arellano. This is an easy win at risk of going stale exactly like other near-done deals — pushing now converts months of integration work into live customer-facing distribution.
