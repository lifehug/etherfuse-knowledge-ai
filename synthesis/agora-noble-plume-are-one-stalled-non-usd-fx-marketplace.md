# Agora + Noble + Plume are one dormant FX-marketplace, split across three cold leads

> The three related leads in Agora's neighborhood — Agora (AUSD USD leg + 'joint FX marketplace'), Noble (an FX DEX for non-USD stablecoins), and Plume (non-USD Nest vaults) — are all Nurture-stage, all owned by Andres Salcedo, all opened Dec 2025–Jan 2026, and all describe the same product; reviving them as one package is far higher-leverage than working three stale leads.

Read together, these are not three unrelated integrations — they are the supply, the venue, and the distribution for a single non-USD on-chain FX product that has never been assembled.

- **Agora = the USD leg.** Agora's AUSD stablecoin plus its stated goal of a 'joint FX marketplace' and AUSD/KTB pairing make it the dollar anchor of any FX venue [wiki/entities/companies/agora.md].
- **Noble = the venue.** Noble is explicitly 'building an FX DEX for non-USD stablecoins' and expressed interest in integrating Etherfuse's sovereign-backed Stablebonds, targeting 60–70 countries by year-end [wiki/entities/companies/noble.md]. Its only stated resource blocker is trivial: it holds $250M in stablecoins but needs just '$1–2M for FX pools' [wiki/entities/companies/noble.md]. That is a tiny, seedable gap standing between Etherfuse and a $250M distribution channel.
- **Plume = the distribution wrapper.** Plume wants MXN-denominated Nest vaults and other non-USD offerings, integration assessed as LOW complexity, gated only on Plume's internal prioritization [wiki/entities/companies/plume-network.md].

All three share the same owner (Andres Salcedo), the same Nurture stage, and the same origination window (Dec 2025–Jan 2026), and all three then went cold — Noble's reactivation email of April 15 is unanswered [wiki/entities/companies/noble.md], Agora is 'awaiting response' since January [wiki/entities/companies/agora.md], and Plume's Q1 2026 integration is ~6 months overdue [wiki/entities/companies/plume-network.md].

**Why this matters now:** the underlying product — a non-USD FX venue for the live roster (CETES, TESOURO, KTB, GILTS, EUROB, USTRY, plus 11 more in Q3 2026) — is exactly Etherfuse's structural edge over USD-only issuers [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. Noble and Agora both name Korea/Japan as priority markets and both praised the Shinhan partnership [wiki/entities/companies/noble.md, wiki/entities/companies/agora.md], directly overlapping Etherfuse's live KTB and Q3 2026 JAPTDB. The fastest move is a single re-engagement thread that puts AUSD (USD leg), Etherfuse Stablebonds (non-USD legs), and Noble's DEX (venue) in one room — seeding Noble's ~$1–2M pool from Stablebond supply — rather than nurturing three leads that each individually look small.
