# Monad Foundation's payoff to Etherfuse is partner intros, not the grant that doesn't convert

> Etherfuse has concluded the Monad omnibus grant doesn't actually deliver, but the Abound deal shows Monad Foundation's genuine value is high-quality BD introductions — and that relationship should be worked for deal flow, not subsidy.

Etherfuse's grant analysis found that the Solana and Monad omnibus grants several integration plans were sold on don't actually materialize, with only Stellar converting reliably [shared/bd/grants-stellar-solana-monad.md]. That makes it easy to discount the Monad relationship entirely — which would be a mistake.

The Abound opportunity — a Negotiation-stage deal pinned #4 in the Top 15 at $1.5M TVL / $1.1M revenue [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md] — came entirely from a **warm introduction by Raj Parekh, Monad Foundation's Head of Stablecoins & Payments**, who connected Dave Taylor with Abound's founder around the INR opportunity [wiki/entities/companies/abound.md, 19d9426f75179a19]. That is a ~$1M revenue lead delivered through Monad's BD network, not its grant program.

The reframe: stop evaluating Monad on grant conversion (where it fails) and start working the Raj Parekh / Monad Foundation relationship as a deal-flow channel into the stablecoin/payments ecosystem (where it just produced a Top-15 deal). Concretely, ask Raj for the next intros — Monad's 'connecting stablecoin infrastructure players within its ecosystem' posture [wiki/entities/companies/abound.md] is a repeatable sourcing engine that costs Etherfuse nothing and has already paid off once.
