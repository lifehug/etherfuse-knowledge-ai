# Three idle CETES custodians are pre-integrated retail distribution channels

> Actinver, Kuspit, and Casa de Bolsa BASE are already-integrated, regulated Mexican brokerages with their own retail client books — the exact distribution Etherfuse needs to attack Mexico's '2% retail ownership' problem — yet all three sit on file with zero contacts and zero commercial engagement beyond passive custody.

Etherfuse's own framing is that Mexico's $623B bond market with only 2% retail ownership is a *distribution problem, not a product problem* [wiki/markets/mexico-cetes.md], and that 'success in Mexico creates a blueprint for launching in other markets via institutional partners' [wiki/markets/mexico-cetes.md].

The CETES Stablebond is custodied across four regulated institutions: BBVA, Casa de Bolsa BASE, Kuspit, and Actinver [wiki/concepts/cetes.md, shared/products/treasury-mexico-ramps.md]. Three of those four — Actinver, Kuspit, and BASE — are *Casas de Bolsa* (brokerages), institutions whose core business is selling securities to Mexican retail and institutional clients [wiki/entities/companies/actinver-casa-de-bolsa.md]. They are already legally connected to Etherfuse, already hold the underlying bonds, and are already named Category I institutions [wiki/markets/mexico-cetes.md].

The non-obvious gap: the focal Actinver record shows 'No contacts on file,' 'No interactions recorded yet,' and explicitly notes Actinver 'functions as an infrastructure/custody counterparty… rather than as a direct commercial prospect' [wiki/entities/companies/actinver-casa-de-bolsa.md, wiki/entities/companies/actinver.md]. The same is true of Kuspit and BASE — they appear only as custody line-items, never as distribution. Meanwhile the existing BBVA finding already argues for converting the *bank* custodian into a yield partner; the same conversion logic applies with even less friction to the three *brokerage* custodians, because selling MXN sovereign-yield products to retail is literally their existing license and channel.

Why this is an easy win on impact × speed × ease: (1) the integration and custody relationship already exists — no new onboarding, no new KYB, no new regulatory pathway; (2) each brokerage owns a retail distribution book Etherfuse currently reaches B2B2C only through fintech partners; (3) the CETES product already pays a ~3.6-point spread over bank savings that retail brokerage clients would value [wiki/markets/mexico-cetes.md]. An external prospecting vendor even noticed these names are publicly displayed as logos on Etherfuse's site ('land more logos like the ones on your site (BBVA, Actinver, Kuspit)') [teams/sales/2026-04-21-uplead-intent-data-inquiry.md] — implying the market already reads them as partners, while internally they remain commercially dormant.

Action: assign an owner to open a distribution (not custody) conversation with Actinver, Kuspit, and BASE — converting passive custodians into B2B2C resellers of CETES Stablebonds / MXNe to their existing retail clientele, the fastest available lever on the 2%→higher retail-penetration goal.
