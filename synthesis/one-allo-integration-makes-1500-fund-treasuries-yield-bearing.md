# One Allo integration fans Stablebond treasury out to 1,500 fund reserves

> Integrating Stablebonds once into Allo's fund-administration platform turns idle dry powder across its 1,500+ funds into yield-bearing reserves in a single move — the same 'one integration, many endpoints' multiplier as the P0 and CETES-custodian distribution plays, aimed at $2B of VC/PE capital that currently earns zero between capital calls.

Allo administers 1,500+ private funds and $2B+ AUM, with each employee servicing ~70 funds on an AI-powered platform [wiki/entities/companies/alloxyz.md]. That fund-admin layer is a distribution choke point: the proposed Treasury Service Add-On has funds deploy dry powder into Stablebonds between capital calls, and even 5% of the $2B base is ~$100M of deployment [wiki/entities/companies/alloxyz.md].

The leverage is that Etherfuse doesn't have to sell 1,500 funds one at a time — it integrates once into Allo's admin platform and the reserve-yield product becomes a default option across the entire fund book. This is structurally identical to the productized-treasury case Etherfuse already ships (loan reserves and corporate MXN treasury turned from cost center to revenue line via CETES/TESOURO/USTRY at same-day liquidity) [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md], but applied to VC/PE dry powder rather than a single corporate balance sheet. A $50M fund holding $5M in reserves generates $300K–$650K annually instead of $0 [wiki/entities/companies/alloxyz.md] — and Allo's pricing is a revenue share on yield/AUM, which compounds with every fund that flips the toggle.

The fast path: lead with the USTRY/CETES dry-powder add-on (no new legal vehicle needed, unlike the SPV), use it to re-open the stalled deal, then escalate to the Emerging Markets Yield Fund SPV once the integration is live.
