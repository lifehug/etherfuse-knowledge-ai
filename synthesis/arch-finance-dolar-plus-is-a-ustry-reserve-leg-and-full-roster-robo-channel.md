# Arch Finance: Dolar+ is a USTRY reserve-leg, not a single integration

> Arch's Dolar+ already sources yield from a 'diversified selection of digital dollars and active interest generation strategies' — meaning it is a reserve-leg customer that can swap opaque strategy yield for transparent USTRY, and its three risk-tiered robo-portfolios make it a full-roster Stablebond distribution channel into Argentina's dollar-yield demand, not the narrow 'Dolar+ integration point' the wiki notes.

The Arch Finance page scopes the opportunity as evaluating one product — 'Dolar+ (ADDY) as potential Stablebond integration point' — and leaves the lead at Wishlist / low priority [wiki/entities/companies/arch-finance.md]. Two structural facts in the same record reframe it into something larger.

**1. Dolar+ is already a yield aggregator, so USTRY is a reserve-leg swap, not a bolt-on.** Arch describes Dolar+/ADDY as providing 'exposure to a diversified selection of digital dollars and active interest generation strategies' [wiki/entities/companies/arch-finance.md]. That is the same structure as a synthetic-dollar / robo-yield product that sources returns from other protocols — the exact 'active interest generation' that Etherfuse's own sales materials position against: fintech and DeFi yield tends to be a 'black box' whose returns drift toward opaque strategies, versus a transparent sovereign floor [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md]. The winning pitch is not 'add a product' but 'replace your opaque yield engine's stable leg with USTRY (~3.6% APY, US Treasury-backed, on-chain proof-of-reserves)' [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md] — the same reserve-leg logic Etherfuse applies to synthetic-dollar issuers, applied here to a retail aggregator.

**2. Arch is a non-US robo-advisor, which turns a US regulatory headwind into a distribution advantage.** US policy is drifting toward restricting balance-based stablecoin yield, while sovereign-bond tokens keep their yield because it comes from the instrument, not the protocol [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md]. Arch is Argentine and sells to Argentine retail [wiki/entities/companies/arch-finance.md] — outside that constraint — so it can offer sovereign-backed dollar yield in a market where dollarization demand is structural, exactly the non-USD/dollar-store-of-value gap Etherfuse targets [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

**3. The risk-tiered portfolios are a multi-currency channel, not a single-asset one.** Arch runs three managed portfolios (Aggressive/Moderate/Balanced) plus thematic products, all with no minimums [wiki/entities/companies/arch-finance.md]. That maps cleanly onto the Stablebond roster and the documented carry-trade economics — a Balanced tier anchored on USTRY/CETES, an Aggressive tier running the TESOURO-13%-borrow-USDC-5% spread strategy Etherfuse already documents [shared/bd/why-stablebonds-vs-direct-bonds.md]. One integration exposes six sovereign yields, not one product.

**Why it's easy and fast:** the relationship is pure B2B2C revenue-share (10–60 bps of median AUM by yield tier), so onboarding Arch costs Etherfuse nothing but earns on every dollar Arch routes into Stablebonds [shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md]. The near-zero-cost move is to re-pitch Arch as a reserve-leg + robo-distribution partner rather than leaving it dormant on the wishlist.
