# The repo engine is the unquantified answer to AirTM's yield objection

> AirTM's economics team nearly walked because fast-cycling users (1–2 day holds) appeared to earn almost no yield, but Dave revealed Etherfuse already runs daily and 7-day repos on the underlying CETES — meaning the deal-critical deliverable is simply quantifying a capability that already exists.

In the June 2026 AirTM call, the partner's own legal/finance leads raised the objection that almost sank the business case: AirTM's freelancer users **'pretty much within a day or two withdraw the funds,'** and Laura Seamon said they were **'pretty concerned that there wasn't really a lot of return involved'** at those holding periods [shared/partners/airtm.md]. Carlos pressed specifically on whether repos could close the gap — 'how close is the rate of the repos compared to the yield of [CE]TES... at least earning a very small percentage of yield' on fast-moving funds [shared/partners/airtm.md].

Dave's answer reveals a capability not stated on any product page: **'we do use repos... we use daily and seven day repurchase agreements for safety,'** and confirmed there is a positive spread to capture even on short holds [shared/partners/airtm.md]. This sits on top of the custodied CETES held at BBVA/BASE/Kuspit/Actinver [wiki/concepts/CETES.md] — the assets continually compound so that 'when you sell them back to us, even if it's for a short period of time, the price should be higher than it was when you bought it' [shared/partners/airtm.md].

**The connection:** the objection that threatened a JV escalated to a co-owned regulated issuer (with ~$1.2B annual volume in scope) is not a product gap — it is a **modeling gap**. The repo engine already monetizes T+0/T+1 float; what AirTM needs is the math, which AJ promised to send and Laura asked Etherfuse to 'tell us where we're off.' Delivering a concrete repo-spread × notional-average model for fast-cycling balances is the single highest-leverage follow-up to unblock the deal.

**Broader read:** the same repo-on-CETES mechanism is the technical answer to every fast-cycling float partner (PSPs, remittance, payout wallets), where the 'funds move too fast to earn yield' objection is the default sales blocker. It deserves to be a named, quantified line in the partner pitch rather than a detail surfaced only when a partner's CFO digs for it.
