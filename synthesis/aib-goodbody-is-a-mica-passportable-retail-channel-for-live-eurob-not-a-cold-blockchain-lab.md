# AIB/Goodbody: MiCA-passportable retail channel for live EUROB, not a cold lab prospect

> The AIB page treats it as an untouched blockchain-lab research target, but Etherfuse already ships EUROB and Ireland has a documented retail yield gap that AIB's wholly-owned Goodbody wealth arm can close under a single Central Bank of Ireland CASP license that passports EU-wide.

The AIB wiki page frames the bank as a prospective target 'on the radar' with no engagement and open questions about who runs its innovation lab [wiki/entities/companies/aib.md]. That framing misses three facts already sitting in the same neighborhood that collapse the sales cycle.

**1. The product is already live for this market.** EUROB is a live EUR Stablebond backed by eight eurozone sovereigns (Austria, Belgium, Finland, France, Germany, Italy, Poland, Spain) custodied at Spiko [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. Ireland is a eurozone member with no independent currency [wiki/markets/Ireland.md] — so an Irish bank does not need Etherfuse to build a new token before distributing euro sovereign yield to its customers. The distribution motion can start today.

**2. There is a concrete, quantified demand gap.** Irish bank deposit rates average 0.1–0.5% while sovereign yields ran materially higher (5-year IGB ~3.0–3.5% in 2023–24), and Irish retail cannot participate directly in IGB auctions — they are channeled to administratively-priced NTMA State Savings products (Prize Bonds, Savings Certificates) instead [wiki/markets/Ireland.md]. This is precisely the retail-yield-gap democratization play. AIB's wholly-owned **Goodbody Stockbrokers** is a leading Irish stockbroking and wealth-management firm [wiki/entities/companies/aib.md] — a ready B2B2C distribution channel into exactly the retail base locked out of direct sovereign access. Because Etherfuse's model buys *secondary* sovereign debt rather than primary auctions [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md], Goodbody's secondary-market capability is sufficient; no primary-dealer status is required.

**3. One relationship yields an EU passport.** Ireland transposed MiCA in 2024, making the Central Bank of Ireland the competent authority whose CASP licence passports across the entire EU [wiki/markets/Ireland.md]. Ireland's strategic value to Etherfuse is explicitly as an 'EU regulatory gateway and fund administration hub' hosting Coinbase, Gemini, Revolut, and Stripe [wiki/markets/Ireland.md]. Landing AIB is therefore both a revenue channel and a regulatory beachhead.

**Bonus upsell — the missing Irish leg.** EUROB conspicuously omits AA-rated Ireland (Moody's Aa3 / S&P AA / Fitch AA, one of the fastest deleveraging paths in the eurozone) [wiki/markets/Ireland.md; shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. AIB/Goodbody is a natural Irish-paper sourcing partner to add Ireland to the EUROB basket — the same local-institution 'source the paper + distribute retail' pattern Etherfuse runs elsewhere. This turns a single AIB relationship into: (a) immediate EUROB retail distribution, (b) a native Irish IGB Stablebond for the yield gap, and (c) an EU-wide MiCA passport.
