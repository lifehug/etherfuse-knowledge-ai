# 3iQ is the missing fund-wrapper for the Stablebond index NIC already asked for

> 3iQ is an institutional crypto fund manager flagged as a capital-and-distribution partner, and the same Kazakhstan neighborhood already contains an explicit, unfilled demand for exactly the product 3iQ exists to build — a regulated wrapper around an Etherfuse Stablebond index.

The 3iQ page treats the relationship as a single panel handshake at Solana Summit Kazakhstan, with open questions about whether the value is capital or distribution [wiki/entities/companies/3iq.md]. That framing misses a concrete, already-surfaced use case sitting one document away.

**The unfilled demand.** In the NIC NBK meeting, NIC explicitly asked about on-chain index funds, and Dave proposed "an index of Etherfuse stable bonds = ~6% blended yield, diversified across strong and emerging market currencies" [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. NIC is building a fund-of-funds with a dedicated on-chain bucket and is currently parking idle USD pre-deployment [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. That is a buyer asking for a packaged, diversified Stablebond product — not raw single-country tokens.

**The capability gap.** Etherfuse issues individual Stablebonds and even runs its own NAV-anchored market-making framework [shared/products/market-making.md], but nothing in the neighborhood shows Etherfuse operating a regulated fund/index wrapper. 3iQ is precisely an institutional fund manager whose business is wrapping crypto exposure into regulated, allocator-friendly vehicles, and it is described as a dual capital + distribution partner with an institutional LP/client base [wiki/entities/companies/3iq.md]. A Stablebond index packaged by 3iQ converts the loose "6% blended" pitch into a product NIC and similar Gulf/Central-Asian allocators can actually buy.

**Why now / why it compounds.** The same NIC notes describe a planned customer-led Series B with Shinhan, Stellar, BBVA and possibly Halyk [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]; 3iQ's flagged capital role makes it a natural addition to that round, and a fund-distribution partnership and a cap-table investment reinforce each other. It also lines up with the AIFC credit-rating upgrade thesis (B+ → A unlocking institutional allocators) — a 3iQ-managed index wrapper is the distribution surface that upgrade is meant to feed.

**Decision-useful next step:** rather than the page's vague "follow-up call," approach Matteo Perruccio with a specific ask — co-develop a 3iQ-wrapped Etherfuse Stablebond index targeting NIC NBK and GCC family-office allocators, and fold 3iQ into the Series B conversation. This turns a thin one-source contact into a product-plus-capital play.
