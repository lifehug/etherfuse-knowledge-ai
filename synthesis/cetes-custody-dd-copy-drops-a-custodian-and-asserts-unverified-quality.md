# CETES due-diligence copy understates AND overstates custody

> The exact legal/marketing documents Etherfuse hands to rating agencies and institutional DD name only three of the four CETES custodians and assert a 'Category I' quality the KB itself rates as unverified — a fixable credibility gap that sits directly on the institutional-partner conversion path.

Etherfuse's product canon names **four** regulated CETES custodians — BBVA, Casa de Bolsa BASE, Kuspit, and Actinver — explicitly to distribute custodial risk [wiki/concepts/CETES.md][shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. But the two documents written for institutional consumption tell a narrower story:

- The finalized website asset-protection copy states backing assets are held at Category I institutions **'including BBVA México, Actinver Casa de Bolsa, and Kuspit Casa de Bolsa'** — dropping Casa de Bolsa BASE [shared/bd/website-asset-protection-rewrite.md].
- The bankruptcy-remoteness communication guide, which instructs how to speak to **'customers, partners, rating agencies, and in sales conversations,'** repeats the same three-name list and explicitly tells the team to **'name Category I institutions with capitalization data'** when speaking to rating agencies [shared/bd/bankruptcy-remoteness-communication-guide.md].

So the strongest selling point of the structure — **four** independent regulated custodians, not three — is being undersold in precisely the materials where counterparty-risk diversification matters most (Anchorage-grade DD, rating agencies).

Worse, the quality claim being made is one the KB cannot stand behind. Actinver's wiki page rates the 'Category I / 15.23% capitalization index' characterization as **'low trust'** — '0/4 (0%) high-trust... none have been independently corroborated by an authoritative source' — and flags that it 'has not yet been independently corroborated' [wiki/entities/companies/actinver.md]. Yet the DD copy presents Category I status as settled fact to rating agencies [shared/bd/bankruptcy-remoteness-communication-guide.md].

**Why it matters / the easy win:** Winning institutional partners 'who launch their stablecoin strategies on us' depends on DD packages surviving scrutiny. Two near-zero-cost fixes: (1) add Casa de Bolsa BASE back into the custodian list so the diversification story is complete, and (2) pull the underlying CNBV/capitalization filings to convert the Category I claim from 'reported' to 'authoritative' before it lands in front of a rating agency. Both are documentation tasks, not product work — high ease, and they harden the exact materials our biggest pitches already rely on.
