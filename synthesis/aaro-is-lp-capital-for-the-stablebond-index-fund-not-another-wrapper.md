# Aaro is fund-of-funds LP demand for the Stablebond index, not a direct buyer

> Aaro Capital's defining structure is a fund-OF-funds, which means its real role is not buying Stablebonds directly but allocating institutional LP capital into the regulated Stablebond index wrapper that 3iQ/Allo are already designing and NIC NBK explicitly requested — closing the demand side of a product Etherfuse is otherwise building without a named allocator.

## The mis-framing

The Aaro page treats it as either a direct Stablebond purchaser or a generic 'distribution channel' for selling CETES/KTB/GILTS/USTRY into TradFi [wiki/entities/companies/aaro-capital.md]. The cold outreach pitched the raw asset roster — and led with KTB at 2%, the lowest-yield product — as if Aaro would buy bonds onto its own book [wiki/entities/companies/aaro-capital.md].

But Aaro is explicitly a **fund-of-funds**: an 'institutional-quality' vehicle whose entire business is *allocating LP capital across other funds*, run by a chairman with 30+ years working with alternative investment managers [wiki/entities/companies/aaro-capital.md]. A fund-of-funds doesn't custody short-duration sovereign bills directly — it buys *fund units*.

## The connection no single page makes

Elsewhere in this slice, Etherfuse has independent, repeated demand for exactly the product a crypto fund-of-funds would allocate into: NIC NBK 'asked about on-chain index funds,' and Dave proposed 'an index of Etherfuse stable bonds = ~6% blended yield, diversified across strong and emerging market currencies' [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. The existing 3iQ and Allo findings already establish that a regulated wrapper around that index is being designed.

The synthesis: **Aaro is the missing buyer for that wrapper.** A regulated, diversified, ~6%-blended Stablebond index fund is precisely the 'institutional-quality cryptoasset product' a fund-of-funds exists to allocate into. So the correct motion is not 'sell Aaro six tokens' — it's 'route Aaro as an LP into the index wrapper,' turning a stalled cold email into the demand leg of a product Etherfuse is currently building with named issuers (3iQ/Allo) but no named capital.

## Why this matters for ranking

- **Impact:** Converts a deprioritized cold contact into institutional AUM for the highest-leverage packaged product (the index), rather than a one-off bond sale.
- **Ease:** The pitch asset already exists in the pipeline; Aaro's London base and GILTS affinity [wiki/entities/companies/aaro-capital.md] make sterling-denominated and blended exposure an easy first allocation, and Etherfuse already ships GILTS with Archax custody [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].
- **Speed caveat:** Still a single unanswered cold email, so this is a re-framing of the *next touch*, not an imminent close — re-engage with the index wrapper, not the token list.
