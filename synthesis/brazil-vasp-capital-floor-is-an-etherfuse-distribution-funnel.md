# Brazil's R$37M VASP capital floor is a distribution funnel, not just a consolidation risk

> ABCripto's warning that Brazil's R$37M VASP capital requirement will wipe out smaller operators is, read against the Maimone demand and Etherfuse's already-live TESOURO plus full compliance stack, a signal to convert the squeezed players into Etherfuse infrastructure customers rather than let them die or consolidate into incumbents.

ABCripto's only recorded position is that Brazil's new VASP framework — specifically the **R$37 million minimum capital requirement combined with the operational compliance burden** — is calibrated for large exchanges, not the dozens of smaller players in the market, and it has publicly asked for room for smaller companies to survive [wiki/entities/companies/abcripto.md]. That same operational compliance burden (KYB entity-tree, KYC identity-to-wallet mapping, AML screening against OFAC/EU/UN/UK lists, jurisdiction-aware vulnerable-transaction reporting, proof of reserves) is exactly the stack Etherfuse already operates and markets as a 'total compliance and infrastructural platform,' explicitly built to extend 'to any new jurisdiction's format as markets are added' [shared/partners/2026-06-05-shinhan-compliance-presentation.md].

Demand for precisely this is already in the neighborhood: Maimone & Associados sent a 20-question legal inquiry **on behalf of Brazilian Web3 clients who want to offer Etherfuse Stablebonds via Anchor integration** rather than build their own issuance/custody — and Etherfuse's answers confirm the structure works through partner platforms 'under the same regulatory and contractual framework,' with TESOURO already custodied at B3 [shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md]. TESOURO is live today [shared/bond-roadmap.md], and Resolution 561 — which bans regulated eFX providers from settling cross-border in stablecoins from Oct 1 2026 — explicitly positions TESOURO as the supervised local-currency alternative while noting Etherfuse's VASP-channel routing (Resolutions 519–521) sits outside 561's scope [shared/concepts/brazil-efx-resolution-561.md].

The play: small Brazilian crypto/fintech firms facing a regulatory squeeze from two directions at once (R$37M capital floor + 561's stablecoin-settlement ban) can ride Etherfuse's compliance-as-infrastructure and live TESOURO/PIX rails instead of standing up a fully-capitalized VASP. **Caveat:** the Mexico precedent shows partners interfacing with end users may still need their own VASP license — Tala is forming Santamonica App Services as a dedicated licensed VASP specifically to interface with Etherfuse [wiki/concepts/vasp-licensing.md] — so the wedge is removing the *operational* compliance burden ABCripto flags, not necessarily the license itself. That distinction should be answered by Brazilian counsel before pitching, but the partner pipeline (Maimone's clients, plus Bloque's planned Brazil onramp [shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bloque-etherfuse-onboarding-kickoff.md]) is already pointing at it.
