# Australia is the only AAA market with zero AUD stablecoin — and the MXNe template is ready

> Australia has high crypto adoption (17–25% of adults), US$800M DeFi TVL, and zero local-currency stablecoin of any material size, making it the only AAA-rated market where Etherfuse can simultaneously launch both a CGS Stablebond and a sovereign-backed AUDe stablecoin using the MXNe partnership model Brale already built.

The Australia market page flags that stablecoin usage is 'overwhelmingly USD-pegged (USDT, USDC), with no local-currency stablecoin of material size' [wiki/markets/Australia.md]. The stablecoin ecosystem intelligence confirms non-USD stablecoins are up 260% YoY but total only $1.55B globally [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md], and the competitor landscape shows no player operating in AUD [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md].

Meanwhile, the Brale partnership proposal [shared/partners/2023-12-14-brale-mxne-proposal.md] lays out a fully-templated model for issuing a sovereign-bond-backed stablecoin: Brale provides issuance/redemption/custody infrastructure across nine chains, MXNe is backed by CETES held in Brale's treasury, and the revenue share scales to 90% for Etherfuse at $50M+ market cap. The proposal explicitly states the model is 'intended to replicate across additional jurisdictions.'

Australia is the ideal replication target because:

1. **AAA credit quality** — CGS carry the highest sovereign ratings from all three agencies [wiki/markets/Australia.md], making an AUDe the safest-backed non-USD stablecoin on the market by credit quality.
2. **US$800M DeFi TVL** — material on-chain demand already exists from Australian addresses [wiki/markets/Australia.md], but there is zero local-currency yield product to absorb it.
3. **Bank term deposits average 4.5–5.0%** [wiki/markets/Australia.md] — a CGS-backed AUDe earning comparable or better yield (the RBA cash rate is 4.35%) while being liquid and on-chain would undercut bank deposits on convenience and composability.
4. **CGS already on the Q3 2026 roadmap** [shared/bond-roadmap.md] — the underlying Stablebond is scheduled; the stablecoin wrapper is incremental.

Brazil's Resolution 561 experience [shared/concepts/brazil-efx-resolution-561.md] shows that regulators are increasingly nervous about USD stablecoin dominance creating systemic FX risk. An AUDe backed by domestic sovereign debt is precisely the kind of local-currency alternative regulators want — positioning Etherfuse on the right side of the regulatory trend before ASIC's tokenization framework is finalized.

**Action:** When CGS launches in Q3, bundle an AUDe stablecoin (sovereign-backed, Brale-model) into the go-to-market. First-mover in a $800M-DeFi, AAA-rated, English-speaking market with zero AUD competition.
