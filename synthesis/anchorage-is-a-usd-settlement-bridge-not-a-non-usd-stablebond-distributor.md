# Anchorage's 'promote the US economy' stance defines what the relationship can actually monetize

> Anchorage flatly rejected endorsing non-USD stablecoins, which means the durable revenue from Anchorage is its USD stablecoins settling into local currency via Etherfuse — not distributing the non-USD Stablebond roster to Anchorage clients, and the pitch strategy should be re-pointed accordingly.

When Brandon asked Anchorage to endorse the idea that 'the world needs non-USD stablecoins,' the response was 'absolutely not — our goal is to promote US Economy and we're part of the team' [teams/sales/2026-06-10-bd-daily-sync.md]. This is a hard strategic constraint that reframes the entire relationship.

It aligns with Monica's own read: 'large Mexican institutions aren't interested in on-chain CETES (can get locally). The real opportunity is international fintechs needing non-USD on-chain sovereign yield' [teams/sales/2026-06-19-anchorage-digital.md]. So Anchorage neither wants to champion non-USD assets to its book nor sees strong CETES demand from its Mexican institutions.

The synthesis: Anchorage's value to Etherfuse is not as a distribution channel for the non-USD Stablebond catalog. It is (1) a federally-regulated custody badge that provides institutional credibility for CETES [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage.md], and (2) a bridge that lets Anchorage's *USD* stablecoins reach local-currency finality through Etherfuse rails [shared/partners/anchorage-one-pager.md]. Both monetizable vectors point the same direction — USD-in, local-currency-out settlement — and away from the multi-currency Stablebond distribution the strategy documents originally emphasized [shared/partners/anchorage-digital-strategy.md]. Practical implication: stop pitching Anchorage on TESOURO/GILTS/EUROB/KTB breadth and concentrate on the USD-stablecoin→MXN settlement economics, where the counterparty's incentives actually align. (KTB via Anchorage's APAC stablecoins lead Dongwuk remains a possible exception worth testing, but only if framed as USD→KRW settlement rather than 'non-USD yield.')
