# Anchorage is a one-to-many Mexico corridor, not a single CETES deal

> Integrating Etherfuse once into Anchorage makes CETES the cheap MXN settlement layer for every USD stablecoin Anchorage issues (Tether, Ethena, Western Union, OSL) — turning a custody pilot into a remittance-rail multiplier that the deal framing currently undersells.

Anchorage issues 4+ USD stablecoins under its OCC charter — USA-T (Tether), USDtb (Ethena), USDPT (Western Union), USDGO (OSL) [shared/partners/anchorage-one-pager.md]. Etherfuse's own one-pager already maps the mechanic: any holder of an Anchorage stablecoin in a Porto wallet can swap to CETES and off-ramp via SPEI at 5-20 bps, so "Etherfuse becomes the Mexico settlement layer for Anchorage's entire stablecoin ecosystem" [shared/partners/anchorage-one-pager.md]. Dave's follow-up pushes the higher-value half: "we can create markets between Etherfuse CETES and your US products to create remittance flows via your assets" [shared/partners/anchorage.md]. This dovetails with Anchorage's separately-noted strategic posture — "stablecoin correspondent banking for non-US banks" letting them "settle dollar transactions instantly without Fed access or SWIFT" [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md].

**Why it matters now:** the deal is live and de-risking — Monica confirms "finalizing the asset review for the CETES on Stellar" as of May 27, with a CDMX meet planned for the June stablecoin conference [shared/partners/anchorage.md]. The synthesis no single page states: the CETES-on-Stellar listing is not the prize, it's the on-ramp; once live, every Anchorage stablecoin partner (including Western Union, a remittance giant) inherits a 5-20 bps MXN corridor, and Western Union's own $94B-corridor use case [shared/partners/canton-mexico-infrastructure.md] becomes addressable through a partner we're already onboarding. Push for the WU/USDPT corridor as the named second step the moment CETES support ships.
