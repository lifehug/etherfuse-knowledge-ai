# Agridex is a DeFi collateral venue + agricultural trade-finance vertical

> Agridex is an agricultural RWA DEX on Stellar, so the highest-value motion is listing TESOURO/CETES as yield-bearing collateral for on-chain agricultural credit markets — the Loopscale-on-Solana model applied to the $1.7–2.5T trade-finance gap — not the generic Mexico treasury onboarding the wiki tracks.

## The mis-framing

The Agridex page treats the relationship as a stalled, signed-KYB onboarding to be revived, with the only product question left as an open item: "What specific integration or product usage is Agridex onboarding for?" [wiki/entities/companies/agridex.md]. That question has a strong answer hiding in adjacent sources — and it points to a much larger motion than a peso treasury account.

## What Agridex actually is

Agridex is a DEX focused on real-world assets in the agricultural sector, tagged `dex`, `rwa`, `defi`, and it attended Stellar House CDMX — i.e., it operates on Stellar [wiki/entities/companies/agridex.md]. Etherfuse already ships every Stablebond on Stellar with full smart-wallet (Soroban) support [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md]. So the integration surface is native, not a new build.

## The right product: sovereign collateral for ag-credit markets

Etherfuse's own case-study library already describes exactly this counterparty: "A multi-chain lending protocol needed to diversify its collateral base... wanted to offer non-USD lending markets to attract borrowers in emerging economies, but no regulated, yield-bearing non-USD assets existed on-chain" — solved by listing CETES/TESOURO/GILTS/USTRY as collateral, launching MXN and BRL lending markets, and enabling carry trades where borrowers' effective cost of capital drops by the collateral's 7–13% yield [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md]. This is not hypothetical: the first BRL credit market on Solana already went live via Loopscale integrating Etherfuse TESOURO [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. Agridex is the Stellar-side analog — a DEX that can host the same yield-bearing collateral for agricultural borrowers.

## Why agriculture makes this bigger than a DeFi listing

Agriculture sits squarely in Etherfuse's strongest currencies. Brazil (TESOURO, ~13% APY) is a global agricultural exporter; Mexico (CETES) and the broader LatAm base are commodity-heavy. Global unmet trade-finance and supply-chain-finance demand is $1.7T–$2.5T, with tokenization named as the key mechanism to close it [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. An agricultural RWA DEX that settles trades in yield-bearing local-currency Stablebonds turns idle settlement float and posted collateral into sovereign yield — the same structure Etherfuse proves in the SMB-lender ($700K/yr on $8M reserves across MXN/BRL) and DeFi-collateral case studies [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md].

## Why this out-competes

Every named RWA competitor (Securitize, Ondo, OpenEden, Backed) is USD-only; Etherfuse's differentiator is non-USD sovereign bonds, multi-currency, multi-country [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md]. An agricultural DEX whose borrowers earn and repay in BRL/MXN cannot use a USD-only collateral token without forcing dollarization — which is precisely the gap Stablebonds fill.

## The move

Re-engage Agridex (KYB already signed [wiki/entities/companies/agridex.md]) not on "when are you ready for Mexico" but on a concrete Stellar collateral listing: TESOURO + CETES as collateral for agricultural credit markets, mirroring the Loopscale BRL market. That reframes a Low-priority Nurture lead into a product-usage and asset-distribution channel in Etherfuse's two deepest markets.
