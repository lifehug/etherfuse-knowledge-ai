# AO's $200M is a treasury-float win — but US-based means the Reg-S wall the wiki ignores

> AO is framed as clean 'new geography (US)' validation, but its real value is a $200M idle micro-credit book that maps exactly onto Etherfuse's already-documented Baubap treasury-yield model — while its US domicile collides head-on with the Reg-S non-US-person restriction that Etherfuse's entire US-access thesis is separately trying to solve.

## What the AO page actually says vs. what it misses

The AO wiki treats the lead as an early-discovery opportunity to "expand [[Etherfuse]] adoption into the US merchant micro-credit space," noting only that $200M AUM makes it "a meaningful-sized platform whose integration could validate our product in a new geography." It explicitly wishes for a "replicable playbook for merchant micro-credit platforms" but marks that claim `[source needed]` [wiki/entities/companies/ao-micro-credit.md]. Two connections in this neighborhood resolve both the opportunity and a risk the page never names.

## 1. The playbook already exists — and it's a treasury-yield play, not an 'onboarding'

Etherfuse has a fully-built, ~48-hour-integration microfinance playbook: the lender parks idle loan capital in a yield-bearing sovereign Stablebond between loan cycles, swaps into a local stablecoin to disburse, and swaps back to earn yield until the next cycle — zero-fee ramps, no FX exposure [shared/partners/baubap-integration.md, shared/partners/baubap.md]. The Anchorage one-pager already codifies "Microfinance Platform" and "Fintech Lender" as standing customer personas with real analogues (2M+ customer microlender; 4M-customer fintech lender) [shared/partners/anchorage-one-pager.md]. AO's $200M AUM micro-credit book is precisely this persona: the wedge isn't a generic KYB onboarding, it's yield on the large idle float that sits between micro-loan cycles. That reframing makes AO a treasury-yield customer on day one, not a speculative integration.

## 2. But 'US-based' triggers the exact barrier the company is already fighting

Here is what the AO page silently skips. A US micro-lender's natural Stablebond is USTRY (US Treasuries), but Etherfuse's US posture is "Reg S compliance (non-US persons only)" [shared/overview.md]. AO is described as US-based and serving (US) merchants [wiki/entities/companies/ao-micro-credit.md] — i.e., US persons who cannot hold USTRY directly under Reg S. This is the *same* structural wall that multiple in-flight workstreams exist to break through (the '40 Act / fund-wrapper and offshore-issuer routes to give US investors compliant exposure). Treating AO as clean 'new geography' validation obscures that closing it requires the unsolved US-access structure, not a standard sandbox onboarding.

## Decision-useful takeaway

- Re-scope AO from "discovery / onboarding" to a treasury-float yield pitch using the documented Baubap model as the template [shared/partners/baubap-integration.md].
- Before the follow-up (still pending since April 2026 cold outreach [wiki/entities/companies/ao-micro-credit.md]), qualify AO's investor/entity structure: if the underlying holders are US persons, route through the fund-wrapper/offshore-issuer path rather than direct USTRY, and set expectations accordingly.
- The '[source needed]' Shara-playbook gap on the AO page should point to the Baubap integration doc as the canonical repeatable micro-credit playbook.
