# Nigeria: stacked demand + a rare regulated rail, but no NGN product on the roadmap

> Etherfuse already holds a rare regulated Nigerian fiat/digital-asset partner, has a live $50–75M Meta NGN repatriation mandate, and an inbound Nigerian bank wanting gold tokenization — yet Nigeria carries no NGN Stablebond or named product line, the same unworked-easy-win pattern flagged for Argentina and Bolivia.

Three independent Nigeria signals sit in this neighborhood with no unifying product owner:

1. **Meta repatriation.** Etherfuse priced a $50–75M one-time NGN→stablecoin→USD trapped-capital flow for Meta, and explicitly notes its Nigeria local partner is a "Regulated local digital-asset and fiat-rail partner with NGN deposit/withdrawal coverage and onshore compliance controls. Rare in the Nigerian market — many stablecoin desks are offshore-only routing through informal P2P." [teams/ceo/docs/inbox/2026-05-01-meta-round2-pricing-brazil-nigeria.md]
2. **Gold tokenization inbound.** "A Nigerian bank has reportedly expressed interest in tokenizing its physical gold holdings via the Etherfuse platform" with a follow-up scheduled. [wiki/concepts/gold-tokenization.md] This maps onto the same commodity-exporter treasury vertical Etherfuse already runs (the 9 Dots / ORO playbook).
3. **Standard Chartered Africa coverage.** SC's pipeline rollout via the Stellar partnership explicitly names Ghana and Kenya, with "40+ direct footprint markets" addressing cash accounts, FX, correspondent banking, and custody. [teams/ceo/2026-06-15-standard-chartered-strategic.md]

**The gap:** the regulated NGN rail Etherfuse already paid to establish for Meta is a reusable asset — it is the hardest piece to acquire in Nigeria, and it currently underpins exactly one one-time flow. The same rail plus SC's correspondent-banking coverage is the foundation for a recurring NGN settlement product and a gold-backed treasury offering for the inbound bank. Nigeria is the African analogue to the Argentina and Bolivia 'stacked demand absent from the bond roadmap' findings: warm demand and infrastructure already in hand, but no product instantiated to convert it.
