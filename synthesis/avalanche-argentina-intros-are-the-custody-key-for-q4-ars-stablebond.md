# Avalanche's BIND/Comafi Intros Solve Argentina's Missing Custody Leg

> Leandro Davo's volunteered intros to Banco Industrial (BIND) and Banco Comafi are the untracked path to the local custody partner the Q4 2026 ARS Stablebond requires — the same role Halyk plays in Kazakhstan, Shinhan in Korea, BBVA in Mexico, and IBT is proposed for in Tajikistan — making these intros potentially worth more to Etherfuse's roadmap than the $3M Avalanche LOI they're attached to.

## The Gap

The bond roadmap schedules an Argentine Treasury Bill (ARS) Stablebond for Q4 2026 [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. Distribution in Argentina has a named solution (Alfred Pay holds a VASP license there). But there is **no identified custody partner** to purchase and hold Argentine sovereign bonds — the structural prerequisite for every Stablebond launch.

## The Intros Sitting Untracked

During the May 5, 2026 negotiation call, Leandro Davo committed to introducing Etherfuse to two Argentine banks:

- **Banco Industrial (BIND)** — described as Argentina's 'primary fintech bank'
- **Banco Comafi (Coinak)** — alternative; Coinak is its crypto subsidiary [wiki/entities/companies/avalabs.md]

These intros are logged in the Avalabs page's open questions section — 'Argentina banking intros (BIND, Comafi) — when to engage?' — with **no follow-up tracked and no owner assigned** [wiki/entities/companies/avalabs.md].

## Why This Maps Onto the Proven Playbook

Every Etherfuse market launch follows the same pattern: a local bank acquires the underlying sovereign paper in the primary or secondary market and holds it in custody while Etherfuse issues and manages the on-chain token.

| Market | Local Bank Partner | Role | Source |
|--------|--------------------|------|--------|
| Mexico | BBVA, Casa de Bolsa BASE, Kuspit, Actinver | Custody / purchase | [shared/master-knowledge-base.md] |
| Korea | Shinhan Securities | Custody / purchase | [shared/master-knowledge-base.md] |
| Brazil | B3 | Custody | [wiki/concepts/tesouro.md] |
| Kazakhstan | Halyk Finance | Custody / nominee account | [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-framework.md] |
| Tajikistan (proposed) | IBT | Primary-market purchase + custody | [shared/partners/2026-05-30-etherfuse-ibt-concept-brief.md] |
| **Argentina** | **None identified** | — | [shared/bond-roadmap.md] |

BIND and Comafi, both Argentine banks with fintech/crypto operations, are exactly the candidate institutions for this role. The Halyk Finance six-element framework (segregated nominee account, secondary-market purchases, monthly reporting, trade confirmations, independent auditor, monthly public attestation) is a ready-made operational template to share with them during initial conversations [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-framework.md].

## The Relative Value

The Avalanche deal is tracked at $3M LOI — meaningful but bounded. If BIND or Comafi converts into the Argentina custody partner, it enables an entire country launch in the world's third-largest Latin American economy. The IBT concept brief illustrates the scale: even capturing 0.5–1% of Tajikistan's remittance flow yields $29–58M annual Stablebond volume [shared/partners/2026-05-30-etherfuse-ibt-concept-brief.md]. Argentina's economy and bond market dwarf Tajikistan's.

## Recommended Action

Follow up with Leandro on the BIND/Comafi intros independently of the $3M LOI outcome. Assign an owner. Send the Halyk framework document as the operational starting point for the custody conversation. These intros should be treated as a separate pipeline item, not a contingent side benefit of the Avalanche treasury deal.
