# Circle sits on both sides of the Abound deal — accelerant and shared incentive

> Circle Ventures is an Abound investor while Circle is simultaneously Etherfuse's institutional USDC partner, and Abound already clears ~20% of India's USDC off-ramp — meaning all three parties share a single incentive (grow USDC↔INR liquidity) that makes Circle a warm accelerant rather than a neutral rail.

Abound raised its $14M Series A from NEAR Foundation, Circle Ventures, and Times Internet [SALES-424], and Abound 'accounts for approximately 20% of USDC off-ramp volume in India' [shared/meetings/2026-04-27-abound-times-of-india-inr-stablecoin.md]. On the other side, Etherfuse holds a Circle institutional partnership enabling instant, zero-fee USDC↔USD conversion that underpins its remittance and FX flows [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. 

Circle therefore has a direct equity stake in Abound and a commercial stake in Etherfuse, and both relationships point at the same outcome: more USDC converting to/from INR. The INR Stablebond/yield-bond product Etherfuse and Abound are building [shared/meetings/2026-04-27-abound-times-of-india-inr-stablecoin.md] would give Abound's USDC off-ramp a yield-bearing INR landing asset — exactly the kind of usage that grows Circle's India footprint. This is a usable lever in two ways: (1) Circle Ventures' presence on Abound's cap table is a trust/diligence signal that should shorten the LOI cycle, and (2) Etherfuse can frame the deal to Circle as a USDC-adoption win to pull Circle in as a co-marketing or introductions partner for the broader India corridor, rather than treating Circle purely as a settlement vendor.
