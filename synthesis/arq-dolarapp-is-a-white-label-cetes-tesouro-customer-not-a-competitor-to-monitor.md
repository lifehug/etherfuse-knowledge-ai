# ARQ (DolarApp) is a white-label CETES+TESOURO customer, not a competitor to monitor

> ARQ just raised $70M to build exactly the 'high-yield local accounts' in Mexico and Brazil that Etherfuse already ships as CETES and TESOURO — and because it cannot replicate Etherfuse's CNBV/Brazil regulatory moat, the fast play is to supply the yield to its 2M users before it stands up a black-box alternative.

## The mismatch

The ARQ page files it as a passive "market signal and competitive/contextual reference point" with an open question of whether it is "a potential partner, competitor, or customer" [wiki/entities/companies/arq-dolarapp.md]. That framing understates a ready B2B2C target. ARQ raised $70M from Sequoia/Founders Fund specifically "to expand into high-yield local accounts and lending across the region," with Mexico and Brazil as primary markets and ~2M users processing $10B+ annualized volume [wiki/entities/companies/arq-dolarapp.md].

"High-yield local accounts in Mexico and Brazil" is not adjacent to Etherfuse's product — it *is* CETES (MXN, ~5.6% APY) and TESOURO (BRL, ~13% APY), both live [shared/bond-roadmap.md, shared/overview.md].

## This is the digital-wallet case study, almost line-for-line

Etherfuse's own published case study is "Digital Wallet Adds Yield-Bearing Savings for **2M+ users** across Latin America" — allocating balances to CETES and TESOURO, with the wallet earning a 10–60 bps revenue share on distributed AUM [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md]. ARQ's profile (2M users, MX+BR, pivoting from USD access to local-currency yield) matches that composite scenario almost exactly [wiki/entities/companies/arq-dolarapp.md]. The revenue mechanics are already priced: yield-tier revenue share of 10–60 bps of median monthly AUM plus 5–20 bps ramp fees [shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md]. On $10B+ annualized flow, even modest float and AUM capture outweighs most of the current pipeline.

## Why ARQ can't just build it — the moat forces a partnership

Etherfuse is the only company with CNBV authorization to tokenize Mexican government bonds (Resolution P090/2024) and the only regulated issuer in Brazil, Korea, and Mexico [shared/master-knowledge-base.md, shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Etherfuse's own competitor analysis notes that fintech/neobank yield is "usually funded by riskier asset composition or subsidized by CAC... Black box — yields tend toward central bank rate," versus Etherfuse's transparent sovereign backing [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md]. So ARQ's build path is either a lower-quality, CAC-subsidized black box, or white-labeling Etherfuse's institutional platform — which ships compliance, reporting, fiat rails (SPEI + PIX), and partner APIs out of the box [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md]. The whitelabel-KYB and embedded-wallet stack means ARQ could offer branded local-currency savings without building brokerage infrastructure in two jurisdictions.

## Added urgency: Brazil Resolution 561

If any part of ARQ's Brazil stablecoin business touches cross-border settlement, BCB Resolution 561 (effective Oct 1, 2026) bans regulated fintechs/eFX providers from using stablecoins to settle cross-border payments [shared/concepts/brazil-efx-resolution-561.md]. Etherfuse's TESOURO / VASP-channel routing sits outside 561's direct scope, and local-currency yield instruments are explicitly positioned as the compliant alternative to USD stablecoin rails [shared/concepts/brazil-efx-resolution-561.md]. That converts a looming compliance problem for a Brazil stablecoin fintech into an Etherfuse pitch.

## Warm-network note

ARQ's founders are Revolut alumni [wiki/entities/companies/arq-dolarapp.md], and the same neighborhood carries a live (if stalled) Revolut lead — LEA-232, cold outreach bounced [wiki/entities/companies/Revolut.md]. Neither is a clean bridge on its own, but both point at the same idle-multi-currency-balance thesis Etherfuse pitches Revolut ("savings vaults backed by sovereign bonds instead of bank deposit passthrough") [wiki/entities/companies/Revolut.md].

## Recommended move

Reclassify ARQ from "monitor" to an outbound B2B2C target: lead with the 2M-user digital-wallet case study and the CNBV/Brazil regulatory moat (ARQ cannot legally replicate it), and use Resolution 561 as the Brazil-specific hook. Assign an owner and a contact — the page currently has zero contacts on file [wiki/entities/companies/arq-dolarapp.md].
