# iExec's confidential-computing layer is the missing unlock for the $100B Korea derivatives-collateral play, not a generic 'when we have eng capacity' nurture

> iExec is parked as an exploratory 'privacy demand is secondary' nurture, but the exact barrier its TEE selective-disclosure solves — proving compliance without exposing on-chain positions — is the unstated blocker for the named institutional deals in this slice (Shinhan AM's $100B derivatives collateral, NIC NBK sovereign allocations), which reframes iExec from speculative tech fit to an enabler tied to concrete revenue.

**iExec's own thesis names the barrier.** iExec's TEE-based confidential-computing layer offers "selective disclosure enabling compliance-friendly private transactions targeting institutional adoption blocked by on-chain transparency concerns," letting institutions "prove compliance without exposing positions" [wiki/entities/companies/iexec.md]. Its deal is stuck at Nurture with the explicit blocker that "privacy demand still secondary to liquidity/lending" and no engineering commitment [wiki/entities/companies/iexec.md]. That prioritization looks wrong once you line it up against the biggest institutional opportunities in the same neighborhood.

**Shinhan AM's $100B use case is fundamentally a position-privacy problem.** Heyryung Choi's proposal is to tokenize Korean MMFs/ETFs as collateral for offshore derivatives so Korean capital stops fleeing ($180B in stablecoin outflows in 2025; ~$100B specifically for derivatives collateral) — a fully-regulated, FSS/FSC-supervised build where every transaction is reported to government [shared/partners/2026-06-04-shinhan-asset-management-clevel.md]. Institutional derivatives collateral posted on a public chain exposes strategy and size to the entire market; that on-chain transparency is exactly the adoption barrier iExec exists to remove. Etherfuse's role there is already 'public-chain tokenization bridge after Canton' [shared/partners/2026-06-04-shinhan-asset-management-clevel.md][shared/partners/2026-06-05-shinhan-cover-letter.md] — the public-chain step is precisely where selective disclosure becomes the difference between a regulator saying yes or no.

**The same privacy gap recurs in other institutional pitches.** NIC NBK (sovereign wealth, building on-chain investment infrastructure) evaluates counterparty and custody risk and would not want its sovereign allocation positions readable on-chain [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Etherfuse's institutional platform pitch already leans on compliance/reporting as its differentiator [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md]; iExec adds the one capability that stack lacks — confidential positions with provable compliance — and it credibly ships with Intel/IBM/Microsoft TEE backing [wiki/entities/companies/iexec.md].

**Implication:** Re-scope iExec from a technology curiosity to a named-deal enabler. The next iExec conversation should be framed around Shinhan AM's derivatives-collateral privacy requirement, and the outstanding action (Erwan sharing institutional-privacy market research) should be pulled forward and pointed at Korea, not left to 'after testnet.'
