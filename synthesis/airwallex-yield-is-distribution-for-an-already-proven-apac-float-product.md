# Airwallex Yield is ready distribution for Etherfuse's already-proven APAC float product

> Airwallex's $1B+ AuA 'Airwallex Yield' product and its multi-currency APAC settlement business are an exact match for the float-yield case study Etherfuse already ships and the SE-Asian Stablebond roadmap already in flight — yet the deal sits at 'Event / priority not set' on a hook that undersells it.

**The focal page sells Airwallex short.** Airwallex is logged as a Money2020 Asia Tier-B event contact with ECV and priority both 'Not set,' and the hook is generic: 'complementary underlying asset… CETES 5.6%, TESOURO 12.29%… six currencies, one integration' [wiki/entities/companies/airwallex.md]. But Airwallex's own stated top priority is *Building Airwallex Yield ($1B+ AuA)* on top of $1.2B ARR and a $1B+ US/EMEA expansion [wiki/entities/companies/airwallex.md] — i.e. they are a global payments platform building a yield product and shopping for underlying assets at scale. That is the highest-intent buyer profile Etherfuse has, not a passive 'diversification' pitch.

**Etherfuse already has a packaged, documented product for exactly Airwallex's business.** The Hong Kong PSP case study describes a multi-currency APAC settlement operation parking idle PHP/IDR/THB/VND nostro float into USTRY (~4.5% APY) during 48–72h batch windows, while collapsing six correspondent-bank relationships into one integration and cutting corridor FX from 80–150bps to 10–22bps [shared/bd/case-study-psp-hongkong.md]. Airwallex is the same business one or two orders of magnitude larger — a global treasury/payments platform running multi-currency settlement float. The case study is, in effect, a redacted Airwallex pitch deck. The hook should be reframed from 'complementary underlying asset' to 'here is a live, written case study of an APAC PSP running your exact model on our rails.'

**The Stablebond roadmap already targets Airwallex's corridor currencies.** Airwallex Yield's gap is that idle local-currency balances earn nothing, and a USD-only money-market product forces dollarization. Etherfuse's Standard Chartered build covers PHP (Q2), MYR/GHS (Q3), IDR/VND/KES (Q4) plus HKD [shared/partners/standard-chartered-flow-of-funds.md], and the Shinhan/BBVA pipeline adds THB, PHP, MYR, IDR, VND, HKD [shared/partners/kaia-stablebond-overview.md] — precisely the APAC float currencies in the PSP case study. So Etherfuse can offer Airwallex Yield *local-currency sovereign yield on in-corridor balances without dollarizing*, a differentiator no USD MMF (the standard Airwallex Yield input) can match.

**Why it matters / next step:** A single integration turns Airwallex's $1B+ AuA yield product into a Stablebond distribution channel across six-plus markets, and the conversion friction is low because Etherfuse already has (a) the proof artifact and (b) the underlying assets either live or scheduled. The action is to elevate Airwallex off 'priority not set,' route the PSP case study + APAC roadmap currencies to Brian Joseph (Director, Financial Partnerships) [wiki/entities/companies/airwallex.md], and pitch local-currency-yield-on-float rather than generic EM diversification.
