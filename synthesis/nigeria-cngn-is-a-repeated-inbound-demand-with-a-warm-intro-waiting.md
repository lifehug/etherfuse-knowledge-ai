# Nigeria yield demand + a volunteered CNGN intro is an unworked market opening

> Dave is repeatedly asked for Nigerian yield and has a standing, volunteered warm introduction to CNGN (Nigeria's regulated stablecoin, whose distributor's investor 'Yuan' is close to the CNGN team) — a fast path into a market the Freedom case study already sizes at 26M stablecoin users.

This demand signal appears three times across the neighborhood and has never been consolidated into an action:

1. In the BeKey/bmoni call Dave says plainly: 'I keep getting people asking me for Nigerian yield... we don't have ramps, we don't have anything,' and bmoni immediately offers to introduce him to CNGN, adding that their backer 'Yuan's actually invested in them... they're very dear friends to us' and one of CNGN's strongest distributors [shared/partners/bekey.md].
2. The follow-up bmoni onboarding meeting logs the same thread as a live action item — 'Natasha to introduce Dave to CNGN (Nigerian stablecoin) team for a parallel integration conversation' [shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bmoni-etherfuse-kyb-business-onboarding.md].
3. The Freedom case study independently cites Nigeria as proof the adoption curve is real: '26 million stablecoin users (12% of its population)' [shared/partners/freedom-etherfuse-case-study.md].

The pieces fit together into a near-zero-cost play: CNGN supplies the regulated NGN stablecoin and on/off-ramps Etherfuse lacks, bmoni/Yuan supplies the warm intro and distribution, and Etherfuse supplies the yield product — either sourcing Nigerian sovereign paper 'through one of our brokers in Eurasia' as Dave floated, or backing an NGN Stablebond the way MEKKAM/KTB were built. The one caution already documented internally: Nigeria is a high-risk jurisdiction requiring enhanced due diligence in KYB [wiki/concepts/KYB.md], so structure the entry through CNGN's existing regulated rails rather than direct retail. This is a genuine 'go where people aren't served yet' opening with the introduction already offered and unbooked.
