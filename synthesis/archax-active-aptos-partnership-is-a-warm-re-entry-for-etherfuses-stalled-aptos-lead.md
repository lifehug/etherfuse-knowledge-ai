# Etherfuse's custodian is Aptos's active RWA partner while Etherfuse's own Aptos lead sits stalled

> Archax — Etherfuse's GILTS custodian — has a live, executive-level RWA partnership with Aptos, while Etherfuse's direct Aptos deal has been dormant in Nurture since Q1 2026, making the custodian a warm co-pitch bridge that the Aptos page never considers.

The Archax and Aptos pages in this same neighborhood describe two relationships that should be connected but aren't:

- Archax "expanded its tokenization footprint to the Aptos blockchain, partnering with Aptos to bring RWAs on-chain" (starting with MembersCap's tokenized reinsurance fund), and Archax CEO Graham R. co-presented on "RWA regulatory momentum on Aptos" alongside Aptos Labs' Ryan Zega and Marissa Trew in March 2026 [wiki/entities/companies/archax.md].
- Etherfuse's own Aptos lead (SALES-79) went quiet through Q1 2026, sits at Nurture with no recorded deal value, and its last logged step was David Yao merely "planning to reach out" in April 2026 [wiki/entities/companies/Aptos.md].

Etherfuse already has strong personal relationship capital at Aptos Foundation (Andy's ex-Bitso colleague; David Yao's ITAM classmate) [wiki/entities/companies/Aptos.md], but the missing accelerant is an institutional proof point on Aptos itself. Archax is exactly that — a shared, credible RWA node that already custodies BlackRock, Fidelity, State Street, abrdn, and LGIM tokenized MMFs [wiki/entities/companies/archax.md] and is already building on Aptos. A refreshed Aptos GTM should co-anchor on GILTS (already Archax-custodied) landing on Aptos, using Archax's existing Aptos partnership as the warm bridge rather than working the stalled foundation lead cold. This turns a maintenance-mode custody relationship into a distribution vector for a chain lead that has otherwise gone nowhere.
