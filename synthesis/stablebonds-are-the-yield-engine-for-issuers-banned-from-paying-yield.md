# Every stablecoin-yield ban makes Etherfuse more valuable, not less

> Regulators worldwide (US White House, Mexico's AVE framework) are converging on banning balance-based stablecoin yield — but each ban turns Etherfuse into the only legal way for regulated issuers to deliver yield, because Stablebond returns come from the sovereign instrument, not the protocol.

A repeatable structural moat is hiding across the pipeline. The Alfred Pay notes state plainly that Etherfuse "is not an IFPA; acts as treasury/yield provider to IFPAs, which legally cannot offer yield themselves — strong positioning advantage in front of FAS partners" [shared/partners/alfred-pay-2026-04-22.md]. The stablecoins intel file generalizes the macro driver: the White House is "floating limited stablecoin rewards tied only to transaction activity (not balances)" and "if stablecoin yield gets restricted to transaction activity only, stablebonds (tokenized government bonds) have a structural regulatory advantage — yield comes from the sovereign instrument, not the protocol" [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md]. The same logic underpins the BBVA AVE dynamic already noted, and every issuer deal in the book is structured so the issuer captures Stablebond yield: Alfred earns 100% of yield if retained / 50-50 if passed through [shared/partners/alfred-pay-2026-04-22.md]; AirTM gets "yield revenue" on collateral [shared/partners/airtm-partnership-proposal.md]; Bloque's model is "low brokerage + yield on assets, avoiding licensing costs" [shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bloque-etherfuse-onboarding-kickoff.md].

**The non-obvious connection:** these are usually pitched as separate deals, but they share one sales thesis that should be the lead message to every stablecoin/IFPA issuer in pipeline — *you legally cannot pay your users yield; we can wrap a Stablebond that does, under your brand, and the harder regulators clamp down on stablecoin yield, the wider our moat gets.* This converts a regulatory headwind for the entire stablecoin sector ($13B+ already in yield-bearing stables [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md]) into Etherfuse's wedge. It is the fastest, cheapest message to standardize across Alfred, AirTM, Bloque, B3, Galileo, and the IFPA/neobank segment generally.
