# Anchorage's non-US-bank correspondent product is the real Etherfuse distribution multiplier

> Anchorage launched a stablecoin correspondent-banking product letting non-US banks settle dollars without Fed/SWIFT access — and every one of those banks needs an EM local-currency leg, which is exactly Etherfuse's CETES/TESOURO/roadmap settlement, a far larger channel than the four stablecoins the current one-pager pitches.

The active Etherfuse↔Anchorage pitch frames Anchorage as a USD-stablecoin issuer (USA-T/Tether, USDtb/Ethena, USDPT/Western Union, USDGO/OSL) that needs a cheap MXN off-ramp, and proposes Etherfuse as the Mexico settlement layer at 5–20 bps [shared/partners/anchorage-one-pager.md]. That framing undersells the bigger surface.

On 2026-02-19 Anchorage launched **stablecoin solutions for non-US banks as a correspondent-banking alternative — letting non-US banks settle dollar transactions instantly without Fed access or SWIFT** [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md]. Each of those non-US banks (think LATAM, Asia, MENA frontier institutions) ultimately has to convert USD into a *local currency* for their customers — precisely the leg Etherfuse already operates as regulated FX/settlement desks and is building across its 50+ country bond roadmap [wiki/entities/companies/Etherfuse.md].

So the structural play is not 'four stablecoins get an MXN ramp' — it's that Anchorage's correspondent-banking network is a *distribution funnel of foreign banks* that each need EM-currency settlement, and Etherfuse is the only CNBV-authorized, multi-custodian sovereign-backed rail positioned to be the default local-currency endpoint behind it [shared/partners/anchorage-digital-strategy.md, shared/partners/anchorage-one-pager.md]. The pitch should be reframed from a product integration to a co-marketed settlement layer for Anchorage's entire non-US correspondent book.
