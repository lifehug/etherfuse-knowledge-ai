# Allo's RWA marketplace is a same-day listing channel for the 6 live Stablebonds

> All three partnership structures on Allo's wiki page require building something new (an SPV, a white-label stack, or a revenue-share treasury add-on), but Allo already self-positions as the largest RWA marketplace with 1,600+ RWAs listed and 20,000+ HNW investors — meaning the fastest, lowest-effort win is simply listing Etherfuse's six already-live Stablebonds as new inventory on a marketplace that exists to distribute exactly this asset class.

The Allo.xyz page frames the opportunity around three structures — a treasury add-on, a dedicated 'Allocations Emerging Markets Yield Fund' SPV, and white-label Stablebond infrastructure — all of which are net-new builds gated on legal/technical work and a pilot [wiki/entities/companies/allo-xyz-allocations.md]. But the same page records that Allo brands itself as the largest RWA marketplace with $2.2B+ in assets, 1,600+ RWAs listed, and a base of 20,000+ high-net-worth investors, and is actively expanding ecosystem partnerships (Mantra confirmed; Bitlayer, NuBit pending) [wiki/entities/companies/allo-xyz-allocations.md].

Etherfuse already ships six live Stablebonds — CETES, TESOURO, GILTS, EUROB, USTRY, KTB — with named regulated custodians and monthly proof-of-reserves [shared/bond-roadmap.md, shared/overview.md]. These are precisely the kind of yield-bearing RWA inventory Allo's marketplace exists to distribute, and they carry the built-in KYC/KYB, Reg-S restrictions, and attestation reporting that HNW/institutional buyers demand [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md, shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md]. Listing them requires no SPV formation, no white-label integration, and no capital-call treasury deployment — it's product Etherfuse can hand over today.

Ranked by impact × speed × ease, a marketplace listing beats all three logged structures: it puts existing assets in front of 20,000+ qualified investors immediately, it is a distribution motion (not an engineering project), and it can run in parallel with the slower SPV/index work already scoped in the neighborhood. It also de-risks the deal — a live listing generating AUM is the natural proof point that justifies the larger 'Emerging Markets Yield Fund' SPV later. This channel is distinct from the Stablebond-index-wrapper thesis (which builds a new bundled product); here Etherfuse is distributing the current roster as-is. Given the deal has sat at 'Nurture / no activity' since March 2026 [wiki/entities/companies/allo-xyz-allocations.md], re-engaging Luis Brecci with 'list our six live products on your marketplace' is a lower-friction re-open than pitching a fund build.
