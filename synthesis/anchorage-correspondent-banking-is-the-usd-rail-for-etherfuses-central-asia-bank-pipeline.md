# Anchorage's non-US-bank correspondent product is the USD rail Etherfuse's Central Asia banks are begging for

> Anchorage launched a stablecoin correspondent-banking alternative that lets non-US banks settle USD instantly without Fed access or SWIFT — the exact pain point Freedom Bank and the rest of Etherfuse's institutional Central Asia pipeline described in person, making an Anchorage co-sell far more concrete than the vague 'custody/issuance' framing on Anchorage's page.

Anchorage's page frames the relationship as generic 'institutional custody and stablecoin issuance' [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital-bank.md]. But a sharper, revenue-relevant fit is hiding in the stablecoins intelligence: **Anchorage Digital launched stablecoin solutions for non-US banks as a correspondent-banking alternative — settling dollar transactions instantly without Fed access or SWIFT** [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md].

That product solves a problem Etherfuse's own institutional pipeline states explicitly. Freedom Bank Kazakhstan told Dave and Mike in person that its **China cross-border flows take a full week for a $100 transfer via SWIFT/correspondent banking**, and it is actively hunting for a faster rail (China–Kazakhstan stablecoin bridge, stalled on China regulatory issues) [shared/partners/2026-05-21-freedom-bank-aidos-zhumagulov.md]. Freedom is a NASDAQ-listed bank with a 5M-user Super App and is a top-15 pipeline deal [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md].

The same pipeline is stacked with non-US banks needing exactly this: Halyk (Proposal, $50M TVL), International Bank of Tajikistan (Proposal), Freedom Holdings (Discovery) [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md]. Etherfuse already positions itself to give institutions 'seamless interoperability between fiat, traditional finance, and on-chain banking' [shared/meetings/2026-05-21-freedom-bank-followup.md] and supplies the local-currency Stablebond leg; Anchorage's OCC-chartered rails supply the USD-settlement leg those same banks can't get without a US correspondent.

**The play:** pitch Anchorage not as an abstract US custodian but as the USD-correspondent partner Etherfuse bundles into its Central Asia bank deals — Etherfuse brings the KZT/regional Stablebond + distribution, Anchorage brings compliant instant USD settlement. Anchorage's reported active Mexico/LATAM expansion [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital-bank.md] means the same bundle generalizes to Etherfuse's LATAM institutional targets. This is impact (unlocks stuck bank deals) × speed (both products already shipped) with an easy, complementary-not-competitive framing.
