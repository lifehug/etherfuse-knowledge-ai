# Addi is the launch customer for the COP Stablebond already in build with BBVA

> Addi's wiki pitch is built entirely around its Mexico/MXN expansion float, but Addi is a Colombian BNPL with $300M raised whose home-market currency is exactly the COP Stablebond Etherfuse is actively building with BBVA for next quarter — making Addi named demand for a product the addi page doesn't even mention.

The Addi page treats it as a generic 'Mexico expansion = MXN float' wishlist lead, with an opening angle that turns idle MXN into '10%+ sovereign yield' plus a white-label savings product [wiki/entities/companies/addi.md]. That framing misses the larger, faster opening: Addi is headquartered in Colombia and is a $300M-funded LATAM BNPL embedded in retail checkouts [wiki/entities/companies/addi.md], and its home currency is precisely where Etherfuse's next Stablebond is already landing.

Colombia is no longer a roadmap blind spot — it is in active build. The Tempo profile states plainly that 'Etherfuse is working on COP with BBVA, targeting next quarter,' anchored by a 'super large enterprise' that wants to pay contractors in a COP stablecoin and off-ramp via Bre-B to local bank accounts [shared/partners/tempo.md]. BBVA is the same custodian already running Etherfuse's CETES backing and FX operations [shared/partners/anchorage-digital-strategy.md], so the COP rail reuses an existing relationship rather than standing up new custody.

That converts Addi from a cold MXN-float pitch into a dual-currency, two-market customer: COP yield/white-label savings at home (where the product is shipping) and MXN float in its Mexico expansion (CETES, live today). The float-to-yield mechanics are already a proven, repeatable BNPL pattern — the same one Etherfuse routed Aplazo through and codified in the Bando rev-share structure (basis points on median monthly AUM in partner wallets, optional FX partner fee) [shared/partners/bando.md]. Airtm's partnership proposal already pencils a Colombia 'new issuance' Stablebond for Q3 2026 with P2P off-ramp coverage [shared/partners/airtm-partnership-proposal.md], corroborating standing COP demand beyond Addi alone.

Action: re-scope the Addi opening angle to lead with COP-in-home-market (launch-customer framing for a Q-next product) and treat MXN as the expansion add-on — and route the warm intro through the existing BBVA/Tempo COP workstream rather than starting Addi as an unconnected cold lead [shared/partners/tempo.md].
