# Agora's parked AUSD/KTB pair is the USD liquidity leg for a Korea launch that has 10x'd

> Etherfuse has Agora's AUSD/KTB pairing sitting at 'Nurture / Low priority / awaiting response' since January 2026, but the Korean opportunity it was meant to seed has since become an order of magnitude larger, and AUSD can anchor an FX marketplace across the entire Stablebond roster — not just KTB.

## The stall

The Agora relationship is logged as **Nurture, Low priority, blocked on 'Awaiting response on AUSD/KTB pair creation'** since the January 16, 2026 outreach, with open questions like 'Did the AUSD/KTB pairs get created for the Korean launch?' still unanswered [wiki/entities/companies/agora.md]. The original framing was narrow: create one AUSD/KTB pair on Monad 'to have liquidity ready for Korean users on day one,' with Shinhan committing to co-marketing [wiki/entities/companies/agora.md].

## Why that 'day one' is now far more valuable

When the Agora pair was scoped, KTB was a modest KRWQ launch. Since then the Korea picture has changed dramatically:

- Shinhan Asset Management's C-level meeting surfaced a **~$100B addressable Korean derivatives-collateral market** ($180B in stablecoins left Korea in 2025 to access offshore derivatives) plus a proposed **$2B/day Olive Young** payment-settlement rail — and Etherfuse can 'launch a tokenized MMF or fixed-rate asset tomorrow' [shared/partners/2026-06-04-shinhan-asset-management-clevel.md].
- Etherfuse's own June 5 cover letter explicitly flags the need for **on-chain USD↔KRW liquidity** ('Circle (USDC) and Tether Mint have direct banking infrastructure in Korea... creates a liquidity bridge') [shared/partners/2026-06-05-shinhan-cover-letter.md].

A day-one, on-chain USD/KTB pool is precisely the missing market-making leg for that bridge — and Agora's AUSD, a neutral USD stablecoin built for treasury/FX, is a ready-made counter-asset on Monad, a network both parties already support [shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md].

## The demand is independently corroborated

Agora is not the only partner asking for exactly this. Noble independently wants an **FX DEX for non-USD stablecoins with Korea/Japan as priority markets**, has $250M in stablecoins, and is blocked only on $1–2M of FX-pool liquidity [wiki/entities/companies/noble.md]. Two separate USD-stablecoin infrastructures (Agora AUSD, Noble) arriving at the same KTB-paired FX-marketplace need is a category signal, not a coincidence.

## The deal is scoped too small

Agora is being worked as a single KTB pair. But AUSD is a USD leg that pairs against **every** live and roadmap Stablebond — CETES, TESOURO, EUROB, GILTS, USTRY and the 50+ instruments queued through Q4 2026 [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. Agora's stated product is 'treasury management for corporate clients with enhanced FX services' and an 'on-chain FX marketplace' [wiki/entities/companies/agora.md] — i.e., a distribution surface for Stablebond FX across many currencies, not one. The deal framing (low priority, one pair) badly undersells this.

## Action

Re-prioritize Agora from Nurture to active, reframe the ask from 'AUSD/KTB pair' to 'AUSD as the USD market-making leg for the Korea launch and a multi-currency Stablebond FX marketplace on Monad,' and tie the timeline to Shinhan AM's regulatory window. **Lead to verify:** the founder is listed only as 'Nick van Eck' [wiki/entities/companies/agora.md]; if the VanEck asset-management connection is real, Agora is also a candidate fund-wrapper/distribution partner in the same vein as the 3iQ thesis and should be qualified accordingly.
