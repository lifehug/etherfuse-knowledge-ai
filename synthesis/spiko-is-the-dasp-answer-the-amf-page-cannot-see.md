# Spiko Already Answers Every Open Question on the AMF Page

> The AMF wiki page frames France market entry as an unsolved regulatory challenge with four open questions about finding a French legal entity or DASP-registered partner, but Etherfuse's own EUROB custodian Spiko is a French-regulated entity that almost certainly holds DASP registration — meaning the regulatory bridge already exists inside an active commercial relationship, though the competitor-custodian duality needs explicit management.

## The Gap

The AMF page lists four open questions [wiki/entities/companies/autorite-des-marches-financiers.md]:
1. Has Etherfuse identified a French legal entity or local partner to support a DASP application?
2. What is the current timeline for MiCA passporting to become fully operational in France?
3. Is engagement with the AMF's FinTech, Innovation and Competitiveness division already on the roadmap?
4. Are there existing DASP-registered firms in France that Etherfuse could partner with as an alternative to direct registration?

Questions 1 and 4 have the same answer: **Spiko**.

## The Connection

Spiko is Etherfuse's custodian for EUROB — the live EUR Stablebond backed by sovereign debt from eight eurozone countries including France [shared/master-knowledge-base.md] [shared/overview.md]. Spiko is a French entity operating as a digital-asset custodian in France. Under France's Loi PACTE framework, any firm providing digital-asset custody services in France requires AMF DASP registration [wiki/markets/France.md]. Spiko therefore almost certainly already holds the DASP status Etherfuse is framing as a future milestone.

This means Etherfuse does not need to pursue a direct DASP application or identify a new French partner. It already has one — and that partner already knows Etherfuse's product, compliance architecture, and custody mechanics intimately because it custodies EUROB.

## The Risk

Spiko appears on Etherfuse's own competitor list alongside Securitize, Ondo, and Backed [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md]. This competitor-custodian duality creates a specific strategic exposure: Spiko has full visibility into EUROB's structure, flows, and economics, and could replicate the model. Any deepening of the Spiko relationship for DASP passporting needs to be structured to bind Spiko as a partner (revenue share, exclusivity, joint MiCA submission) rather than leaving it as a pure custodian that learns the playbook and then competes.

## Recommendation

Reclassify the AMF engagement from 'research-stage regulatory challenge' to 'deepen existing Spiko relationship' — the regulatory path is already built, and the marginal cost of activating it through Spiko's existing DASP status is dramatically lower than a greenfield AMF application. But do so with explicit competitive protections in the agreement.
