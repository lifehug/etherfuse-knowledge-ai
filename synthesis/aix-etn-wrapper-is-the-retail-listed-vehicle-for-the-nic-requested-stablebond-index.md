# AIX's ETN wrapper is the retail-listed home for the Stablebond index NIC asked for

> The Stablebond index NIC NBK requested has only been costed as an institutional SPV, but AIX already runs a retail ETN wrapper reachable at $1 from age 16 via TabaDo — the cleaner, faster distribution vehicle for the same product.

In the NIC NBK meeting, NIC explicitly asked about on-chain index funds, and Dave proposed an index of Etherfuse Stablebonds at ~6% blended yield, diversified across strong and emerging-market currencies [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Every wrapper conversation in this graph so far (3iQ, Allo, Aaro) frames the index as an institutional SPV or fund-of-funds — a demand answer for allocators, not a retail product.

AIX is sitting on a different, complementary structure that no one has connected to this demand: it already operates an **ETN wrapper** (live today on Bitcoin, equities, and debt) with **retail access from age 16 and from as little as $1 via TabaDo** [wiki/entities/companies/aix.md]. An AIX-listed ETN over the Stablebond index is a distinct instrument from the SPV wrappers — you list once and instantly reach TabaDo's retail base, on a Nasdaq-engine venue running 2x KASE's volume [wiki/entities/companies/aix.md]. Because an AFSA/AIFC-linked listing carries passporting into the GCC once the Oman agreement lands [wiki/entities/companies/AIFC.md], the same ETN can travel to Gulf retail and family-office channels with no re-listing work.

The leverage: this turns the index from an institution-only structure into an exchange-traded retail savings product in a market Etherfuse is already setting up in — and AIX's existing Bitcoin ETN proves they can wrap a crypto-adjacent asset. Yet there is **zero commercial engagement with AIX** and no Linear lead [wiki/entities/companies/aix.md], so this is an untapped near-term channel, not a build.

Concrete first move: propose a single Stablebond-index ETN listing to Birzhan Astayev, scoped against the same index Dave already sketched for NIC — closing NIC's institutional demand and opening TabaDo retail distribution with one product.
