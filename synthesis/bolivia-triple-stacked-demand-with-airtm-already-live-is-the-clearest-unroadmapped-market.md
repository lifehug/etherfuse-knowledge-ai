# Bolivia has triple-stacked inbound demand and an existing AirTM rail — but no Stablebond

> Three independent partners are simultaneously asking Etherfuse for Bolivia access, AirTM already powers live payments there via Muru (whom Etherfuse has a direct contact at), yet Bolivia appears nowhere on the Stablebond roadmap — making it a higher-conviction market entry than most roadmap items.

The May 29 AirTM sync flags Bolivia as "a priority market with three simultaneous inbound requests (LI.FI, AirTM, and a third party)" and notes "AirTM is currently powering Muru's payments in Bolivia" [wiki/entities/companies/airtm.md]. The same source records the internal decision to "double efforts to unlock Bolivia" precisely because three inbound requests make it "a clear priority," and confirms Etherfuse already has a contact at Muru [shared/partners/2026-05-29-airtm-etherfuse-sync.md].

This is a sharper signal than the Argentina roadmap gap already flagged: unlike Argentina, Bolivia has (a) a named live distribution rail (AirTM→Muru) already moving payments, (b) three unrelated demand sources converging on the same quarter, and (c) an existing Etherfuse relationship (Muru) to shortcut onboarding. Yet the official 2026 Stablebond expansion pipeline lists PHP, MYR, GHS, IDR, VND, KES, HKD, and AED/SAR — with no BOB or Bolivia entry anywhere [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

Mechanically, Bolivia fits the exact model AirTM described for the JV — local stablecoin issuance targeting "50%+ margin improvement" with Argentina and Mexico as first targets [wiki/entities/companies/airtm.md] — and the Bermuda Section 8 exemption means adding a new-currency Stablebond requires only a notification to the BMA, not a re-application [shared/partners/2026-05-13-airtm-jv-proposal.md]. That removes the usual multi-month licensing gate that makes new-country launches slow.

Action: scope a Bolivia (BOB) short-term sovereign Stablebond into the JV pipeline and use the existing Muru contact + AirTM's live rail as the launch channel, rather than treating Bolivia as an ad-hoc inbound. The triple demand + live rail combination is a rare impact×speed×ease alignment.
