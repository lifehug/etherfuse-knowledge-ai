# Etherfuse can sell the corridor-treasury product in frontier markets years before a local Stablebond exists

> Three independent B2B case studies reveal Etherfuse already operates regulated FX/P2P partner desks across Nigeria, Angola, Egypt, Jordan, and four SE-Asian currencies — none of which have a live Stablebond — meaning the 'convert-at-receipt + USTRY float yield + corridor consolidation' product is a faster, already-packaged revenue line that doesn't wait on the bond roadmap.

Three customer case studies run the identical play, and together they expose an under-leveraged asset: a frontier FX-desk network.

- The Dubai FMCG case repatriates $5M/week from Nigeria (NGN) and Angola (AoA) via 'Etherfuse's regulated Nigerian P2P desk' and 'Etherfuse's Southern Africa partner network,' converging on USDC→Circle redemption [shared/bd/case-study-fmcg-dubai-africa.md].
- The Rotterdam freight case converts EGP at receipt via 'Etherfuse's Egyptian regulated partner' and routes Jordan flows through 'Etherfuse's Levant partner desk' [shared/bd/case-study-freight-eu-egypt-mena.md].
- The Hong Kong PSP case parks 48–72h settlement float in USTRY (~4.5% APY) and converts PHP/IDR/THB/VND through 'Etherfuse's Southeast Asia partner network' [shared/bd/case-study-psp-hongkong.md].

Cross-referenced against the bond roadmap, none of Nigeria, Angola, Egypt, Jordan, Philippines, Indonesia, Thailand or Vietnam has a live Stablebond — they are 2026 Q3/Q4 entries or absent entirely [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. The corroborating signal that the FX capability is real-and-current: the bmoni meeting has Etherfuse taking a warm intro to CNGN (Nigerian stablecoin) for a 'parallel integration' [shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bmoni-etherfuse-kyb-business-onboarding.md].

The non-obvious conclusion: the corridor product needs only (a) local FX partner desks Etherfuse already has, and (b) USTRY, which is already live [shared/overview.md] — it requires no new sovereign-bond issuance. That decouples a whole revenue line from the multi-quarter bond roadmap and lets Etherfuse monetize the highest-remittance corridors (Tajikistan 45% of GDP, Uzbekistan 18%, India $130B) on the float-yield-plus-FX economics shown in these studies, today. The marketing collateral is already written; what's missing is treating 'corridor treasury' as a named, prioritized product sold ahead of, not after, local Stablebond launches.
