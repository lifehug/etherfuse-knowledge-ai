# Ant entered the pipeline via Fintech Mexico — work it on home turf, not just at a conference

> The Ant International lead was sourced from the FTMX (Fintech Mexico) attendee list, meaning Alipay+ is active in Etherfuse's single regulated home market, yet the deal is being worked entirely as a distant Money20/20 Asia prospect instead of via the CNBV-moated MXNe/CETES and Visa card-collateral playbooks Etherfuse can deploy in Mexico today.

**The overlooked origin.** Ant's lead was 'originally sourced from the FTMX (Fintech Mexico) attendee list, with cold outreach sent on February 23, 2026' [wiki/entities/companies/ant-international.md]. That places Alipay+/Ant in Etherfuse's home market — the one place Etherfuse holds an exclusive regulatory position (CNBV Resolution P090/2024, only Mexican company authorized to tokenize sovereign bonds) [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. But the entire deal is now framed around a Money20/20 Asia Tier B meeting, with the Mexico thread dropped [wiki/entities/companies/ant-international.md].

**Why the home-market angle is faster.** In Mexico, Etherfuse already ships the products an Alipay+ merchant/wallet network would consume: native MXN SPEI ramps and MXNe backed by CETES [shared/partners/2023-12-14-brale-mxne-proposal.md], and the Visa local-issuer card-collateral model where a partner's settlement float earns CETES yield instead of sitting at 0% — deployable in any market where Etherfuse has a tokenized local asset, live today only in Mexico [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md]. None of these require a new sovereign launch or an Asia regulatory path; they are live rails in the jurisdiction where Ant already has a presence.

**Why this beats waiting on a conference.** The follow-up framework's core lesson — persistence and a specific value hook beat a single stale touch [shared/bd/followup-framework.md] — argues for reviving the Mexico thread with a concrete CETES/MXNe distribution or card-float proposal rather than parking the relationship on a 'Meeting Requested' status with priority 'Not set' [wiki/entities/companies/ant-international.md].

**Caveat.** Sources confirm Ant attended a Mexican fintech event and is expanding globally, but do not document a specific Ant Mexico product footprint or MXN treasury need — hence low confidence. The action is to test it: qualify Ant's Mexico operations on the next call before defaulting to the Asia-only framing.
