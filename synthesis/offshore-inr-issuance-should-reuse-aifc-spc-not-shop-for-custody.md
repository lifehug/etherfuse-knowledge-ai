# Abound's offshore INR issuance need is already solved by the AIFC SPC / Bermuda vehicles in flight — stop custody-shopping

> Dave's action item to 'research custody for INR via Shinhan, BBVA, Halyk' is bespoke effort for a problem Etherfuse is already building reusable infrastructure for — the AIFC SPC and Bermuda DABA offshore-issuer vehicles — and the KTB precedent shows the offshore-issuance pattern already works for a home-restricted Asian currency.

On the April 27 call both sides agreed on an **offshore issuance structure to navigate US regulatory restrictions**, and Dave's follow-up was to 'research custody + regulatory options for INR issuance; follow up with Shinhan Securities, BBVA, Halyk' [shared/meetings/2026-04-27-abound-times-of-india-inr-stablecoin.md]. None of those three is an Indian custodian — they are the custodians Etherfuse already uses for Korea, Mexico, and Kazakhstan. That signals the real question isn't 'who holds Indian paper' but 'which offshore wrapper issues the token,' and Etherfuse is already standing up exactly such wrappers for other deals.

Two reusable vehicles are in motion. First, the **AIFC SPC (Etherfuse SPC Limited)** — bankruptcy-remote under English common law, with a fast-track AFSA license Talgat created specifically for tokenization projects, and an explicit GENIUS Act Sec. 18 foreign-issuer pathway [shared/partners/2026-05-25-aifc-talgat-amanbayev.md, shared/overview.md, shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Second, the **Bermuda BMA/DABA** route being built for the AirTM JV, where a new-currency Stablebond requires only a Section 8 notification — no re-application [shared/partners/2026-05-13-airtm-jv-proposal.md]. Either is a faster path to an INR product than negotiating a one-off Indian custody chain.

The KTB deal is the proof point that the offshore-issuance pattern works for a restricted Asian currency: Korea launched KTB **offshore** using Etherfuse's own initial liquidity, the same approach flagged for the Halyk/Kazakhstan launch [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. An offshore INR Stablebond can copy that structure rather than invent a new one. The implication: route the Abound product through whichever offshore SPC clears first (AIFC or Bermuda) and reuse the bankruptcy-remote / Swiss DLT framework already documented [shared/bd/bankruptcy-remoteness-communication-guide.md], instead of letting the deal stall on a custody search that the existing institutional partners (Shinhan, BBVA, Halyk) were never the right answer for.
