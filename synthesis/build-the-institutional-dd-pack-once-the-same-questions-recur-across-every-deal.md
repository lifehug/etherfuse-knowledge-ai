# Build the institutional DD pack once — the same gaps recur across every deal

> Etherfuse keeps creating compliance documents reactively, one partner at a time, even though the Tala due-diligence gap list is the same checklist every institutional partner asks for — so closing the few remaining critical gaps proactively would accelerate the entire institutional pipeline at near-zero marginal cost.

The Tala DD gap analysis is effectively a master list of what every partner questionnaire asks: ISO 27001 / SOC 2 status, RTO/RPO, a formal disaster-recovery plan, backup strategy, whistleblowing policy, anti-corruption policy, and a standalone InfoSec policy — and its own note says the document "should inform what policies/documents to create or formalize before the next partner DD questionnaire." [shared/partners/tala-dd-ekb-gaps.md]

The reactive pattern is visible: Etherfuse "created a new anti-bribery and corruption policy specifically to support" the Kazakhstan/Monad onboarding — a policy that was missing gifts-and-hospitality provisions and only got built when a *specific* partner DD surfaced the gap. [wiki/concepts/anti-bribery-corruption-policy.md] That same anti-corruption policy is gap #6 on the Tala list, meaning two separate deals independently demanded the same artifact.

With ~9–10 organizations going through KYB onboarding at any given time [wiki/entities/companies/Etherfuse.md] and a structured KYB lifecycle already operationalized [wiki/concepts/kyb-onboarding-lifecycle.md], the bottleneck on institutional conversion is increasingly the repeatable DD packet, not the product. **Decision-useful action:** close the named-critical Tala gaps (ISO 27001/SOC 2 roadmap, formal DR plan, whistleblowing + InfoSec policies) once as a shared institutional DD pack, rather than re-discovering each gap per deal. This compounds across Anchorage, Shinhan, and every future bank-grade counterparty, all of which run the same questionnaire.
