# Al Rajhi is the ready-made Saudi anchor for the Q3-2026 SAUTB launch

> The Al Rajhi page treats Saudi Arabia as a cold research target with no contact, but four other sources in this slice converge to show the launch is already scheduled, demand is already live, the regulatory path already exists, and Al Rajhi's primary-dealer status is the one missing paper-sourcing leg — plus a proven tokenize-your-own-issuances upsell.

The Al Rajhi wiki page frames Saudi Arabia as pure research-stage: no contacts, no outreach, and open questions like 'what is the best entry point' [wiki/entities/companies/al-rajhi-bank.md]. But cross-reading the neighborhood shows Saudi entry is far more actionable than any single page states — and Al Rajhi is the single node that unlocks it.

**The launch is already scheduled.** The authoritative bond roadmap lists a Saudi Arabia Treasury Bill (ticker SAUTB, A+ rated) as a Q3 2026 launch — the same near-term window as the other MENA/APAC issuances Etherfuse is committed to shipping [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. So this is not a hypothetical market; it is a product on the build calendar that currently has no named local partner.

**Demand is already live, pre-token.** Etherfuse already processes active SAR corridor flows for a Rotterdam freight client, compressing AED/SAR corridor cost from 80–120 bps to 8–12 bps [shared/bd/case-study-freight-eu-egypt-mena.md]. The Saudi market page itself flags this as proof 'SAR corridor demand exists and Etherfuse's infrastructure is already handling Saudi Arabia-linked flows' [wiki/markets/saudi-arabia.md].

**The regulatory home already exists.** The Saudi market page notes the blocking gap is that 'no documentation exists on CMA/SAMA requirements' — but it also documents the AIFC→GCC passporting path, where an AFSA license carries recognized relationships across DFSA Dubai, ADGM, and the UAE regulators, with an imminent Oman agreement extending to the entire GCC [wiki/markets/saudi-arabia.md]. Talgat Amanbayev (AIFC CIO) confirmed the Oman passporting agreement lands 'later in 2026,' giving all AIFC companies GCC market access with no additional regulatory work [shared/partners/2026-05-25-aifc-talgat-amanbayev.md]. Etherfuse is already standing up exactly this AIFC vehicle for Kazakhstan.

**Al Rajhi supplies the one missing leg — and a proven upsell.** A SAUTB needs a Saudi government-securities paper source; Al Rajhi is a primary dealer in Saudi sovereign auctions and is already exploring blockchain partnerships [wiki/entities/companies/al-rajhi-bank.md]. This maps onto the repeatable local-partner playbook Etherfuse has run elsewhere: Halyk Finance (50% of Kazakhstan bond placements) and the BBVA/Shinhan precedent both started with one product and then asked Etherfuse to tokenize all their own issuances [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-meeting.md]. As a $180B primary dealer, Al Rajhi is positioned to become both the SAUTB paper source and a 'tokenize your own placements' customer.

**Action:** Al Rajhi should be moved off 'research/awareness' and worked as the named Saudi anchor for SAUTB — sourcing paper, distributing to Saudi retail/institutional flows, and eventually tokenizing its own auction inventory — with the AIFC SPC as the regulatory wrapper and the live freight SAR corridor as the demand proof point. Note one item to resolve on framing: the roadmap lists SAUTB as USD-denominated while the market page describes a SAR-denominated instrument [shared/bond-roadmap.md] [wiki/markets/saudi-arabia.md]; the SAR/USD peg makes either viable but the product needs a decision.
