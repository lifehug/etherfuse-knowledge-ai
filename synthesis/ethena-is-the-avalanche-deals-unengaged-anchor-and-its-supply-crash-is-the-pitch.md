# Ethena Is the Avalanche Deal's Unengaged Anchor — and Its Supply Crash Is the Pitch

> Etherfuse's native Avalanche deployment is architecturally dependent on Ethena as its named first use case, yet Ethena has zero direct contacts, interactions, or pipeline entry — while Ethena's 57% supply crash signals exactly the funding-rate vulnerability that makes sovereign-yield Stablebonds the natural reserve-backing play, at a scale (~$6.3B) that dwarfs the ~$1M Foundation threshold the deal is currently sized around.

## The Dependency

On June 23, 2026, Ava Labs convened a joint call with Ethena and Etherfuse specifically to "anchor the first concrete use case for deploying Etherfuse's protocol on Avalanche" [wiki/entities/companies/ava-labs.md]. Ava Labs rejected a LayerZero bridge approach, citing "the intended scale of the Ethena integration" as the reason native deployment is required [wiki/entities/companies/ava-labs.md]. The entire engineering commitment hinges on a ~$1M threshold from the Avalanche Foundation [wiki/entities/companies/ava-labs.md], but the commercial rationale for native deployment rests on Ethena.

## The Gap

Despite being the named anchor customer, Ethena's wiki page shows: **zero contacts on file, zero interactions recorded, no active deal, no pipeline stage** [wiki/entities/companies/Ethena.md]. Etherfuse is negotiating its most significant L1 expansion deal around an anchor counterparty with whom it has no direct commercial relationship. The only engagement runs through Ava Labs BD (Leandro Davo), making Ava Labs a single point of failure for the Ethena relationship.

## The Opportunity Hidden in the Supply Crash

Ethena's USDe supply declined from $14.8B (October 2025) to $6.3B (February 2026) — a 57% contraction [wiki/entities/companies/Ethena.md]. Average sUSDe APY was ~19% in 2024 [wiki/entities/companies/Ethena.md], but the supply crash signals the funding-rate compression that makes cash-and-carry yield structurally volatile. This is the identical weakness the existing Aegis finding identified for synthetic-dollar issuers, but Ethena is not a cold target — it is already in an active conversation.

Ethena's established response to this problem is diversifying reserves into BlackRock's BUIDL T-bill fund [source needed]. Stablebonds offer the same sovereign grade with a crucial advantage: non-USD diversification at materially higher yields. CETES at ~5.78% and TESOURO at ~13.18% [shared/master-knowledge-base.md] deliver 2–4x the return of USD T-bill products, and the sales team already frames this as "starting from a 7% floor instead of 0% changes the entire DeFi math" [shared/bd/why-stablebonds-vs-direct-bonds.md].

## The Scale Mismatch

The Ava Labs deal is sized around a ~$1M Avalanche Foundation treasury commitment [wiki/entities/companies/ava-labs.md]. At Etherfuse's 0.7% annual fee on TVL [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md], $1M TVL generates ~$7K/year in revenue. Ethena's $6.3B supply means even a 1% reserve allocation to Stablebonds ($63M) would be **63x the Foundation threshold** and would, at 0.7%, generate ~$441K/year — immediately ranking in the top 15 pipeline. Yet Ava Labs does not appear anywhere in the pipeline valuation snapshot [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md], and neither does Ethena.

## Regulatory Tailwind

The CLARITY/GENIUS Act discussion signals that US stablecoin yield may be restricted to transaction-based rewards only, not balance-based [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md]. Ethena's sUSDe is balance-based yield. If this regulation passes, Ethena's core product is directly impacted, but Stablebonds are unaffected: "yield comes from the sovereign instrument, not the protocol" [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md]. This makes the reserve-backing pitch a regulatory hedge as well as a yield-diversification play.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Open a direct Ethena deal immediately.** Contacts from the June 23 call (Matt Schmenk, Leandro Davo, Olivia Vandewoude from Ava Labs) can facilitate. Get Ethena BD contacts into the CRM.
2. **Reframe the Ava Labs negotiation.** The Foundation treasury allocation is door-opener economics; the Ethena reserve-backing opportunity is the revenue target. Size the deal accordingly.
3. **Pitch Ethena directly on multi-currency reserve diversification.** USTRY (~3.2%) for USD parity, CETES (~5.78%) and TESOURO (~13.18%) for yield premium, all with sovereign backing and DeFi composability on the same chain Ethena already operates.
4. **Assess the "intended scale" claim.** Ethena's 57% supply decline may have changed the scale assumptions Ava Labs used to justify native deployment over bridging. Confirm with Leandro Davo whether the Ethena integration is still sized to justify the engineering commitment.
