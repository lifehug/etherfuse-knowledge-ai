# AFSA 'Oracle Provision' activity + live RedStone feeds turns Etherfuse's price infrastructure into a regulated revenue line

> Etherfuse is licensing 'Oracle Provision for all assets and markets' as one of its six AFSA activities while already running live RedStone oracle feeds for its tokens on Stellar — combining these makes Etherfuse a regulated oracle provider, a monetizable service distinct from bond issuance.

One of the six activities in the Etherfuse SPC license application is **Oracle Provision for all assets and markets** [wiki/entities/companies/aifc.md — FIN-413]. Separately, RedStone Oracles went live on Stellar mainnet providing price-feed data for Etherfuse tokens including CETES and USTRY [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

These two facts together point to an under-noticed revenue line: a regulated, AFSA-licensed oracle service for tokenized sovereign debt and FX rates across the Stablebond roster. KASE's digital-asset platform explicitly plans coupon/dividend payments in stablecoins and needs valuation feeds [shared/partners/2026-05-22-kase-digital-assets.md], and the NIC NBK model priced Etherfuse's edge partly on delivering live local sovereign rates rather than ETF-packaged yield [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md] — i.e. authoritative pricing is itself a product.

**Why it matters:** most of the Kazakhstan file treats the six activities as boilerplate license scope. Oracle Provision is the one that lets Etherfuse charge for something it already produces at near-zero marginal cost (NAV/price feeds it must compute anyway for attestations and market-making). Low-cost to pursue, but confidence is medium because there is no in-neighborhood evidence of a named oracle-services buyer yet.
