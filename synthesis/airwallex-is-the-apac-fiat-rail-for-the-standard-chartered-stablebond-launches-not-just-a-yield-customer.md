# Airwallex is the missing APAC fiat rail for the Standard Chartered Stablebond roadmap

> Airwallex holds local payment/collection rails in exactly the SE-Asian currencies Etherfuse is about to launch via Standard Chartered, so it can supply the 'Southeast Asia partner network' the PSP float case study already assumes — making Airwallex a supply-side rails partner, not merely a yield-distribution customer.

The existing Airwallex finding frames the relationship as distribution for a proven APAC float-yield product. This misses a second, complementary role that spans three sources.

**The gap in Etherfuse's own SE-Asia plan.** The Standard Chartered flow-of-funds names local-currency Stablebond launches in Philippines (PHP, Q2), Malaysia (MYR, Q3), Indonesia (IDR, Q4), Vietnam (VND, Q4), plus Hong Kong (HKD) — with custody solved by SC but *local fiat access* left to per-market execution [shared/partners/standard-chartered-flow-of-funds.md]. The PSP Hong Kong case study reveals Etherfuse already depends on an unnamed 'Southeast Asia partner network' to convert PHP/IDR/THB/VND receivables to USDC at institutional mid rates [shared/bd/case-study-psp-hongkong.md]. That partner network is a dependency, not a named asset.

**Airwallex is the archetype of that network.** Airwallex is 'a leading global payments and financial platform… business account, payments, treasury' operating across the same APAC corridors, with a Money 2020 Asia contact already warm (Brian Joseph, Director of Financial Partnerships) [wiki/entities/companies/airwallex.md]. The exact currencies its multi-currency settlement business runs on — PHP, IDR, THB, VND — are the ones both the SC roadmap and the PSP case study need rails for.

**Why this reframes the deal.** Airwallex's stated priority is 'Building Airwallex Yield ($1B+ AuA)' [wiki/entities/companies/airwallex.md]. Etherfuse's current hook sells CETES/TESOURO as underlying assets for that yield product. But Airwallex simultaneously has what Etherfuse *lacks* in SE Asia: licensed local collection/payout rails. The higher-leverage structure is a two-way deal — Etherfuse supplies EM sovereign yield into Airwallex Yield, and Airwallex supplies the fiat on/off-ramp leg for Etherfuse's PHP/IDR/VND/MYR Stablebond launches, exactly as 1Money was identified as the operational USD/ACH leg for the US market [shared/partners/standard-chartered-flow-of-funds.md]. That doubles the deal's value and gives Etherfuse a reason to prioritize a lead currently sitting at 'priority not set,' because the SC launches are date-committed for Q2–Q4 2026 and need rails now.

**Action:** convert the Money2020 Asia meeting from a one-way asset pitch into a rails+yield barter scoped to the PHP launch, the nearest SC milestone.
