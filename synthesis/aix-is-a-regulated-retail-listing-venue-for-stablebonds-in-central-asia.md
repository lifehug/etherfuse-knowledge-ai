# AIX is a regulated, $1-minimum retail listing venue Etherfuse hasn't worked

> AIX runs a Nasdaq engine with an existing ETN wrapper, retail access from age 16 and $1 via TabaDo, and explicit appetite for innovative fixed-income — yet there is zero commercial engagement, leaving a ready exchange-listing channel for Stablebonds untouched in a market Etherfuse is already setting up in.

AIX sat in the same May 25 room as AIFC but remains commercially idle: "No formal deal or engagement has been initiated as of 2026-06-10" [wiki/entities/companies/AIX.md]. That is a missed easy win, because AIX is unusually well-suited to Stablebonds:

- It already runs an **ETN wrapper on Bitcoin, equities, and debt** and a recent listing (Solid Gold) "yielded 60% in under a year" — proving appetite for innovative fixed-income/digital products [shared/partners/2026-05-25-aifc-talgat-amanbayev.md].
- **Retail access from age 16, from as little as $1, via the TabaDo digital platform** [wiki/entities/companies/AIX.md] — exactly the low-friction retail rail that attacks the same "retail can't access sovereign yield" problem Etherfuse leads with in Mexico's 2% retail-ownership pitch [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].
- It trades **2x KASE volume (6x excluding the state pension fund)** on Nasdaq-grade infrastructure with an AIFC-only data-hosting exemption [wiki/entities/companies/AIX.md].

The connection: Etherfuse is already incorporating an AIFC SPC and pursuing an AFSA license in this jurisdiction — listing Stablebonds (or a MEKKAM ETN) on AIX is an incremental, low-cost distribution add that reaches Central Asian retail through a regulated exchange Etherfuse's own counterparties already attend. The open questions AIX flagged (listing requirements, ETN appetite, whether TabaDo can distribute blockchain-native instruments) are answerable in a single follow-up with Birzhan Astayev — who was already in the room. This should be added to the Kazakhstan workstream list as a fifth track alongside sandbox/SPC/AIX-strategy/IPS, with an explicit commercial ask rather than passive 'identified venue' status.
