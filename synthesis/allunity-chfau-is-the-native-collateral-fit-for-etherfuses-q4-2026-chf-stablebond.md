# AllUnity's CHFAU maps onto Etherfuse's planned CHF Stablebond — on its own Swiss home turf

> AllUnity's flagship product is CHFAU, an ESMA-registered Swiss Franc stablecoin, and Etherfuse is already building a CHF Stablebond backed by Swiss Confederation Bills for Q4 2026 on the very jurisdiction that is already its legal tokenization home — making the MXNe playbook (back the coin with the Stablebond as yield-bearing collateral) an immediate CHF-native fit that none of the existing AllUnity findings capture.

AllUnity is tracked here purely as a CHF stablecoin issuer: "AllUnity is launching CHFAU, an ESMA-registered Swiss Franc stablecoin on Ethereum" [wiki/entities/companies/allunity.md], and the master competitor table confirms "AllUnity (CHFAU) | CHF | ESMA-registered Swiss Franc stablecoin on Ethereum" [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

The non-obvious connection is that Etherfuse's own roadmap already schedules the exact underlying asset AllUnity needs: "Switzerland — planned CHF Stablebond backed by Swiss Confederation Bills targeted for Q4 2026; Switzerland currently serves as the legal tokenization layer for all Stablebonds under Art. 973d CO" [wiki/markets/europe.md]. In other words, Switzerland is not a new market for Etherfuse to enter — it is already the legal spine under which every live Stablebond (CETES, EUROB, GILTS, etc.) is registered as a ledger-based security [shared/bd/website-asset-protection-rewrite.md]. A CHF Stablebond is therefore the lowest-friction sovereign product Etherfuse can ship, and it lands squarely on AllUnity's home currency.

The play is the documented MXNe model, not a new build: Etherfuse co-issues MXNe (a CETES-backed peso stablecoin via Brale) on the same collateral that backs its CETES Stablebond [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. Applied to AllUnity, the CHF Stablebond becomes the yield-bearing reserve leg behind CHFAU — turning a zero-yield pure-peg CHF coin into a Swiss-sovereign-yield instrument while AllUnity keeps its ESMA registration and distribution. This is the same collateral-layer pattern already generalized in the Visa local-issuer card playbook, which explicitly names replicability "in any market where Etherfuse has a tokenized local treasury asset" [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md].

Data note for whoever picks this up: the three existing AllUnity findings treat it as a EUR issuer (EURAU) — the EUR-leg-of-the-FX-marketplace and reuse-MXNe theses — whereas this neighborhood's sources describe it as a CHF issuer (CHFAU) [wiki/entities/companies/allunity.md] [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. Either AllUnity spans both currencies or one label is stale; if it spans both, AllUnity is a collateral partner for Etherfuse's live EUROB *and* its in-flight CHF Stablebond, doubling the surface area. The CHF angle is the one no existing finding covers, and it is the faster of the two because it sits on Etherfuse's existing Swiss legal infrastructure.
