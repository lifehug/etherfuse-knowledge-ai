# Etherfuse holds ~$3M of foundation grant allocation it can deploy to close DeFi integrations

> Dave has standing authority to distribute roughly $1M each from Stellar, Solana, and Monad as liquidity-seeding grants to partners — a repeatable, near-zero-cost conversion accelerator that is currently being offered ad hoc rather than run as a system.

Across four separate DeFi deals the same lever recurs: Etherfuse acts as an **omnibus grant distributor** for chain foundations, seeding liquidity pools for partners who integrate Stablebonds. On the SBC call Dave states it plainly — he can source grants 'from Stellar, Solana and Monad,' $50–120K per partner (Stellar up to $120K), and 'in each we've been allocated like a million to give out' [teams/sales/2026-04-13-stablecoin-xyz-grant-liquidity.md]. That implies **~$3M of standing allocation** spread across three foundations.

The same instrument shows up everywhere in this neighborhood: the Solana Foundation offers 'up to $70K per partner' and 0.xyz's deal explicitly routes Jeremie to Antonio Neto for a $70K integration grant [wiki/entities/companies/Solana.md; teams/sales/2026-04-21-0xyz-p0-won.md]; Assetto's Abtin is offered a '$50–100K' Solana omnibus grant to seed liquidity pools [teams/sales/2026-04-16-assetto-precept-labs.md]; and a prior Monad deal was cited at ~$1M liquidity support [teams/sales/2026-04-16-assetto-precept-labs.md]. The grant is structured so the *partner* applies and the money funds liquidity/market-making for Etherfuse assets — directly deepening the order books that every collateral and FX-DEX integration depends on.

Two decision-useful implications. First, this is an under-systematized growth engine: the same $70–120K subsidy that's being offered case-by-case to SBC, Assetto and 0.xyz should be packaged as a standard close-accelerator on every DeFi integration pitch, because it removes the partner's single biggest objection (cold-start liquidity) at no cost to Etherfuse. Second, there is an embedded reputational risk to manage: Dave coaches partners to word applications so they don't 'say we're giving this money to Etherfuse' to avoid the appearance of self-dealing [teams/sales/2026-04-13-stablecoin-xyz-grant-liquidity.md] — meaning the program's scale should be governed deliberately rather than improvised per deal.
