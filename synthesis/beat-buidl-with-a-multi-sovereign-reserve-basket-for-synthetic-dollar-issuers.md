# Out-yield BUIDL: sell synthetic-dollar issuers a diversified sovereign reserve basket, not just USTRY

> The existing Aegis pitch (sell USTRY as a stable reserve leg) leaves Etherfuse competing head-on with BlackRock's BUIDL on the same ~3% US-Treasury yield — but Etherfuse's real edge is a multi-sovereign Stablebond basket (~6% blended) that out-yields the USD-only incumbents synthetic-dollar issuers currently default to.

**The gap in the current thesis.** The Aegis finding correctly identifies that delta-neutral / synthetic-dollar issuers need a stable reserve leg when funding-rate yield collapses — Ethena solved this by parking reserves in BlackRock's BUIDL [shared/bd/collateral-playbook-ktb-krwq.md], and USDe's supply cratered from a $14.8B peak to $6.3B precisely when the cash-and-carry trade compressed [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md]. Aegis (~$40M TVL, delta-neutral, full DeFi composability) sits at Wishlist with only 'initial outreach' as its next step [wiki/entities/companies/aegis.md]. But pitching 'USTRY as your reserve leg' puts Etherfuse in a straight fight with BUIDL and OpenEden — both USD-only US-Treasury products [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md] — on a yield they can't differentiate on (USTRY ~3.2%, same underlying as BUIDL) [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

**The non-obvious edge: reserve yield is the pain point, and only Etherfuse can raise it.** A synthetic-dollar issuer whose funding yield is compressing needs its reserve leg to *make up* return, not just preserve capital. The incumbent option caps at the US Treasury rate (~3.08% 7D APY on tokenized treasuries; BUIDL ~$2.9B AUM, 40% of the market) [shared/ecosystems/sovereign-debt.md]. Etherfuse is the only issuer that can hand them a diversified sovereign basket — the exact ~6% blended-yield index concept Dave already sketched for NIC NBK, 'diversified across strong and emerging market currencies' [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md] — combining CETES (~5.7%), TESOURO (~13%), and USTRY (~3.2%) into a reserve leg that out-yields BUIDL by ~300bps while keeping sovereign-grade backing and on-chain proof of reserves [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

**Why this wins.** 95%+ of the tokenized-treasury market is USD-only [shared/ecosystems/sovereign-debt.md], so no competitor can match a multi-currency reserve basket. The KRWQ collateral playbook already codifies the 'don't explain intermediaries, explain the collateral' messaging that lets an issuer market a sovereign-backed reserve to its own users without exposing the structure [shared/bd/collateral-playbook-ktb-krwq.md] — the same wrapper works for a synthetic-dollar issuer's reserve disclosures.

**Action.** Reframe the Aegis outreach (and the broader delta-neutral cohort) from 'match your BUIDL leg' to 'beat it': lead with a configurable multi-sovereign reserve basket, cite the ~6% blended index, and position Etherfuse as the only reserve provider that raises yield instead of just parking cash. This is an out-compete play against BUIDL/OpenEden with near-zero incremental product work since all component Stablebonds already ship.
