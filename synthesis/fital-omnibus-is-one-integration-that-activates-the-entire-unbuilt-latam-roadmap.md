# Fital: one omnibus integration activates ~25 LatAm countries at once

> Jorge Puflea's Fital operates crypto ramps in ~25 countries and wants a single omnibus integration of Etherfuse yield stablecoins — making it the fastest way to monetize the Colombia/Argentina/Bolivia demand that has no live Stablebond yet.

Jorge Puflea (puflea@fital.xyz) runs a custodial/non-custodial ramp platform across **~25 countries — Mexico, Brazil, Argentina, Colombia, Chile, Peru, Bolivia, Guatemala, Panama, Honduras, US, Europe** — and on the June 18 2026 call indicated willingness to proceed with integration and marketplace listing [teams/ceo/2026-06-18-jorge-puflea-partnership-terms.md]. The model he proposed is an **omnibus account**: a single Etherfuse integration (20bps base fee, scaling to 5bps at $100M+/mo) under which Jorge handles per-customer KYC and sets the yield split, converting customer cash straight into CETES/EUROC/BRL/KRW/USDC the moment it lands [teams/ceo/2026-06-18-jorge-puflea-partnership-terms.md].

The non-obvious payoff: Fital is already live in exactly the markets where Etherfuse's bond roadmap has *gaps*. Separate findings flag stacked, unworked partner demand in Colombia, Argentina, and Bolivia with no COP/ARS/BOB Stablebond shipped — Fital independently penciled **Colombia and Argentina launches for end of year** [teams/ceo/2026-06-18-jorge-puflea-partnership-terms.md], the same timeline partners elsewhere are using. Crucially, Fital doesn't need a local bond to add value: it 'avoids FX risk by issuing yields in the customer's preferred currency,' so it can deploy idle local cash into the *existing* six Stablebonds today and grow into new-currency bonds as they ship.

This reframes a single 'complementary yield' lead into a distribution multiplier across the entire LatAm wishlist. Jorge already carries an MXNB integration as first mover and treats Etherfuse as the complementary yield layer [teams/ceo/2026-06-18-jorge-puflea-partnership-terms.md] — so the competitive displacement is yield, the one thing MXNB structurally cannot offer. Andy was assigned to send integration docs + API link post-call; that is the whole gating action. Impact (25 countries), speed (partner ready to proceed), and ease (omnibus = one integration, partner owns KYC) all rank this near the top of the queue.
