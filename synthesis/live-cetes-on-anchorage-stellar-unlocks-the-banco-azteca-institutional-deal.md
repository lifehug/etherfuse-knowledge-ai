# The June-15 CETES-on-Anchorage go-live is the missing rail for the Banco Azteca / Grupo Salinas deal

> Etherfuse is structuring an institutional CETES distribution deal into Banco Azteca that depends on Anchorage custody on Stellar — and that exact rail went live on June 15, meaning the deal's key technical precondition is already satisfied and the two efforts should be run as one.

Two threads in this neighborhood are the same play seen from different sides, and no page connects them.

Thread one: Anchorage's CETES custody on Stellar went live and was jointly announced June 15, 2026 [shared/partners/anchorage.md], validated by third parties as 'institutions can hold tokenized exposure to Mexican sovereign debt on a federally regulated platform' [teams/cmo/docs/research/linkedin-scout/2026-06-22-linkedin-scout.md].

Thread two: as of June 22, Etherfuse is structuring an institutional Cetes/Seites distribution product into **Banco Azteca (Grupo Salinas)** using 'Anchorage Digital as custodian and Stellar as the settlement network' [wiki/entities/companies/banco-azteca.md]. Anchorage already holds the USD stablecoin-rails position inside Grupo Salinas via the Coinpro integration announced May 15, 2026 [shared/research/raw/2026-06-19-grupo-salinas-anchorage-stablecoin.md], and Grupo Salinas is an active Etherfuse negotiation counterparty (SALES-148) [wiki/entities/companies/grupo-salinas.md].

The non-obvious link: the Banco Azteca deal's stated architecture (CETES, custodied at Anchorage, settled on Stellar to institutional clients) is *precisely* the integration that just shipped seven days earlier. The custody/technical risk that would normally gate such a deal is gone. Combined with the fact that Anchorage is already embedded in Grupo Salinas on the USD side, Etherfuse can position CETES as the non-USD sovereign-yield layer riding on rails the counterparty already trusts — a low-friction expansion, not a cold build. This turns a large underbanked-consumer conglomerate into a live distribution node, and argues for jointly owning the Anchorage and Banco Azteca tracks rather than running them as separate leads.
