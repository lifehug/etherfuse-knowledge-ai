# Abound's USDC off-ramp float is a USTRY yield customer Etherfuse can close before INDTB ships

> Abound's existing USDC↔INR off-ramp business — clearing ~20% of India's USDC volume — sits on idle USD/USDC settlement float that maps exactly onto the PSP float-yield product Etherfuse already ships, making Abound a revenue-generating USTRY customer today, independent of the Q3 2026 INDTB stablecoin launch the deal is currently waiting on.

The Abound deal is framed entirely around a future product: an INR stablecoin / INDTB launch targeted for 2–3 months out and contingent on regulatory readiness [shared/meetings/2026-04-27-abound-times-of-india-inr-stablecoin.md], with INDTB itself not live until Q3 2026 [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. That framing leaves money on the table now.

Abound is described as clearing **~20% of India's USDC off-ramp volume** with 500K+ users on UPI rails [shared/meetings/2026-04-27-abound-times-of-india-inr-stablecoin.md]. Any business running USDC→fiat off-ramp at that scale holds substantial USD/USDC settlement float between conversion and payout — precisely the idle-float position the Hong Kong PSP case study monetizes: settlement float is parked in **USTRY (~4.5% APY) during holding windows, redeemable same-day**, turning capital that earns 0% into sovereign yield [shared/bd/case-study-psp-hongkong.md]. The Visa collateral playbook makes the same point generically — settlement float held as token collateral earns the sovereign rate instead of sitting at 0% [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md].

The non-obvious move: this reframes Abound from a *distribution channel* for a not-yet-built INR product into a *treasury-yield customer of USTRY today*. It requires no new asset, no Indian custody, no offshore vehicle, and no regulatory readiness — only the float-yield integration Etherfuse already runs for an APAC PSP. It is the fastest, easiest revenue line in the relationship and de-risks the larger INR build by establishing live commercial value while the stablecoin clears regulatory questions. The pitch should lead with 'put your off-ramp float in USTRY,' not 'let's launch a stablecoin in 2–3 months.'
