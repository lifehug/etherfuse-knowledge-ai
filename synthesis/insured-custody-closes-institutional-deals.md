# Insured custody is the unshipped feature that closes sovereign deals

> Finishing the stalled Marsh crypto-crime policy and adding "insured" to the institutional platform pitch directly answers the residual-risk objections raised by Etherfuse's most valuable prospects — and the technical answers Marsh is waiting on already exist in our own security docs.

The Marsh MMA engagement stalled mid-conversation with the core underwriting question unresolved: does losing a token equal losing the underlying asset, and are the minting-contract controls strong enough [teams/cfo/legal/mexico/2026-04-14-etherfuse-mma-crypto-crime-policy.md]. **The answers Marsh needs are already documented elsewhere and were apparently not put in front of them.** The minting flow uses "multi-sig M-of-N hardware wallet approval" with no pre-minting, OtterSec audited the contracts (13 findings, all resolved), an in-house CISO runs continuous audits via Highland Security, and Swiss DLT Act Art. 242a–242b gives bankruptcy-remote token protection [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. The newer Principal-Token program even requires freeze authority at mint and an Etherfuse co-signature on every wrap/unwrap [wiki/entities/companies/Etherfuse.md]. These collectively shrink the residual attack surface to exactly what crypto-crime policies are designed to cover — sending them is a same-day action that could reopen and close the policy.

**Why this matters far beyond one insurance policy:** the institutional platform overview markets tokenization, compliance, reporting, fiat rails, and APIs — but lists *no insurance layer* [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md]. Meanwhile the highest-value prospects in this neighborhood are explicitly risk-gated. NIC NBK (National Bank of Kazakhstan's fund-of-funds) pushed back on Etherfuse's single-counterparty BBVA model as concentration risk versus their traditional 10–15 bank custody setup [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. GCC sovereign wealth funds (\$4T+) accessible via AIFC passporting are "constrained by minimum credit rating requirements" and need the B+→A story plus an investor-protection wrapper [shared/partners/aifc-gcc-passporting-opportunity.md]. DTCC's collateral thesis is literally that "tokenization reduces counterparty risk" [shared/partners/2026-06-02-dtcc.md]. A named crypto-crime policy from a Marsh-grade broker is a cheap, fast credibility asset that converts these objections into checkmarks — turning an exploratory insurance call into a repeatable institutional sales lever. Recommendation: close Marsh now using existing security artifacts, then productize "insured reserves" as a line item in the institutional deck.
