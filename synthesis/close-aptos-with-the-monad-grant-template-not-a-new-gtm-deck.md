# Aptos: close it with Monad's grant template, not a refreshed GTM deck

> The stalled Aptos lead keeps being framed as a 'rework the flows / refresh the GTM proposal' problem, but the Monad deal in the same neighborhood already proves the exact structure that converts an L1 foundation in weeks — a corridor-specific incentive grant that the L1 pays for — and Etherfuse should hand Aptos' internal champions that concrete, self-funding deal shape rather than more abstract ammunition.

## The situation

Aptos has sat in Nurture since before April 2026: David Yao planned re-engagement the week of April 6, and there was no confirmed response by the June pipeline snapshot; Aptos carries no deal value in the top-15 [wiki/entities/companies/aptos.md]. The recurring internal framing is to 'revisit the flows Aptos previously requested and rework them to give POCs stronger ammunition internally,' and the open questions ask whether to update the GTM proposal with Shinhan/BBVA/Monad as proof points [wiki/entities/companies/aptos.md]. That treats Monad as a *logo*, not as a *deal template* — which is the miss.

## Monad is the template, and it closed fast

Monad is one of Etherfuse's deepest integrations, and the mechanism that unlocked it is explicit: Monad's Investment Committee approved **$300k in incentives** split **80% MXN ($240k) / 20% KRW ($60k)** — i.e., tied to specific corridors, not a vague 'ecosystem partnership' — and the grant agreement was executed via DocuSign on January 21, 2026 after KYB [wiki/entities/companies/Monad.md]. Full token deployment via LayerZero, a live validator through Kiln, and Curve KTB/USDC pools followed, plus event co-sponsorship in Mexico [wiki/entities/companies/Monad.md]. The signal that matters for Aptos: once a corridor-specific incentive number was on the table, the L1's IC moved in weeks.

## Why this is the fast path for Aptos specifically

1. **The champions are already inside.** Etherfuse has two personal-relationship POCs at the Aptos Foundation (Andy's former Bitso colleague; David Yao's ITAM classmate), and Aptos previously ran a grant-program evaluation and requested specific flows [wiki/entities/companies/aptos.md]. What they lack is not warmth — it's a concrete, IC-ready ask. The Monad IC memo shape (corridor + dollar figure + deployment plan + revenue share) is exactly that.
2. **Deployment is near-zero marginal cost.** The platform is built so adding a chain is 'a configuration change rather than an engineering project' [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md] [wiki/entities/companies/aptos.md]. So an Aptos grant is close to pure upside: the L1 pays to onboard Etherfuse, and Etherfuse earns the standard 10–60 bps of AUM revenue share plus ramp fees on the resulting TVL [shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md]. This is a distribution channel that the counterparty *subsidizes*.
3. **The differentiator is ready-made.** Aptos' existing RWA presence is USD-only issuers; Etherfuse's non-USD sovereign roster (CETES, TESOURO, KTB, GILTS, USTRY, EUROB) is the differentiation the foundation is looking for [wiki/entities/companies/aptos.md] [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md]. Pair that with a corridor grant (MXN + KRW mirrors what Monad's IC already funded) and the internal pitch writes itself.

## The move

Don't rebuild the flows deck. Draft an Aptos-Foundation incentive proposal that mirrors the Monad IC package — a defined corridor (MXN, plus a KRW leg given the live Korean Stablebond/Shinhan momentum), a dollar figure, the config-only deployment path, and the partner revenue-share LOI terms [shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md] — and hand it to the two POCs as the artifact they take to their IC. Re-entry is warm and free: Varmeta's Frank Le pinged in Feb 2026 about RWA synergies and was never worked [wiki/entities/companies/aptos.md].
