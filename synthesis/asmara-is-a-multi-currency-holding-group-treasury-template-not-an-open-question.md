# Asmara is a ready-made multi-currency treasury template, not an open question

> Asmara Holding Group's own page lists 'What treasury needs does Asmara have?' as an open question, but four case studies sitting in the same neighborhood already answer it: an APAC infrastructure holding group is the exact idle-multi-currency-balance / FX-consolidation / float-yield profile Etherfuse has already templated and can pitch off-the-shelf.

The Asmara page is a stub: APAC 'corporate treasury prospect,' Nurture stage, no priority, a single gmail contact (Arturo Lujan), and three literal open questions — 'What specific treasury optimization needs does Asmara have? Is there a concrete use case for Stablebonds? Has any outreach been made?' [wiki/entities/companies/asmara-holding-group.md]. Those questions are already answered by material in the same neighborhood.

A *holding group* is by definition a diversified parent with subsidiaries spanning countries and currencies — and its treasury pain is idle balances plus multi-corridor FX overhead. Etherfuse has four productized answers to exactly this shape:

- **Idle local-currency reserves → sovereign yield:** the corporate-treasury case study turns $10M idle MXN into ~$600K/yr via CETES with same-day redemption and no workflow change [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md]; the Singapore SaaS case does the same for an SGD buffer into USTRY at ~4.5% APY with 5–8bps FX vs 40–80bps bank spread [shared/bd/case-study-tech-singapore.md].
- **Multi-currency float across APAC → USTRY + consolidated settlement:** the Hong Kong PSP case parks $3M of rotating PHP/IDR/THB/VND float in USTRY and collapses six correspondent-bank relationships into one integration [shared/bd/case-study-psp-hongkong.md].
- **Trapped/capital-controlled balances → on-chain repatriation:** the Dubai FMCG (Angola/Nigeria) and Rotterdam freight (Egypt/MENA) cases both convert stranded local currency to USDC at receipt, cutting FX drag from 300–500bps to ~20bps [shared/bd/case-study-fmcg-dubai-africa.md][shared/bd/case-study-freight-eu-egypt-mena.md].

Because USTRY, CETES and the multi-chain rails are already live, the pitch requires no new product — it is a segment-matched deck, not a build. The corporate-treasury sales angle (weekend yield capture, tax-efficient offshore holding, one API for multi-country access) is also already written up [shared/bd/why-stablebonds-vs-direct-bonds.md].
