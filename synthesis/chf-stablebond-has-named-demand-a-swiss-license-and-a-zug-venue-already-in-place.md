# A CHF Stablebond has a named DeFi customer, a Swiss license, and a Zug listing venue — but isn't on the roadmap

> P0 explicitly named Swiss franc as a token it wants for future forex collateral, and Etherfuse already holds a Switzerland DLT Act registration plus an existing Zug-domiciled venue relationship (Aqex) — meaning the regulatory and venue pieces for a CHF Stablebond are already standing while the asset itself is absent from the bond roadmap.

In the P0 collateral discussion, Jeremie specifically flagged that **'Swiss franc would be of interest for future tokens'** as P0 builds out forex/non-USD collateral support beyond its existing EURC [teams/sales/2026-03-26-0xyz-p0.md]. That demand lands on infrastructure Etherfuse already has: the company carries a **Switzerland DLT Act registration** [wiki/entities/companies/Etherfuse.md], and its Aqex relationship is anchored in a Zug domicile that matches that Swiss registration (per the existing Aqex finding). 

So the typically hardest parts of launching a new sovereign-bond asset — a credible domicile, a regulatory wrapper, and a listing venue — are already in place for CHF, yet a CHF Stablebond appears nowhere among the live six (CETES, TESOURO, GILTS, EUROB, USTRY, KTB). This is a low-cost roadmap addition where a concrete DeFi customer (P0) is pre-committed to using it as collateral, and where the Swiss footprint gives Etherfuse a non-USD asset it can credibly issue before most competitors. The constraint is sourcing/custody of the underlying Swiss paper, not regulatory access.
