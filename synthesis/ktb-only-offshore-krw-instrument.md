# KTB Stablebond is the only way to hold KRW value offshore — a structural monopoly nobody is selling

> Because KRW is a legally non-deliverable currency that cannot be held in any bank outside Korea, Etherfuse's KTB Stablebond is effectively the only retail/institutional instrument on earth that delivers offshore KRW exposure in actual won terms — a moat worth far more than the ~2% yield the marketing leads with.

## The connection no single doc makes

Our own research establishes that **KRW is officially non-deliverable offshore**: "You cannot hold Korean Won in a bank account outside Korea through normal banking channels," Korean-diaspora US banks (Shinhan Americas, Woori, Hana) offer only one-way USD→KRW remittance, and the entire ~$27B offshore KRW market is USD-settled NDF derivatives that hold no actual won [shared/research/krw-offshore-holding.md]. Deliverable offshore KRW infrastructure won't exist until the MOEF nostro trial in **September 2026** and full rollout in **2027** [shared/research/raw/2026-06-05-krw-offshore-holding-regulatory-framework.md].

Meanwhile, KTB Stablebond already does exactly this today: it is KRW-denominated, redeemable, multi-chain (Base, Solana, Polygon, Stellar, Monad), with no minimum, and bonds custodied at Shinhan Securities — and BDACS/KRW1 is already buying via USDC, KRW wire, and OTC paths [shared/products/krw1-operational-guide.md]. A holder of KTB carries "KRW/USD FX exposure on the underlying" — i.e. real won exposure — without a Korean bank account [shared/products/krw1-operational-guide.md].

**That means KTB is not a yield product competing against other yield products. It is the only deliverable offshore KRW instrument in existence.** No Korean bank, no NDF, no diaspora bank offers what one KTB token offers.

## Why this matters commercially

- The KTB/KRWQ collateral playbook currently leads with "AA-rated sovereign debt" and ~2% yield [shared/bd/collateral-playbook-ktb-krwq.md]. At 2% APY, KTB looks weak next to TESOURO (~12.5%) or CETES (~5.6%) [shared/volatile.md]. The yield framing buries the actual moat.
- The correct pitch to any non-Korean entity needing won exposure — Korean diaspora, importers/exporters invoicing Korea, funds wanting WGBI-correlated KRW after Korea's April 2026 index inclusion [shared/research/raw/2026-06-05-krw-offshore-holding-regulatory-framework.md] — is: *"There is no other way to hold won offshore. We are it, until at least 2027."*
- This window has a clock on it: once MOEF's 2027 deliverability infrastructure lands, banks may eventually offer KRW accounts and the monopoly narrows. Sell the scarcity now.
