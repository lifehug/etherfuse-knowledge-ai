# Australia Has Fewer Barriers Than Tajikistan and Kazakhstan Yet Gets Less Attention

> Foreign investors already own 50-55% of Australia's CGS through mature settlement infrastructure with no capital controls — meaning Australia's Q3 2026 launch requires only a custody handshake, not the regulatory campaigns and capital-account negotiations consuming resources in Kazakhstan and Tajikistan.

## The Barrier Comparison

Etherfuse is actively investing significant leadership time in two Central Asian markets. Compare their structural blockers to Australia's:

| Barrier | Kazakhstan | Tajikistan | Australia |
|---------|-----------|------------|----------|
| Capital account | Controlled; NBK approval required for foreign access [shared/meetings/2026-05-21-freedom-bank-followup.md] | Controlled; NBT notification required for capital outflows, 'make-or-break' item [shared/partners/2026-05-30-tajikistan-ibt-stablebond-analysis.md] | **Open**; foreign investors hold 50-55% of CGS already [wiki/markets/australia.md] |
| Regulatory approval | NBK sandbox letter pending; 6+ rounds of business plan review [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md] | Decree 798 permits investment assets; NBT engagement needed [shared/partners/2026-05-26-international-bank-of-tajikistan.md] | ASIC Innovation Hub + Treasury consultation paper on tokenization published 2024 [wiki/markets/australia.md] |
| Primary market access | Only 5 banks can buy primary; Etherfuse buys secondary [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md] | CASE auction access via authorized member only [shared/partners/2026-05-30-tajikistan-ibt-stablebond-analysis.md] | 13-bank primary dealer panel with established foreign participation [wiki/entities/companies/australian-office-of-financial-management.md] |
| Secondary liquidity | Adequate for KZT treasuries | 'Effectively nil' [shared/partners/2026-05-30-tajikistan-ibt-stablebond-analysis.md] | ~A$600B market, highly liquid [wiki/markets/australia.md] |

| Market size | Single-digit billions in KZT treasuries | Single-digit millions per T-bill issuance [shared/partners/2026-05-30-tajikistan-ibt-stablebond-analysis.md] | **~US$400B** CGS outstanding [wiki/entities/companies/australian-office-of-financial-management.md] |

## The Attention Mismatch

Kazakhstan has consumed an in-country trip, meetings with NBK, AIFC, Halyk, Freedom, KASE, and NIC NBK. Tajikistan has a full concept brief, market analysis, and scheduled intro call. Australia has: a research page, zero contacts, zero interactions, and four open questions about basic positioning [wiki/entities/companies/australian-office-of-financial-management.md].

Yet Australia's launch requires operationally less: identify a custody partner (likely solvable through Standard Chartered or AOFM panel members), structure the product (same CGS short-end paper, same Stablebond architecture), and go. No regulatory sandbox, no capital-account negotiation, no central bank letter.

## The Revenue Case

The pipeline report values Kazakhstan (Halyk + Freedom) at **$3.5M + $3.5M** in revenue overrides [shared/bd/pipeline-report-operations.md]. Australia's TAM is estimated at US$2-4B in addressable flow over five years [shared/research/raw/2026-07-03-market-research-australia-websearch.md], with a GDP per capita of ~US$64,500 and 99.5% banked population [shared/research/raw/2026-07-03-market-research-australia-websearch.md]. Even conservative capture rates on a US$400B market dwarf the Central Asian opportunity.

## Recommendation

Assign an owner and a Linear lead to the Australia launch. The immediate action is identifying which AOFM primary dealer panel members overlap with existing Etherfuse contacts. This is a Q3 2026 roadmap item [shared/bond-roadmap.md] with no contact and no deal — it needs one now.
