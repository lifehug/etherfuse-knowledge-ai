# ARQ is a four-market play; the MXN-only proposal buries its strongest hook (Brazil TESOURO)

> ARQ operates in Mexico, Brazil, Argentina, and Colombia, yet the only proposal Etherfuse ever sent was a 6-page MXN-only brief — self-limiting a stablecoin-native neobank whose Brazil footprint maps directly onto the highest-yield live Stablebond (TESOURO ~13% BRL).

ARQ Finance (formerly DolarApp) is a stablecoin-native neobank serving Mexico, Brazil, Argentina, and Colombia with 2M+ customers and $10B+ annualized volume [wiki/entities/companies/arq-finance.md]. On the April 2026 discovery call Andy walked through the full Stablebond line with concrete yields — CETES ~5% MXN, USTRY ~3.15% USD, TESOURO ~13.18% BRL — but the follow-up material that actually went out was a **6-page MXN-only proposal**, re-sent unchanged in June and then left to go stale [wiki/entities/companies/arq-finance.md].

That framing throws away ARQ's best fit. Two of ARQ's four core markets already have **live** Stablebonds: CETES (MXN, live, BBVA custody) and TESOURO (BRL, live, B3 custody, ~13.14% APY) [shared/bond-roadmap.md][shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. TESOURO is the single highest-yield product in the entire live roster — and ARQ is a stablecoin-native player already operating in Brazil, exactly the profile that benefits most from a 13% local-currency yield floor versus the zero-yield USD balances neobanks default to [shared/bd/why-stablebonds-vs-direct-bonds.md]. Pitching MXN (~5%) first, alone, to a company whose Brazil arm can offer 13% is leading with the weaker number.

The remaining two markets are also in Etherfuse's near-term path: Argentina and Colombia are both flagged for 2026 Stablebond issuance in adjacent deals (Colombia via the in-flight COP Stablebond with BBVA, Argentina via Etherfuse's own roadmap references), meaning ARQ is a rare prospect where **all four of its markets are either live or explicitly on the build plan**. The right motion is a four-currency embed anchored on Brazil, not an MXN savings feature — and it directly matches the proven 'Digital Wallet Adds Yield-Bearing Savings for Millions of Users' case study built for a 2M+ user LATAM multi-currency wallet [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md]. Reviving the deal on a Brazil-led, multi-currency pitch changes both the size and the credibility of the offer.
