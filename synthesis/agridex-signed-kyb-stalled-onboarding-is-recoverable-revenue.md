# Agridex is a signed-KYB deal abandoned mid-onboarding — your own playbooks say to chase it

> Agridex completed and signed KYB but went stale after a single follow-up attempt — the exact failure mode the sub-issue handoff and the 'persistence pays' follow-up framework were written to prevent — making re-engagement a near-zero-cost shot at an Onboarding-stage (92%-weighted) conversion.

**The fact pattern.** Agridex (agricultural RWA DEX) has KYB "completed and signed," yet the deal is parked at Nurture / Low priority after a single outreach attempt that hit a vacationing contact (Henry) in early Feb 2026 [wiki/entities/companies/agridex.md]. The same record shows four duplicate onboarding tickets (ONB-28, ONB-26, ONB-23, ONB-20) had to be merged and archived in Feb 2026 — i.e., the deal generated ownership/visibility confusion before anyone re-engaged the customer [wiki/entities/companies/agridex.md].

**This is the precise problem two internal documents already solve.** The March 2026 sub-issues proposal was written to kill exactly this duplicate-ticket / "who owns the lead" ambiguity at the Won→onboarding handoff, keeping one BD owner accountable on the parent issue [shared/bd/pipeline-sub-issues-proposal.md]. And the follow-up framework explicitly states the conversion math being left on the table: "70% of sales emails stop after first attempt; 21% reply rate on second email if first unanswered" — Agridex stopped after one attempt to a contact who was simply on vacation [shared/bd/followup-framework.md].

**Why this matters for revenue, not just hygiene.** The June 2026 valuation weights Onboarding at 92% probability — the second-highest stage in the funnel [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md]. A deal that has already passed KYB signature is past the slowest, most expensive gate (compliance docs, Mexican proof-of-address friction described elsewhere [shared/partners/bekey.md]); reviving it costs one personalized message, not a new sales cycle. Agridex is the visible symptom of a systemic leak: with 86 companies in Compliance and 36 in Onboarding [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md], any signed-KYB record that lapses to passive 'Nurture' is near-won revenue silently aging out.

**Concrete move.** Treat Agridex as the test case: send a single value-first re-engagement message (the framework's Template 2/3) tied to a specific product hook — an ag-commodity DEX holds idle settlement float between trades, the textbook 'yield on idle balances' Stablebond pitch — and reclassify the deal back into an owned Onboarding sub-issue per the new handoff model. Then run the same sweep across every other signed-KYB record sitting in passive Nurture.
