# Anchorage already owns a USD-treasury bet (OpenEden) — pitch them the non-USD white space, not USTRY

> Anchorage's two named lead products in the Etherfuse deal are CETES and USTRY, but Anchorage is itself a backer of OpenEden, a tokenized US T-bill issuer — so USTRY competes with Anchorage's own portfolio while CETES and the 50+ non-USD roadmap are exactly the exposure OpenEden can't give them.

The Anchorage strategy doc names CETES (~6.19% APY) and USTRY (~3.63%) as the two lead products for a Tier-1 custody integration [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md]. But two independent ecosystem files flag that Anchorage Digital is the backer of OpenEden, whose flagship product is tokenized US T-Bills (TBILL) [shared/ecosystems/sovereign-debt.md][shared/ecosystems/competitors.md]. That is the same instrument USTRY tokenizes — meaning half of Etherfuse's lead pitch to Anchorage is a product Anchorage has already placed a bet on through a competing issuer.

**Why this matters for the close.** Anchorage's stated blocker has never been technical — the asset review on CETES/Stellar is finalizing and the technical analysis came back positive [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md]. The blocker is an internal business case around GTM viability and incremental demand [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md]. Pitching USTRY into a house that already custodies/backs OpenEden TBILL gives the Anchorage business-case owner (Kyle Clark, institutional sales) an easy reason to say 'we already have tokenized treasuries' — it frames Etherfuse as redundant rather than additive.

**The reframe.** Etherfuse's own competitive positioning is explicitly 'non-USD sovereign bonds, multi-currency, multi-country' versus USD-only RWA issuers [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md]. The bond roadmap is dominated by non-USD paper Anchorage cannot source through OpenEden — CETES (live), KTB, GILTS, EUROB, TESOURO live today, plus 50+ planned including the entire 2026 Q3/Q4 Asia and MENA set [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. Leading the Anchorage conversation with CETES + the non-USD roster (and demoting USTRY to a 'we also cover the dollar leg' footnote) converts the OpenEden relationship from an objection into a proof point: Anchorage has validated the tokenized-treasury thesis with a USD product, and Etherfuse is the only way to extend that thesis into every other sovereign currency.

**Bonus on economics.** Anchorage's defined revenue share — 30 bps for 6–7.9% yield, 40 bps for 8–9.9%, 60 bps for 10%+ [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md] — is identical to Etherfuse's standard partner tiers [shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md], and the higher tiers only trigger on the high-yield non-USD products (e.g., TESOURO ~13%). So the non-USD lead is also the higher-margin lead for Anchorage, reinforcing the same pivot.
