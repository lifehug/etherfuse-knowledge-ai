# AIFC fast-track approval is the single lever that unlocks Freedom Bank's 5M-user channel

> Freedom Bank's stalled distribution deal is explicitly gated on an NBK/AIFC regulatory signal, and the very AIFC official who created the first-ever fast-track approval is also Freedom CEO Turlov's close friend who volunteered to personally navigate Freedom — making the AIFC in-principle license the one action that both satisfies the precondition and warm-opens the channel.

Freedom Bank Kazakhstan is a confirmed partner (a NASDAQ-listed fintech, ~$10B market cap) whose Super App has 5M+ customers and ~10,000 new downloads/day, and it has agreed to distribute the KZT Stablebond — but it named three hard preconditions before moving, the second of which is explicit: "Regulatory endorsement — NBK/AIFC approval is a prerequisite for trust; Freedom will feel more confident once the National Bank has publicly signaled," with the earliest evaluation window pushed to July–August 2026 [shared/partners/2026-05-21-freedom-bank-aidos-zhumagulov.md].

The AIFC neighborhood contains the exact instrument that satisfies this precondition faster than anyone expects. AIFC CIO Talgat Amanbayev personally created a fast-track in-principle approval process — the first in AIFC's 8-year history — that grants "AFSA regulated entity" status with a license number and limited onboarding rights well ahead of full licensing [wiki/entities/companies/aifc-afsa.md]. In-principle approval publicly confers exactly the kind of regulatory signal Freedom said it needs.

The non-obvious link is the person: Talgat is a close friend of Freedom Holdings CEO Turlov (Freedom sponsors AIFC events), and he explicitly "offered to personally help navigate Freedom" [wiki/entities/companies/aifc-afsa.md, shared/partners/2026-05-25-aifc-talgat-amanbayev.md]. So the AIFC license track is not just Etherfuse's international wrapper — it is simultaneously (a) the public regulatory endorsement that de-blocks a 5M-user distribution deal and (b) a warm, top-down introduction into that same partner. Dave also already cited Freedom Holdings' SPC ($1B bond programme) as the precedent for Etherfuse's own SPC vehicle in the NIC NBK pitch [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md], so the structural template is shared too.

Decision: sequence the AIFC in-principle application to land inside Freedom's July–August window and ask Talgat to make the Freedom introduction concurrently — the same milestone pays off twice.
