# Georgia is inbound government demand with a ready custody path — and no pipeline entry

> The Government of Georgia approached Etherfuse to tokenize its sovereign debt, Halyk Finance already has a Georgia presence to source/custody the paper, and Georgia sits on the internal roadmap — yet it appears in no pipeline, making it the same shovel-ready easy win as Uzbekistan but with inbound demand already in hand.

Three independent signals in this neighborhood converge on Georgia, but nothing connects them into an active deal. First, demand is **inbound and government-level**: *"The Government of Georgia has approached Etherfuse to tokenize their sovereign debt"* [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-meeting.md] — a stronger starting position than the cold government outreach Etherfuse is running in Uzbekistan and Pakistan [shared/partners/2026-06-05-uzbekistan-government-overview.md, shared/partners/2026-06-11-pakistan-pvara-etherfuse-introduction.md]. Second, the **custody/sourcing path already exists**: Halyk Finance has a presence in Georgia, and Dave flagged that 'Etherfuse would want to buy Georgian debt through Halyk Finance if that deal proceeds' [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-meeting.md] — reusing the exact confirmed partner standing up the KZT Stablebond. Third, it's **already on the internal roadmap**: in the NIC NBK pitch Dave named Central Asia next steps as 'Uzbekistan, Georgia' [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md].

The regulatory wrapper is also pre-cleared: AFSA already holds a Banking MoU with the National Bank of Georgia [shared/partners/aifc-gcc-passporting-opportunity.md], meaning the AIFC SPC vehicle Etherfuse is incorporating for Kazakhstan can extend to a Georgian product without a fresh jurisdictional setup — the same 'reuse the AIFC SPC, don't shop for bespoke custody' logic already applied to offshore INR.

Despite inbound demand + ready custody + roadmap presence + MoU coverage, Georgia has no Linear lead, no concept brief, and no follow-up owner anywhere in this slice. This is the Uzbekistan pattern (shovel-ready, matches the playbook) but warmer, because the counterparty is the one who reached out. The action is to convert the Halyk Georgia conversation into a concrete intro and produce a Georgia concept brief on the IBT/Uzbekistan template.
