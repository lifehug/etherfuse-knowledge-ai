# Colombia: unbuilt market, stacked partner demand

> At least six independent partners/prospects touch Colombia and it sits on Stellar's Q4 2026 roadmap, yet Etherfuse ships no Colombian product — a COP Stablebond would activate Addi, Cobre, Airtm, Bitso and Reap simultaneously instead of one at a time.

Addi's profile frames the company purely as a Mexico-expansion float play, but Addi is fundamentally a **Colombian** BNPL lender [wiki/entities/companies/addi.md] — and Colombia is the connective tissue across a surprising number of names in this neighborhood that no single page names as a cluster:

- **Addi** — Colombia HQ, expanding to Mexico [wiki/entities/companies/addi.md]
- **Cobre** — "runs significant daily cash flows across Colombia, Mexico, and the US" and is in Negotiation [shared/meetings/2026-04-30-cobre-meet-the-team.md]
- **Airtm** — Colombia listed as "New issuance, Q3 2026" in the priority-countries table [shared/partners/airtm-partnership-proposal.md]
- **Bitso** — "Colombia expansion planned" [shared/partners/meetings/2026-02-03-meeting-insights.md]
- **Reap** — live operations in Mexico, Brazil, Colombia [shared/partners/meetings/2026-04-08-reap-research.md]
- **Galileo** — expanded to Colombia in 2021, 1M+ regional accounts [shared/partners/galileo-research.md]
- **Stellar 2026 roadmap** — Colombia is LATAM-4, slotted for Q4 2026 [teams/ceo/2026-01-27-stellar-stanChart-strategic-context.md]

The gap: Etherfuse has **no live Colombian sovereign product**. Every Colombia conversation today defaults to USTRY/CETES/USDC rather than a COP-denominated instrument that matches what these partners' users actually earn and spend in. The synthesis is that Colombia has crossed the threshold where it should be a **product decision, not a per-deal afterthought** — a single COP Stablebond (TES short-term paper) would give Addi a home-market white-label savings product, Cobre a third-currency treasury rail, Airtm its already-scheduled Q3 issuance, and Bitso/Reap a local-yield collateral asset, all off one custody build. Standard Chartered's flow-of-funds custody model already covers the mechanics for adding a new sovereign market in ~4 weeks [shared/partners/standard-chartered-flow-of-funds.md], and Airtm explicitly offers P2P off-ramp coverage where Etherfuse lacks institutional rails [shared/partners/airtm-partnership-proposal.md]. Sequencing Colombia ahead of the Q4 Stellar slot converts a roadmap line item into a multi-partner unlock.
