# Aplazo's handoff to Bando lands on a channel currently blocked at onboarding

> Etherfuse routed its highest-fit Mexican BNPL lead (Aplazo: 9,000+ merchants, ~$100M revenue, High priority) to Bando — the exact reseller whose own clients are failing to convert because of the IFL signing/onboarding friction Bando flagged as a 'big issue' — so the handoff is parked on a rail that is structurally not converting.

Aplazo is a strong-fit, High-priority discovery lead — inbound from SVP Finance Kunal Kanjolia, 9,000+ merchants (Walmart, AliExpress, Sephora, Aeroméxico), ~$100M revenue, with idle MXN float between merchant settlement and consumer repayment that maps cleanly onto tokenized CETES yield [wiki/entities/companies/aplazo.md]. In April 2026 the opportunity was handed entirely to Bando, with all follow-up (CETES mechanics, merchant-wallet examples, AJ Taylor loop-in) routed through Bando going forward [wiki/entities/companies/aplazo.md].

The non-obvious problem: Bando's pipeline is currently blocked precisely at the step Aplazo would need to clear. On June 10, 2026 Bando reported that 'operational/legal onboarding friction is preventing their clients from completing onboarding,' explicitly flagged as a 'big issue for Etherfuse,' triggering an evaluation to replace the IFL signing tool with DocuSign [wiki/entities/companies/Bando.md]. Bando's strategic value 'is undermined' if its end-clients cannot complete onboarding, and that fix has no confirmed Linear ticket or timeline [wiki/entities/companies/Bando.md].

So Etherfuse took its best Mexican BNPL lead and routed it through the one partner whose conversion engine is jammed — and the open question 'has Bando made contact with Kunal Kanjolia?' remains unanswered on the Aplazo page [wiki/entities/companies/aplazo.md].

**Decision-useful moves (low cost, high impact):**
1. Keep the direct line warm: Kunal Kanjolia came in inbound as the primary commercial contact — Etherfuse should not let the only relationship run through a stalled reseller [wiki/entities/companies/aplazo.md].
2. Sequence the DocuSign fix *ahead of* a 9,000-merchant rollout; pushing Aplazo through IFL invites the same drop-off Bando is already seeing [wiki/entities/companies/Bando.md].
3. Reconsider the channel topology: Bando has referred four named accounts [wiki/entities/companies/Bando.md]; Aplazo alone is two orders of magnitude larger in merchant reach, arguing it should be treated as a distribution partner in its own right rather than a sub-lead beneath Bando.
