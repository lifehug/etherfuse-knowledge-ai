# ASIC's open tokenization consultation is the CNBV moat playbook — and the window is open now

> ASIC is actively consulting on a tokenization framework with Treasury (2024), which is the exact same pre-regulatory window that Etherfuse used to secure its 'only authorized tokenizer' status via a 2-year CNBV dialogue in Mexico — and engaging now, before the framework is finalized, is the highest-leverage regulatory action for Australia.

The ASIC page and Australia market page both note that ASIC is 'consulting on a tokenisation framework in coordination with Treasury (2024)' [wiki/entities/companies/australian-securities-and-investments-commission.md] [wiki/markets/Australia.md]. The Innovation Hub is the formal sandbox channel for fintech engagement with ASIC [wiki/entities/companies/australian-securities-and-investments-commission.md].

The Mexican precedent is instructive and well-documented: Etherfuse engaged CNBV in a '2-year regulatory dialogue with multiple rounds of questions' that produced a '300-page official letter' and the determination that 'tokenized assets are not classified as securities' [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. The result: 'Only Mexican blockchain company authorized to tokenize government bonds' and a regulatory precedent that 'lowers barriers for other fintechs' [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md]. This exclusive status is Etherfuse's #1 competitive moat in Mexico [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

The same strategy is already in flight in Bermuda, where the BMA published a tokenization discussion paper (November 2025) and consultation paper (April 2026) proposing frameworks that distinguish 'digital twins' from 'native tokens' [wiki/concepts/tokenization.md]. And in Kazakhstan, Etherfuse has already 'completed securities regulatory processes' and received 'informal confirmation that its government bonds are not classified as securities' [wiki/concepts/regulatory.md].

ASIC's consultation is at the formative stage — the rules are being written, not enforced. Submitting a response now that positions CGS Stablebonds as a use case (and argues for a non-security classification for tokenized secondary-market sovereign debt) would replicate the CNBV playbook: shape the determination before competitors even show up.

The ASIC open questions on the wiki page — 'What is the current status of ASIC's tokenisation framework consultation, and what are the expected timelines for finalisation?' and 'Is there a preferred channel or intermediary for initial regulatory dialogue with ASIC?' [wiki/entities/companies/australian-securities-and-investments-commission.md] — are the specific items to resolve immediately.

**Action:** File a submission to ASIC's tokenization consultation and/or apply to the Innovation Hub sandbox before Q3 2026 CGS launch. The goal is a non-security classification for CGS tokens, mirroring CNBV P090/2024. Every month the consultation stays open without Etherfuse engaging is a month a competitor could enter instead.
