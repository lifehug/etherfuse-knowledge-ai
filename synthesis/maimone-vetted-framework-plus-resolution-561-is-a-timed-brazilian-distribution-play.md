# Maimone's Vetted Legal Framework + Resolution 561 = Timed Brazilian Distribution Channel

> Brazil's Resolution 561 (effective October 1, 2026) bans eFX providers from using stablecoins for cross-border settlement, but Maimone & Associados has already completed a 20-question legal vetting of Etherfuse's structure on behalf of Web3 clients who want to distribute Stablebonds via Anchor — meaning Etherfuse has pre-cleared distribution partners positioned in the exact regulatory gap that 561 creates, on a hard deadline.

## The Regulatory Opening

Resolution BCB No. 561 bans regulated fintechs and eFX providers from using stablecoins or any cryptocurrency to settle cross-border payments, effective October 1, 2026 [shared/concepts/brazil-efx-resolution-561.md]. This shuts down the settlement path that companies like Nomad and Braza Bank built. But the resolution explicitly targets eFX providers, not VASP/SPSAV operators — and Etherfuse's VASP-channel routing (Resolutions 519–521) is outside Resolution 561's direct scope [shared/concepts/brazil-efx-resolution-561.md].

## The Pre-Cleared Distribution Partners

Maimone & Associados, a Rio de Janeiro law firm, sent a formal 20-question legal inquiry on behalf of 'Web3 clients who want to offer Etherfuse Stablebonds via Anchor integration' [shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md]. Etherfuse responded with a comprehensive, externally-distributable legal framework covering:

- Token ownership mechanics and bankruptcy remoteness under Mexican law (Q2, Q15)
- Multi-jurisdiction treatment of backing assets (Q5, Q6, Q12, Q13)
- Brazilian-specific concerns including the Lei de Usura interest rate cap (Q19)
- AML/CFT compliance architecture with full auditor credentials (Q11, Q18)
- Open-end redemption structure (Q20)

This means Maimone's clients have already done the legal DD that typically takes months. They are pre-qualified distribution partners.

## Why This Converges Now

Brazil's crypto market runs $6–8B/month (~$70–90B annualized), with stablecoins at ~90% of volume — 3–4x larger than the regulated eFX market [shared/concepts/brazil-efx-resolution-561.md]. When Resolution 561 forces eFX providers off stablecoin rails on October 1, their volume needs to go somewhere. TESOURO, Etherfuse's live BRL Stablebond at ~13% APY [shared/master-knowledge-base.md], is the local-currency alternative that directly addresses the dollarization concern BCB designed 561 to solve.

Resolution 561 explicitly validates Etherfuse's thesis: 'Local-currency alternatives have a structural opening. USD stablecoins force dollarization on people who earn and spend in local currency' [shared/concepts/brazil-efx-resolution-561.md].

## Recommended Action

Treat Maimone's client cohort as a timed distribution pipeline with an October 1 forcing function. The legal vetting is complete; the regulatory gap is documented; the product is live. The Maimone response document should be productized as the standard Brazilian institutional DD package and used to accelerate onboarding of any VASP-routed Brazilian partner before 561 takes effect.
