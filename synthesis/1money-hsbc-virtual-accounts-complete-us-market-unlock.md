# 1Money's HSBC US Virtual Accounts are the missing operational rail behind the US market unlock

> The US-market thesis to date solves the legal barriers (OCC reputation-risk rule + sub-$100K '40 Act fund) but not the operational one — actual USD/ACH fiat rails for a US entity — and 1Money offers exactly that in a near-zero-cost barter where Etherfuse trades MX/BR/EU/UK/KR treasury coverage it already ships.

Etherfuse's documented US-market unlock is regulatory: the OCC/FDIC reputation-risk rule removes debanking risk and a domestic '40 Act exempt fund can be stood up for under $100K (existing finding: us-market-doubly-unlocked-banking-plus-40-act-fund). But neither solves the practical gap — Etherfuse has *no US fiat rails*. The 1Money discussion fills precisely that hole: 1Money's **HSBC-backed US Virtual Accounts were discussed as a US rails solution for Etherfuse's US entity/rails gap (ACH and potentially FX)** [wiki/entities/companies/1money.md].

What makes this a fast, easy win is the structure of the deal: it is a two-sided barter. 1Money currently does **only US tokenized treasuries and explicitly needs Etherfuse to fill the MX/BR/EU/UK/KR gap** — products Etherfuse already has live [wiki/entities/companies/1money.md][shared/bond-roadmap.md]. So Etherfuse can obtain US ACH/USD rails by giving 1Money access to inventory it produces at zero marginal cost, rather than building or buying a US banking relationship.

The connected picture no single page states: the US market needs (1) the legal path [covered] and (2) USD fiat rails for a US-facing entity [1Money]. 1Money supplies leg (2) cheaply, while simultaneously becoming a distribution channel into the $60B+ US-Mexico remittance corridor via CETES/MXNe settlement [wiki/entities/companies/1money.md][wiki/markets/mexico-cetes.md]. That dual payoff — inbound US rails *and* corridor distribution from one barter — makes scheduling the stalled demo call (the only blocker) a higher priority than its 'Discovery' pipeline stage implies.
