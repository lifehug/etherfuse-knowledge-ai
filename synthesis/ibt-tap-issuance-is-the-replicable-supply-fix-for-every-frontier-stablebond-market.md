# IBT's Tap-Issuance Model Is the Replicable Supply-Side Fix for Every Frontier Stablebond

> The 'tap-style' rolling T-bill issuance facility proposed for Tajikistan's Ministry of Finance solves a supply constraint that recurs across every frontier market on the 2026 pipeline — where even 1% capture of target demand dwarfs existing domestic T-bill issuance — and should be templated as a standard Etherfuse MoF engagement offering.

## The Supply Bottleneck

The IBT concept brief proposes that Tajikistan's Ministry of Finance adopt a UK Gilt-style 'tap' issuance model: a standing short-term GKV (treasury-bill) line reopened on a rolling basis as Stablebond subscriptions accumulate, rather than issued in infrequent lump auctions [shared/partners/2026-05-30-etherfuse-ibt-concept-brief.md]. The reason: even at 1% capture of Tajikistan's $5.8B annual remittance flow, the ~$58M demand dwarfs the entire domestic T-bill market at 'single-digit millions per issuance' [shared/partners/2026-05-30-tajikistan-ibt-stablebond-analysis.md].

## The Same Constraint Recurs Elsewhere

This is not a Tajikistan-specific problem. The 2026 expansion pipeline includes Ghana (Q3), Kenya (Q4), and other frontier markets [shared/master-knowledge-base.md] that share the same characteristics: high unmet demand (remittances, diaspora savings), small domestic T-bill issuance volumes, and near-absent secondary markets.

Even in Kazakhstan — a larger market — the operational framework explicitly locks Etherfuse into secondary-market-only purchases [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-framework.md], and NIC NBK's conversation surfaced capacity questions about how much paper Etherfuse can absorb relative to market depth [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Halyk Finance confirmed 50% market share in Kazakhstan bond issuance, signaling that primary-market access through Halyk could eventually be the same tap-style path [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-meeting.md].

## Why a Template Matters

The IBT proposal packages three innovations that generalize:

1. **Demand-led issuance**: the MoF issues as Stablebond subscriptions accumulate rather than on a fixed calendar
2. **Local anchor partner as aggregator**: the partner bank (IBT, Halyk, etc.) aggregates tokenized demand and channels it into the primary auction
3. **The token IS the liquidity layer**: hold-to-maturity laddering means secondary-market illiquidity is not a constraint — the Stablebond's instant-redemption infrastructure replaces the secondary market [shared/partners/2026-05-30-etherfuse-ibt-concept-brief.md]

If Etherfuse templates this MoF engagement (pitch deck, sample tap facility terms, demand-sizing model keyed to remittance/diaspora flows), every frontier market launch starts with a solved supply problem rather than discovering it post-commitment.

## Recommended Action

Productize the IBT tap-issuance proposal into a reusable MoF engagement template. Priority targets: Ghana and Kenya (Q3–Q4 2026 pipeline) where domestic issuance volumes are likely in the same single-digit-millions range as Tajikistan. For Kazakhstan, track whether Halyk Finance's expressed interest in tokenizing their own bond placements [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-meeting.md] effectively creates the same tap mechanism through a different path.
