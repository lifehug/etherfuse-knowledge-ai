# AppWorks assembles the whole Taiwan TWD launch stack

> AppWorks is a single warm inbound relationship that bundles capital, a licensed exchange, and an institutional financial group for a market Etherfuse already plans to enter — Taiwan's TWD Stablebond (TAIWTB) is scheduled for Q4 2026 on the authoritative roadmap, yet the AppWorks page treats it as a low-priority 20-minute call and never mentions the roadmap at all.

## The disconnect

The AppWorks page frames the relationship as an open-ended 'compare notes' inbound: a $375M Taiwan crypto/AI fund that reached out expressing conviction in EM sovereign-bond-backed stablecoins and requested a 20-minute intro call, with deal status 'No meeting confirmed yet' [wiki/entities/companies/appworks.md]. It lists distribution and capital as generic value-adds but never connects to a concrete product.

The concrete product already exists on the roadmap. The authoritative bond roadmap schedules a **TWD Treasury Bill (TAIWTB) for Taiwan in 2026 Q4** [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. So AppWorks is not a research pen-pal — it is warm, inbound demand landing on a launch Etherfuse is committed to shipping regardless.

## Why AppWorks maps onto the proven local-partner playbook

Etherfuse's repeatable market-entry pattern is: a licensed local venue for listing/distribution + a local financial institution for paper sourcing and custody + a capital/coordination partner. This is exactly the Kazakhstan structure (KASE as Solana-native listing venue, AIX as the TradFi Nasdaq-engine exchange, Halyk as custodian/broker) [shared/partners/2026-05-22-kase-digital-assets.md, shared/partners/2026-05-25-aifc-talgat-amanbayev.md, shared/partners/2026-05-21-kazakhstan-partnerships.md] and the Korea structure (Shinhan Securities as FSC-licensed custodian for KTB) [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

AppWorks' network supplies all three legs for Taiwan through one door:

- **Licensed exchange / retail listing venue:** AppWorks shares a parent (Taiwan Mobile group) with TWEX, Taiwan's licensed crypto exchange [wiki/entities/companies/appworks.md] — the direct analog to KASE/AIX as the in-market listing rail.
- **Institutional financial group:** close ties to Fubon, one of Taiwan's largest financial groups [wiki/entities/companies/appworks.md] — the candidate custody / bond-sourcing partner, the role BBVA/Shinhan/Halyk play elsewhere. (Fubon's specific primary-dealer status is inferred, not confirmed in these sources — a first-call diligence question.)
- **Capital + coordination:** a $375M fund that does primary deals [wiki/entities/companies/appworks.md], relevant to the customer-led Series B Etherfuse is planning with strategic partners like Shinhan, Stellar, BBVA and Halyk [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md].

## Why this is underpriced

Etherfuse's KTB communication playbook shows exactly how a licensed-exchange + local-bank pairing gets marketed once live ('backed by [sovereign] Treasury Bonds', collateral-first messaging) [shared/bd/collateral-playbook-ktb-krwq.md], and the Visa local-issuer card-collateral model plus float/yield case studies replicate into any market with a tokenized local asset and local rails [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md, shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md]. Taiwan would inherit all of that infrastructure on day one.

The recommendation: treat the AppWorks intro call not as a thesis chat but as the opening move on the Q4 2026 Taiwan TWD launch — scope TWEX as listing venue, probe Fubon's custody/primary-market access, and size AppWorks' check for the customer-led round. The trust caveat: the AppWorks page rests on a single low-trust source [wiki/entities/companies/appworks.md], so the Fubon/TWEX roles need confirmation on the first call before committing sequencing.
