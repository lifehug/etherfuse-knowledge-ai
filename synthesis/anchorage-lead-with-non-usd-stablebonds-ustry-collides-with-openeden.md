# Anchorage: lead with CETES/non-USD, not USTRY — it competes with Anchorage's own OpenEden

> The Anchorage deal is pitched with CETES and USTRY as the lead products, but Anchorage already backs OpenEden's TBILL tokenized US Treasuries — so pushing USTRY invites channel conflict, while the non-USD Stablebonds are pure complements and the faster path to a yes.

The Anchorage entity page names CETES (~6.19%) and USTRY (~3.63%) as the two lead products in the Tier-1 strategy [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md]. But two separate intelligence sources establish that Anchorage Digital is the institutional backer of OpenEden, whose TBILL product is tokenized US Treasuries: 'OpenEden (TBILL): US T-Bills, backed by Anchorage Digital' [shared/ecosystems/sovereign-debt.md], and again in the competitor table, 'OpenEden ... backed by Anchorage Digital (Feb 2026)' [shared/master-knowledge-base.md], which also lists OpenEden as an Etherfuse competitor in the RWA/tokenized-treasuries category.

The implication no single page states: **USTRY (Etherfuse's tokenized US Treasury) directly overlaps with the product of Anchorage's own portfolio company.** Asking Anchorage's institutional-sales team (Kyle Clark, who 'evaluates client demand and revenue justification' [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md]) to build a GTM business case for a USD T-bill token that cannibalizes OpenEden's TBILL is precisely the kind of internal friction that stalls the 'internal business case' hurdle already flagged as the sole remaining blocker [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md].

By contrast, CETES (MXN) and the 40+ non-USD Stablebonds on the roadmap [shared/bond-roadmap.md] are pure complements — Anchorage has no non-USD sovereign product, and they map onto its stated Mexico/LATAM expansion strategy (Monica Ramirez de Arellano's mandate) [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md]. The non-USD angle is also Etherfuse's documented differentiator: '95%+ of tokenized treasuries market is USD-only' [shared/ecosystems/sovereign-debt.md].

**Action:** Reframe the Anchorage business case around CETES + the non-USD roster (and the Mexico SPEI settlement layer at 5–20 bps [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md]) as the wedge, explicitly positioning them as *additive* to OpenEden rather than competitive. Hold USTRY back until the non-USD relationship is live — it removes the one reason an Anchorage product owner has to say no.
