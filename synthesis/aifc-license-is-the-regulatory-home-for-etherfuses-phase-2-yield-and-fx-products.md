# The AIFC SPC license is the regulated venue for Etherfuse's highest-margin phase-2 products, not just a KZ compliance chore

> Two of the six activities Etherfuse is licensing through AFSA — Principal/Yield Separation and FX/Any-Currency Exchange — are the exact products Dave told NIC NBK are the 18-month roadmap and the multi-currency FX marketplace, meaning the Kazakhstan license quietly authorizes Etherfuse's most lucrative future business lines that have no regulated home anywhere else today.

Existing AIFC findings frame the SPC as (a) a tax-deferred wrapper sellable worldwide and (b) a GCC-passporting key for the MENA bond roadmap. Both miss what the *license scope itself* unlocks.

Etherfuse confirmed six target licensed activities for Etherfuse SPC Limited: (1) Asset Issuance, (2) **Principal/Yield Separation — the PT/YT model for DeFi use cases**, (3) Market Making, (4) Oracle Provision, (5) Accept Any Currency, and (6) **FX and Asset Exchange between any currency-denominated asset** [wiki/entities/companies/aifc.md — FIN-491, FIN-413].

Activity #2 maps directly onto the phase-2 roadmap Dave pitched NIC NBK: *"After 18 months: yield trading (principal/interest separation, yield derivatives)"* [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Etherfuse has no regulated venue for yield-stripping/derivatives anywhere in its live stack — Mexico's CNBV resolution covers tokenized bonds, not synthetic yield instruments. AFSA's activity-based, tailor-fit licensing (Talgat even offered a bespoke "sovereign bond bank" license) is the cleanest path to authorize it [shared/partners/2026-05-25-aifc-talgat-amanbayev.md].

Activities #5 and #6 authorize exactly the multi-currency FX exchange that Etherfuse already sells as its "FX Solution" volume tier product [shared/master-knowledge-base.md; shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md] and that the institutional platform already ships as on-chain FX settlement [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md]. AIFC being *currency-free* — contracts denominated in any currency or asset [wiki/entities/companies/aifc.md] — makes it the natural regulated home for a Stablebond FX marketplace spanning the full roster.

**Why it matters:** the license work being tracked as a Kazakhstan setup task is really the regulatory foundation for the two product lines with the fattest margins (yield derivatives, FX at 5–20bps × institutional volume). Sequencing note: Talgat confirmed NBK approval comes first, then the AIFC SPC as a parallel 'sister' vehicle [shared/partners/2026-05-25-aifc-talgat-amanbayev.md] — so the phase-2 products are gated on finishing the NBK sandbox, making that the true critical-path item, not the SPC registration (already done June 10 [wiki/entities/companies/aifc.md — FIN-525]).
