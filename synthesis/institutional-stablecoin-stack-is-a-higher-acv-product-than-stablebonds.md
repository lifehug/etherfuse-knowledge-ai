# Etherfuse is quietly productizing a 'stand up your bank's stablecoin operation' offering

> In frontier-bank deals Etherfuse is bundling primary Circle Mint + Tether institutional mint access, MPC/HSM custody, and compliance alongside Stablebonds — a stablecoin-infrastructure-as-a-service product with far higher ACV than bond tokenization alone, and it should be packaged and named.

Two independent frontier engagements describe the same bundle that goes well beyond tokenizing bonds. The IBT (Tajikistan) concept brief proposes "standing up IBT's institutional stablecoin stack": institutional-grade MPC/HSM custody, "USDC via Circle Mint — direct institutional mint and redemption at 1:1 with no spread," and "USDT via Tether's institutional mint... a direct mint-and-redeem account" — explicitly to give IBT "primary access on the stablecoin with the deepest liquidity" in the Russia–Central Asia corridor [shared/partners/2026-05-30-etherfuse-ibt-concept-brief.md]. The Shinhan cover letter offers the same primitive to a top-tier Asian institution: "Circle (USDC) and Tether (USDT) Mint have direct banking infrastructure in Korea... which creates a liquidity bridge to send or receive KRW in a compliant way" [shared/partners/2026-06-05-shinhan-cover-letter.md]. The Pakistan PVARA brief lists the same lifecycle stack (custody, mint/redeem, compliance, distribution APIs) [shared/partners/2026-06-11-pakistan-pvara-etherfuse-introduction.md].

**The connection no page makes:** this is a distinct, repeatable product — *give a national/regional bank a turnkey, compliant stablecoin treasury (primary Circle+Tether mint + custody + AML + Stablebond yield) in one engagement* — and it's the natural anchor for institutional partners the company is optimizing to win. It carries higher ACV and stickiness than a single Stablebond listing, because it makes Etherfuse the bank's stablecoin operating system. It should be named, priced, and re-used across the entire Central Asia / frontier-bank pipeline (IBT, Freedom, Halyk, Uzbekistan, Pakistan) rather than re-derived per deal.
