# EUROB Is Already France Product-Market Fit — No Standalone OAT Needed for Market Entry

> France's crypto holders prefer EUR-denominated assets (63% of stablecoin holdings) and EUROB already wraps French OATs in a live EUR Stablebond custodied by a French entity, meaning France market entry is a distribution problem solvable through the existing Spiko relationship — not a product or regulatory problem requiring a new standalone OAT token.

## The Mismatch

The France market page proposes developing a dedicated **OAT** token (or BTF/DETTE variants) as the France market entry product [wiki/markets/France.md]. This frames France as needing new product development, new custody arrangements, and new regulatory engagement. But three facts from this same neighborhood converge to show the product already exists.

## Three Converging Facts

**1. EUROB already holds French paper.** The bond roadmap confirms France (Aa2/AA) is one of eight countries backing the live EUROB Stablebond [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. French sovereign debt is already tokenized and earning yield inside EUROB today.

**2. French crypto holders overwhelmingly prefer EUR.** A reported Kaiko study found 63% of French stablecoin holdings are EUR-denominated, vs. only 34% USDT/USDC combined [wiki/markets/France.md]. This is the inverse of nearly every other market Etherfuse serves. EUROB — denominated in EUR, backed by eurozone sovereigns — is already denominated in the currency French holders want.

**3. EUROB's custodian is French.** Spiko custodies EUROB and operates from France [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. The custody relationship, regulatory footprint, and product are all already aligned with the French market.

## Why This Matters for Speed

A standalone OAT token would require: new custody arrangements (potentially with Euroclear France), new AMF engagement for a single-country product, new naming/trademark assessment (the France page itself flags 'potential trademark or AFT branding constraints on using OAT as a ticker' [wiki/markets/France.md]), and cannibalization of EUROB volume. All of this is avoidable for initial market entry.

The faster path: distribute EUROB in France through Spiko's existing regulatory authorization, marketed with the France-specific demand signal — 61% of French citizens view access to state-backed savings as a citizenship right [wiki/markets/France.md]. A standalone OAT can come later as a premium single-country-exposure product once French distribution is proven.

## Revenue Implication

France is the eurozone's second-largest economy with €2.4T in outstanding sovereign debt [wiki/markets/France.md]. Even a modest capture of France's EUR stablecoin holdings (63% of an unknown but significant base) into EUROB would materially increase EUROB TVL — the highest-leverage path because it requires zero new product development.
