# The US window just opened on both axes — banking access and a <$100K product path

> The OCC/FDIC reputation-risk rule (effective June 9, 2026) removes the debanking risk that kept Etherfuse out of US banking, and Brogan's updated analysis shows a domestic '40 Act exempt fund can be stood up for under $100K with no SEC approval — together these collapse the two biggest barriers to the US HNW/qualified-purchaser market at the same moment.

Two CFO-legal sources that don't reference each other line up into a single timing signal. First, the OCC and FDIC issued a final rule 'Prohibition on the Use of Reputation Risk by Regulators,' effective June 9, 2026, that 'gives banks meaningful cover to serve industries they have been quietly avoiding for years' — directly reversing the Chokepoint-2.0 dynamic that froze crypto firms out of US bank accounts [teams/cfo/legal/us/2026-04-21-brogan-reputation-risk-rule.md]. Second, Brogan's March 2026 analysis upgrades the domestic product path from the prior vague '$5M+ registration' to a concrete exempt fund under the '40 Act (3(c)(7)/3(c)(1)) with 'setup cost <$100K,' 'no major legal barriers,' no SEC approval, and Ondo cited as a working precedent [teams/cfo/legal/united-states/brogan-law-domestic-fund-analysis-mar2026.md].

The non-obvious combination: the product structure was already cheap and well-established, but the practical blocker — getting a US bank to hold the fund's cash and serve a crypto-RWA issuer — was the unspoken risk. The reputation-risk rule removes exactly that blocker on a known date. Etherfuse's own demand evidence is domestic and waiting (Allocations' 20,000+ HNW investors and $100M potential deployment [teams/ceo/2026-04-21-allocations-partnership-opportunity.md]; the SME/treasury demand surfacing via system/Stripe). Dave already said 'we will want to do this' but left timing to a strategy session [teams/cfo/legal/united-states/brogan-law-domestic-fund-analysis-mar2026.md]; the June 9 rule is the argument for choosing 'now' over 'next quarter.'
