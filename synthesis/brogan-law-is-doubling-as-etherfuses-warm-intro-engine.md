# Etherfuse's US counsel is quietly a deal-referral channel

> Aaron Brogan is simultaneously Etherfuse's US securities counsel and an active source of warm intros to his other crypto clients — a low-cost, high-trust pipeline that isn't being treated as one.

Aaron Brogan (Brogan Law) authors Etherfuse's core US legal work — the '40 Act exempt-fund analysis, the Reg S/Reg D memo, and the OCC reputation-risk newsletter [teams/cfo/legal/united-states/brogan-law-domestic-fund-analysis-mar2026.md; teams/cfo/legal/us/2026-04-21-brogan-reputation-risk-rule.md]. But in the same neighborhood he also appears as a *connector*: he introduces **CRX** (Jake Schkolnick) to Dave noting 'you are both BL clients and it looks like you'll be at the upcoming Bitso conference in CDMX. Seems like your products have some overlaps' [shared/partners/crx.md], and **Borderless** (Kevin Lehtiniitty) is likewise a Brogan/BL-adjacent client now running a DDQ with Etherfuse and meeting at the same CDMX Bitso event [shared/partners/borderless-innovations.md; teams/cfo/2026-06-12-fin-472-borderless-ddq.md].

The pattern is that a single trusted, crypto-native law firm sits at the center of a cluster of fintech/stablecoin companies and is voluntarily cross-introducing them to Etherfuse around marquee events. That is a warm, pre-qualified inbound channel (counsel has already vetted both sides) that costs nothing to cultivate. The action: treat Brogan as a named referral partner — ask explicitly which other BL clients overlap with Etherfuse's non-USD yield and FX-rail products — rather than receiving these intros passively one at a time.
