# Bareksa is the ready-made INDSUN distributor hiding in AFTECH's membership

> Bareksa — an AFTECH member — already sells retail Indonesian government bonds (ORI/SBR, backed by the same SPN paper) to 5M+ users, making it the single best-fit launch distributor for the planned INDSUN Stablebond, a channel the Indonesia page (which names only IDRX) never mentions.

The Indonesia Stablebond is a tokenized wrapper around **SPN (Surat Perbendaharaan Negara)** — Indonesian treasury bills — targeted for launch via Standard Chartered custody, with [[idrx]] named as the primary local on/off-ramp partner [wiki/markets/Indonesia.md]. The bond roadmap lists it as INDSUN [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. But the market page's partner table is built entirely around a stablecoin peg (IDRX) and never identifies a *retail bond distributor* — the exact channel that made CETES work.

That distributor already exists inside AFTECH. AFTECH is the Indonesian fintech association with 400+ member companies, chaired by Pandu Sjahrir [wiki/entities/companies/aftech.md]. Among those members is **Bareksa**, explicitly described as "a key SBN retail distributor" with 5M+ users, and the government's own retail-bond distribution rail (partnered with the SBN Retail app and e-commerce channels) [shared/research/market-research-indonesia.md]. Bareksa already sells the underlying instrument — retail Indonesian government paper — to millions of Indonesians. INDSUN is simply the tokenized, 24/7, yield-bearing version of the product Bareksa's users already buy.

The demand gap is the same one Etherfuse exploited in Mexico. In Indonesia only ~1.1% of adults directly hold government bonds despite 6–8% sovereign yields versus 2–4% bank deposits [shared/research/market-research-indonesia.md] — structurally identical to Mexico's $623B bond market with only 2% retail ownership that Etherfuse built CETES to attack [shared/partners/canton-mexico-infrastructure.md]. The 200M+ digital wallet users across AFTECH members (GoPay, OVO, Dana, ShopeePay) plus Bareksa's investing base [shared/research/market-research-indonesia.md] are the distribution to close that gap.

**Action:** Route the first Indonesia BD motion to Bareksa (and its investment-platform peers Bibit/Stockbit, also AFTECH members) as INDSUN retail distributors, alongside IDRX for the peg/ramp — an approach that pairs a proven government-bond audience with a ready wallet base rather than relying on a single stablecoin partner.
