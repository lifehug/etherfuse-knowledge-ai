# Akbank's primary-dealer status is the Turkey market-entry unlock, not just a prospect

> Akbank being a Turkish government-bond primary dealer maps it onto the exact local-partner playbook (Halyk/Shinhan/BBVA/IBT) that both removes Turkey's stalled BBVA-Spain custody dependency and reliably converts into a 'tokenize your own bond placements' upsell — yet the page lists zero contacts and no deal.

**The overlooked fact.** Akbank is reported as a primary dealer in Turkey's government bond market [shared/research/raw/2026-07-07-market-research-turkey-websearch.md], and separately runs Akbank LAB with hands-on blockchain trade-finance and tokenization pilots [wiki/entities/companies/akbank.md]. The Akbank page files it as a generic 'strong prospective target' with no contacts and no active deal [wiki/entities/companies/akbank.md].

**Why this is the market-entry unlock.** The Turkey market page currently pins custody of Turkish sovereign bonds on the BBVA Spain depository account — which is itself contingent on Etherfuse first obtaining a Spanish non-resident tax ID (an item flagged as in tension/unresolved) [wiki/markets/turkey.md]. That is a fragile, multi-hop dependency for a whole market. But Etherfuse's proven entry pattern is a *local* primary-dealer/broker who sources and custodies the paper directly: Halyk Finance's six-element framework (segregated nominee account, secondary-market purchases only, monthly attestation) is the template [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-framework.md], and the IBT concept brief explicitly leans on IBT's primary-dealer + FX-bank + custody stack as the thing that makes a frontier Stablebond viable [shared/partners/2026-05-30-etherfuse-ibt-concept-brief.md]. A Turkish primary dealer with an innovation lab is precisely that local leg — collapsing the Spain-tax-ID dependency for a TRY Stablebond wrapping short-duration Turkish sovereign debt (already named on the roadmap) [wiki/markets/turkey.md].

**The repeatable upsell the page misses.** Every time Etherfuse lands a local bond-market institution, that partner asks to tokenize its *own* issuances: Halyk Finance (50% share of KZ bond placements) said the self-issuance angle was 'really very interesting,' and Etherfuse cited BBVA and Shinhan as following the same 'started with one product → tokenize all their assets' arc [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-meeting.md]. Akbank (~$80B assets, primary-dealer relationships, tokenization mandate) is the same profile — making it not just a distribution/custody partner but a potential serial issuer of tokenized Turkish bank/corporate paper.

**Also answers NIC's objection at the same time.** A local direct-access partner is exactly the model Dave defended to NIC NBK against the multi-bank counterparty-concentration critique — highly liquid local desks, T+1 for any size, local rates vs. ETF packaging [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. Akbank fits that argument in Turkey.
