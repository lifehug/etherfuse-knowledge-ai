# NIC NBK's counterparty-risk Q&A is ready ammunition for Avalanche's stalled risk review

> Helio Inaba's unresolved risk assessment is the single blocker on the Avalanche deal, and the NIC NBK meeting two months later produced exactly the counterparty-risk Q&A, cost-comparison framework, and institutional-treasury pitch that a foundation risk lead would demand — meaning the unblocking material already exists but has never been sent to Matt Schmenk.

## The blocker

The Avalanche deal is gated on one person: Helio Inaba, the risk lead, who is conducting an internal risk assessment that has been pending since the March 24, 2026 product walkthrough [wiki/entities/companies/avalanche.md]. No follow-up materials addressing risk concerns are documented.

## The ready-made answer

Two months later (May 26, 2026), NIC NBK — a sophisticated institutional allocator managing National Bank of Kazakhstan capital — raised *exactly* the objections a foundation risk lead would raise [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]:

- **Counterparty concentration risk:** NIC questioned whether Etherfuse's single-counterparty model (BBVA in Mexico) creates concentration risk vs. traditional multi-bank setups. Dave's prepared response: direct local access means tapping highly liquid local desks, T+1 settlement for any size, local rates vs. ETF packaging (ETF = ~4.5–5% yield, 20 bps bid/ask; Etherfuse = ~6.2% with 50 bps fee) [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md].

- **Cost comparison vs. institutional alternatives:** Goldman Sachs route gives packaged product, lower yield, 35% Mexican corporate tax obligation. Etherfuse via offshore vehicle = international investor wrapper, tax-deferred, direct CETES, cheaper [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md].

- **Legal structure / bankruptcy remoteness:** Dual-layer protection — AIFC English-law ring-fence + Swiss DLT registration — is documented and field-tested in institutional conversations [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. The full bankruptcy remoteness communication guide, including the "What NOT to Say" matrix, is already written [shared/bd/bankruptcy-remoteness-communication-guide.md].

- **Idle USD treasury pitch:** Dave told NIC to hold pre-deployment USD in USTRY (same as BNY exposure they'd have anyway, but tokenized and yielding ~3.2%) [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]. This is precisely the pitch for Matias (Avalanche treasury manager) — park foundation treasury in USTRY while evaluating the broader chain deployment.
