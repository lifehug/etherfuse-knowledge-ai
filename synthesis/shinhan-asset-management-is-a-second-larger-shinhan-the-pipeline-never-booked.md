# Shinhan Asset Management is a second, far larger Shinhan the pipeline never booked

> The June pipeline carries one 'Shinhan' line at $50M TVL / $3.5M revenue, but that is the Securities/KRWQ relationship — the newly-opened Shinhan Asset Management entity is a structurally separate counterparty carrying a ~$100B derivatives-collateral TAM plus two live corporate FX-settlement deals (Olive Young $2B/day, HD Group) that appear nowhere in the valuation.

## Two Shinhans, one pipeline line

The June 2026 valuation snapshot pins 'Shinhan' at #14 — Negotiation, $50.0M TVL, $3.5M annual revenue [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md]. The master KB and collateral playbook make clear that line is **Shinhan Securities**, the FSC-licensed arm behind KRWQ/KTB [shared/master-knowledge-base.md][shared/bd/collateral-playbook-ktb-krwq.md].

The June 4 C-level meeting opened a **different legal entity**: Shinhan Asset Management, Korea's second-largest asset manager, building a 100%-owned, fully-regulated digital-asset subsidiary with verbal FSC/FSS non-action approval to tokenize Korean MMFs/ETFs for cross-border use [shared/partners/2026-06-04-shinhan-asset-management-clevel.md]. The doc itself flags the separation explicitly: *'distinct entity from Shinhan Securities… CEO and President of Shinhan Group are personally aware'* — a governance upgrade over the Securities relationship where 'senior leadership was uninformed.' None of this is reflected in the $50M/$3.5M booking.

## The three uncounted revenue legs

1. **Derivatives collateral — ~$100B addressable.** $180B in stablecoins left Korea in 2025 to reach offshore derivatives venues; after Shinhan AM's own 20% share / 50% haircut, ≥$100B is leaving specifically for derivatives collateral. Their plan: tokenize Korean MMFs/ETFs as FX-compliant collateral so Koreans stop exporting stablecoins — with Etherfuse as the public-chain tokenization layer [shared/partners/2026-06-04-shinhan-asset-management-clevel.md].
2. **Olive Young (CJ Group) — $2B daily payments across 180 countries.** Heyryung Choi (who personally founded Olive Young) proposed wiring the MMF token to an Olive Young payment rail for FX settlement across 180 currencies [shared/partners/2026-06-04-shinhan-asset-management-clevel.md].
3. **HD Group bunkering — global ship-fuel FX settlement** for Korea's #1 shipbuilder, in active conversation [shared/partners/2026-06-04-shinhan-asset-management-clevel.md].

Legs 2 and 3 are FX-volume plays that map directly onto Etherfuse's published volume-based FX fee schedule (5–20 bps), not the TVL fee model the pipeline used to size 'Shinhan' [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. At Olive Young's $2B/day even a 5 bps slice is an order of magnitude beyond the entire $3.5M booked.

The unlock Heyryung named is something Etherfuse already ships: *'I can report to the government tomorrow that we picked up Etherfuse because they have this kind of information'* — referring to the FX/tax/transaction reporting stack [shared/partners/2026-06-04-shinhan-asset-management-clevel.md], the exact modules detailed in the compliance deck delivered the next day [shared/partners/2026-06-05-shinhan-compliance-presentation.md]. **Action:** split 'Shinhan' into two pipeline lines, re-size Shinhan AM on FX volume (Olive Young + HD Group) plus the derivatives-collateral TVL, and treat it — not KRWQ — as the larger Korea bet. This is the same understatement pattern already caught for AirTM.
