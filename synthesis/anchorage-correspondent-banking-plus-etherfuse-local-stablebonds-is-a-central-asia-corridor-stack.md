# Anchorage's non-US-bank correspondent product + Etherfuse local Stablebonds = a ready corridor stack for the KZ/Tajik pipeline

> Anchorage launched stablecoin correspondent banking that lets non-US banks settle dollars instantly without SWIFT/Fed — exactly the pain point Etherfuse's already-warm Central Asian bank relationships (Freedom, IBT, Halyk) are stuck on — so pairing Anchorage's USD leg with Etherfuse's local-currency Stablebond leg turns two separate pitches into one full corridor.

Anchorage 'announced stablecoin correspondent banking solutions for non-US banks as a SWIFT/Fed alternative, enabling instant dollar settlement (announced 2026-02-19)' [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md], corroborated in the ecosystem note: 'Non-US banks can settle dollar transactions instantly without Fed access or SWIFT' [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md].

That is the precise problem gating Etherfuse's Central Asian bank pipeline. Freedom Bank 'currently uses SWIFT/correspondent banking for cross-border (e.g., China flows take 1 week for $100 transfer)' [shared/partners/2026-05-21-freedom-bank-aidos-zhumagulov.md]. International Bank of Tajikistan sits on remittances equal to '45% of GDP ($5.8B/year)' routed largely through slow formal banking channels [shared/partners/2026-05-26-international-bank-of-tajikistan.md]. Halyk is the confirmed KZ custodian/broker but still an open question on 'custody path through correspondent network' for foreign sovereign bonds [shared/partners/2026-05-21-kazakhstan-partnerships.md].

The non-obvious synthesis: these banks need two things Etherfuse currently pitches piecemeal — (1) a fast USD settlement rail, and (2) a yield-bearing local-currency instrument. Anchorage supplies (1) as an OCC-chartered bank; Etherfuse supplies (2) via KZT/local Stablebonds and its ramp infrastructure ('~7 basis points vs. 1.5–4% for centralized exchanges' [shared/partners/2026-05-26-international-bank-of-tajikistan.md]). Presented together, Anchorage's dollar correspondent leg + Etherfuse's local-currency leg is a complete corridor product for banks where Etherfuse already has in-person, warm relationships.

**Action:** With the Anchorage in-person meeting already scheduled at the CDMX Stablecoin conference (June 2026) [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital.md], add a second agenda item beyond CETES custody: co-selling Anchorage correspondent banking + Etherfuse Stablebonds into the KZ/Tajik/Freedom relationships. Low incremental cost — both sides are already at the table — and it upgrades Anchorage from a custody customer to a distribution co-seller in markets no US bank currently serves.
