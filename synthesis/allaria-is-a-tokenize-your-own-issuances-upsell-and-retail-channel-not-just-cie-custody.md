# Allaria is an issuance-and-distribution partner, not just CIE custody

> Allaria's page scopes it narrowly as the ALyC that will custody Argentine sovereign bonds for the CIE account, but as one of Argentina's largest broker-dealers with a consumer fintech arm (Alma Fintech), it maps onto both of Etherfuse's proven local-partner patterns — the 'tokenize your own placements' upsell and B2B2C retail distribution — that the page never mentions.

## What the page sees

The Allaria wiki treats the firm as a single-purpose custody counterpart: the ALyC selected to open Etherfuse's CIE (Cuenta de Inversión Extranjera) investment account so Argentine sovereign bonds can back the Argentina Stablebond, with Alma Fintech framed merely as a 'primary contact point' [wiki/entities/companies/allaria.md]. That undersells a partner that is described in the same page as *'one of Argentina's largest ALyC broker-dealers'* with its own *fintech arm* [wiki/entities/companies/allaria.md].

## Upsell #1 — tokenize Allaria's own placements

Etherfuse has now hit the same pattern with three different local partners: a bank/broker starts by custodying paper for one Etherfuse product, then asks Etherfuse to tokenize *its own* issuances. Halyk Finance — which places ~50% of all locally-placed bonds in Kazakhstan — did exactly this, with Roman Assilbekov calling the 'tokenize their own bond issuances' idea *'really very interesting,'* and the Etherfuse team citing BBVA and Shinhan as the same trajectory: *'started with one Mexican product → asked Etherfuse to tokenize all their assets'* [shared/partners/2026-05-21-halyk-finance-meeting.md]. A large Argentine ALyC that underwrites and places corporate and sovereign paper is the identical setup — the custody relationship being opened now is the wedge, and the recurring revenue is tokenizing Allaria's placement book. Etherfuse's positioning as *'the only regulated issuer in Brazil, Korea, and Mexico,'* with a moat built on *'physical presence, local bank partnerships, regulatory licensing'* [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md], is precisely what makes this upsell defensible in Argentina.

## Upsell #2 — Alma Fintech as a B2B2C distribution channel

Alma Fintech is a consumer fintech, not just a contact inbox [wiki/entities/companies/allaria.md]. That makes it a distribution surface for the exact B2B2C model Etherfuse already ships: the digital-wallet case study shows a partner integrating Stablebonds as a yield-bearing savings feature and earning 10–60 bps revenue share on distributed AUM [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md]. An Argentine peso Stablebond backed by Argentine sovereign paper would sit in the top revenue tier (>10% yield → 60 bps partner share, plus the sovereign-coin TVL tiers) [shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md, shared/master-knowledge-base.md] — Argentina is a natural high-yield analog to TESOURO (~13%). So the same relationship that opens the CIE account can, on Etherfuse's own precedent, convert into a distribution deal where Alma Fintech puts the Argentina Stablebond in front of retail and shares yield.

## Why it matters now

The engagement is live but idle — 'awaiting Allaria's account-opening and CIE requirements' with no reply yet as of July 8 [wiki/entities/companies/allaria.md]. Framing the re-engagement around all three legs (custody + issuance tokenization + Alma Fintech distribution) rather than a one-off custody ask both raises the deal's strategic value to Allaria (giving it a reason to prioritize the reply) and multiplies Etherfuse's revenue from a single Argentine relationship. The fallback ALyCs (Puente, Marica, AdCap, Balanz) are custody-only substitutes [wiki/entities/companies/allaria.md]; Allaria's fintech arm is the differentiator that makes it worth pushing before switching.

_Confidence is medium: the page confirms Allaria's scale and the Alma Fintech arm, but Allaria's specific bond-placement volume and Alma Fintech's retail user base are not documented in these sources and should be confirmed on the next call._
