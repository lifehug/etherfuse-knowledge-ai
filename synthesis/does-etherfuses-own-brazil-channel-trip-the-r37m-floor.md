# Flag: does Etherfuse's own Brazil channel trip the R$37M VASP floor?

> The same VASP capital framework ABCripto warns will consolidate Brazil governs the VASP-channel routing (Resolutions 519–521) that Etherfuse relies on to stay outside Resolution 561 — yet no source confirms whether Etherfuse's live TESOURO operation and in-flight Bloque/PIX onramp are themselves exposed to the R$37M floor.

Two sources describe Brazil regulation that touches Etherfuse directly but never reconcile. First, Etherfuse's defense against Resolution 561 is explicitly that its routing runs through the VASP/SPSAV channel under Resolutions 519–521, 'outside Resolution 561's direct scope' [shared/concepts/brazil-efx-resolution-561.md]. Second, ABCripto's warning is about a *different* lever on that same channel — the new VASP licensing framework's R$37 million minimum capital requirement, which it says is calibrated for large exchanges and will push smaller operators out [wiki/entities/companies/abcripto.md].

Etherfuse is not a bystander in Brazil: TESOURO is live with B3 custody [shared/bond-roadmap.md], a Bloque onboarding is actively building a Brazil PIX onramp at '20bps + local taxes' [shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bloque-etherfuse-onboarding-kickoff.md], and Maimone's clients plan to distribute Stablebonds into Brazil via Anchor [shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md]. None of these sources state whether the entity carrying Etherfuse's Brazilian VASP-channel activity must meet the R$37M floor, or whether the live structure (token issuance domiciled in Mexico, bonds in B3 custody) sidesteps it the way it sidesteps 561.

Decision-useful action: this is a compliance/legal escalation, not a synthesis conclusion — Etherfuse should confirm with Brazilian counsel (the Maimone relationship is already open) whether its 519–521 routing is subject to the incoming capital floor before scaling Bloque/Brazil onramp volume, because the downside (a forced R$37M capitalization or channel re-architecture) lands on revenue paths already in flight.
