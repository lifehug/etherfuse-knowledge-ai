# Monad's Raj Parekh is functioning as a stablecoin-deal intro engine, not just a chain partner

> The Abound relationship — one of the most strategically compelling entries in the pipeline — originated entirely from Raj Parekh, Monad Foundation's Head of Stablecoins & Payments, revealing that Etherfuse's Monad grant relationship doubles as a warm-intro channel into stablecoin partners that should be worked systematically.

The entire Abound opportunity traces to a single person: 'Intro to Nishkaam facilitated by Raj Parekh, Head of Stablecoins & Payments at Monad Foundation' [SALES-424], and the wiki confirms the relationship 'sprang from' that intro [wiki/entities/companies/abound-times-of-india.md]. Monad is not just a deployment chain for Etherfuse — Stablebonds are live on Monad [wiki/concepts/stablebonds.md] and Monad infrastructure is also being used to design the USD→USDC→KTB Korea settlement flow [wiki/entities/companies/Etherfuse.md, SALES-278]. 

The pattern: a foundation-grant/integration relationship is producing high-value BD introductions (Abound: 500K users, ~20% of India's USDC off-ramp, $130B remittance corridor) through a named, role-specific contact whose job is literally stablecoins and payments [SALES-424][shared/meetings/2026-04-27-abound-times-of-india-inr-stablecoin.md]. That is a repeatable, near-zero-cost channel — Raj Parekh sits across Monad's stablecoin/payments deal flow and has already shown willingness to broker. Etherfuse should treat Monad Foundation's stablecoin team as a standing intro pipeline (ask Raj for the rest of his stablecoin-issuer rolodex), the same way it should treat its securities counsel's client base — not wait for one-off referrals.
