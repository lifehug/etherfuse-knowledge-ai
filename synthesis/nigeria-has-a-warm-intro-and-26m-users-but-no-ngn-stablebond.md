# Nigeria: stacked demand, a warm CNGN intro, no NGN Stablebond

> Nigeria shows the same 'demand-ahead-of-roadmap' pattern as Colombia/Argentina/Bolivia — an already-integrating partner, a volunteered intro to the national stablecoin issuer, and 26M existing stablecoin users — yet no NGN Stablebond appears anywhere on Etherfuse's bond roadmap.

Three independent signals in this neighborhood point at Nigeria as an unworked easy win:

1. **An already-integrating, Nigeria-focused partner.** bmoni is mid-KYB, routing businesses through Etherfuse's RAMP widget to top up b-money wallets with MXNe and CETES, and explicitly operates 'the Nigerian stablecoin side' of its business [shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bmoni-etherfuse-kyb-business-onboarding.md]. This is a live onboarding relationship, not a cold prospect.

2. **A volunteered warm intro to the national stablecoin issuer.** In the same meeting, bmoni's Natasha committed to introduce Dave to the **CNGN** (Nigerian stablecoin) team 'for a parallel integration conversation' [shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bmoni-etherfuse-kyb-business-onboarding.md]. CNGN is exactly the kind of local-currency issuer that would want an NGN sovereign-yield leg — the AirTM/Alfred 'partner wants to issue a local coin' pattern.

3. **Proven adoption scale.** Etherfuse's own Freedom case study cites that 'Nigeria already has 26 million stablecoin users (12% of its population),' offered as evidence the emerging-market adoption curve is accelerating [shared/partners/freedom-etherfuse-case-study.md] — i.e., the distribution problem is already solved by someone else.

**The gap:** NGN is absent from the full Q3–Q4 2026 Stablebond roadmap [shared/bond-roadmap.md], and the master-KB expansion pipeline lists Ghana (GHS) and Kenya (KES) for Africa but not Nigeria [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. So Etherfuse is penciling smaller African markets while the continent's largest stablecoin market — where it has both a live integrating partner and a free intro to the local issuer — has no product slated.

This is the same blind spot already flagged for Colombia, Argentina, and Bolivia, but with arguably the strongest combination of warm-channel + scale evidence. Action: add NGN (T-bill) to the roadmap and take the CNGN intro bmoni is offering, positioning an NGN Stablebond as CNGN's yield/reserve leg rather than a competing coin.
