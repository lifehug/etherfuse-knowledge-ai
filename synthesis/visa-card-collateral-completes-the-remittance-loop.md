# Visa card collateral is the missing piece that makes remittance Stablebonds spendable

> Etherfuse already has a documented, replicable playbook for local-currency-token-collateralized Visa cards — the exact mechanism that turns the 'hold + earn yield' remittance pitch in the IBT, Abound, and Uzbekistan deals into a 'hold + earn yield + spend' product, yet none of those deal docs reference it.

The Visa local-issuer playbook spells out a complete, bank-grade mechanic that is being treated as a Mexico-only artifact: a local card issuer funds a cardholder wallet with a yield-bearing local Stablebond token, the collateral accrues sovereign yield while idle, and domestic taps convert 1:1 off Visa rails so 'we are not passing stablecoin, we are passing a transaction with [fiat]' — explicitly stated as 'replicable in any market where Etherfuse has a tokenized local treasury asset' [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md].

The frontier remittance deals describe wanting exactly this product but never connect it to the existing spec:
- The IBT (Tajikistan) concept brief proposes that a worker abroad sends value over stablecoin rails, 'the recipient holds it in IBT's wallet, can earn Stablebond yield on idle balances, and spends via IBT's Visa/UnionPay cards' — and notes IBT is the country's 'leading Visa issuer + UnionPay issuer/acquirer' with the IBT24 wallet [shared/partners/2026-05-30-etherfuse-ibt-concept-brief.md, shared/partners/2026-05-30-tajikistan-ibt-stablebond-analysis.md]. IBT is the textbook 'local card issuer holding a BIN' from the playbook.
- Abound brings 500K+ users on UPI rails into the $130B India remittance market [shared/meetings/2026-04-27-abound-times-of-india-inr-stablecoin.md]; a planned INDTB makes it a Visa-collateral candidate the moment the bond is live.
- Uzbekistan has $16.1B/yr remittances (18% of GDP) and, as of Jan 1 2026, legally recognizes stablecoins as a payment method [shared/partners/2026-06-05-uzbekistan-government-overview.md] — removing the regulatory blocker to a spendable yield-bearing wallet.

The synthesis: the recurring weakness in remittance pitches is that a yield-bearing balance is inert until it can be spent. The Visa collateral model — already built, already proven in Mexico (CETES), already documented as turnkey for any Stablebond market — is the spec that closes the loop. It should be bundled by name into every remittance-corridor proposal (IBT, Abound, Uzbekistan, and the GCC roadmap), converting 'savings token' deals into 'spendable account' deals that win the highest-value use case in each country.
