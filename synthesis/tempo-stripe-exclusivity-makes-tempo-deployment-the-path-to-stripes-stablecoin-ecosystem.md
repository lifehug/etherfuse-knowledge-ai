# Tempo's Stripe exclusivity makes Tempo the path to Stripe's stablecoin ecosystem, not cold outreach

> The Stripe research page positions Stripe as a cold outreach target, but Tempo's Ravi Patel reveals Stripe has already 'ripped and replaced' its core stablecoin infrastructure to run exclusively on Tempo — meaning the path to becoming the yield layer for Stripe's $4.2B Treasury deposits and 100-country stablecoin card rollout is deploying on Tempo, where Ravi already sees Etherfuse as the only non-USD yield player.

Two sources in this neighborhood tell contradictory stories about how to reach Stripe:

**The cold-outreach story (Stripe research):** Stripe acquired Bridge for $1.1B, has $4.2B in Treasury deposits, and is expanding stablecoin cards to 100+ countries — but has "no local-currency yield products for non-USD markets." The research page lists two cold contacts (Amy Roberti, René Salazar Palafox) with outreach sent March 27, 2026 [shared/partners/stripe-research.md].

**The warm-path reality (Tempo transcript):** Ravi Patel (Tempo's FX/ramps lead, ex-Conduit) said on June 22, 2026: *"Pretty much everything [Stripe is] doing now is going to be exclusively on Tempo… they've already ripped and replaced a bunch of their core stablecoin offerings to be exclusively on Tempo."* Ravi added that Tempo is "building yield venues," has a "tradfi team" very close to BBVA, and explicitly called Etherfuse "the only player delivering yield on non-USD stables" [shared/partners/tempo.md].

**Why this matters:** If Stripe's stablecoin infrastructure is migrating to Tempo, then deploying Stablebonds on Tempo and building the yield venues Ravi's team is asking for is the fastest path to becoming Stripe's non-USD yield layer. The cold outreach to Stripe directly bypasses the chain Stripe has already chosen. Tempo also has a concrete lead — a "super large enterprise" wanting COP contractor payments — that aligns with the BBVA/COP Stablebond Etherfuse is building next quarter [shared/partners/tempo.md].

**Agreed next steps already exist:** Tempo is sending a Slack invite, both sides are setting up mutual NDA, and the COP marketplace is the lead use case [shared/partners/tempo.md]. The gap is that this isn't being prioritized as a Stripe-proxy opportunity — it's sitting at the Tempo relationship level.
