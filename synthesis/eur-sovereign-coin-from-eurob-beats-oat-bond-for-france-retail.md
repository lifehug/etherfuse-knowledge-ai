# A EUR Sovereign Coin on EUROB is the faster France retail wedge than an OAT bond

> France's stablecoin demand is 63% EUR-denominated and its retail culture treats state yield as a civic right, so the fastest entry is a EURe Sovereign Coin built on the EUROB collateral Etherfuse already ships — the MXNe model — not the longer-duration OAT bond token the AFT/OAT pages propose.

The OAT and AFT pages assume the France product is a bond token named after the French Treasury instrument [wiki/concepts/oat.md] [wiki/entities/companies/agence-france-tresor.md]. But the France market data points to a different, faster wedge. French stablecoin holdings are **63% EUR-denominated** (EURS/EURT), with USDT/USDC only 34% [wiki/markets/france.md], and 61% of French citizens view access to state-backed savings as a citizenship right — mapping directly onto Etherfuse's "government yield as a right" framing and the Marianne branding [wiki/markets/france.md].

Etherfuse already owns the collateral: EUROB is a live EUR sovereign T-bill token spanning eight eurozone issuers including France [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. The proven Sovereign Coin pattern — a fixed-value balance token collateralized by a Stablebond, with yield accruing to the holder — is exactly MXNe on CETES [wiki/entities/companies/Etherfuse.md] [shared/partners/tala.md]. Applying that to EUROB yields a **EURe**: a 1-EUR balance stablecoin capturing eurozone yield, dropping straight into the EUR-stablecoin demand that already dominates France, requiring no new custodian (Spiko is live) and no AFT relationship. This makes the single-country OAT bond token a later, institutional/duration play, while the retail launch reuses assets Etherfuse ships today — the same 'reuse-the-collateral, don't-rebuild' logic already applied to MXN partners, now pointed at the EUR corridor.
