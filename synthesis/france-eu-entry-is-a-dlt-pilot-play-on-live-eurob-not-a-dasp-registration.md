# France's real EU unlock is the DLT Pilot Regime on already-live EUROB, not DASP/MiCA

> The AMF/DASP pages frame France entry as a crypto-services registration that passports MiCA across the EU, but Etherfuse's Stablebonds are tokenized sovereign securities — so the correct pathway is the DLT Pilot Regime, which France dominates (4 of 6 EU-authorized pilots) and where Etherfuse already tokenizes French government debt through live EUROB.

## The misframing

Both the AMF and DASP wiki pages treat France entry as a single funnel: register as a Digital Asset Service Provider with the AMF, which then unlocks MiCA passporting across all 27 EU markets [wiki/entities/companies/amf-france.md, wiki/concepts/DASP.md]. That is the correct path for a *crypto-asset service* — but it is likely the wrong path for Etherfuse's core product.

The MiCA concept page itself flags the unresolved question: "How would a tokenized OAT be classified under MiCA — as an ART, an EMT, or under the DLT Pilot Regime? This classification determines the licensing path and reserve requirements" [wiki/concepts/MiCA.md]. A tokenized French treasury bill is a claim on sovereign debt — a security — not a crypto-asset service or a fiat-referenced token. In Mexico, Etherfuse spent a two-year CNBV dialogue precisely to get Stablebonds classified as *not securities* under Mexican law [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]; that determination does not travel to the EU, where tokenized bonds sit in the securities/DLT-Pilot bucket, not the MiCA-DASP bucket.

## Why this makes France doubly strategic

The AMF page notes, almost in passing, that "France also hosts 4 of the 6 EU-authorised DLT market-infrastructure pilots under the DLT Pilot Regime" [wiki/entities/companies/amf-france.md]. That is the buried headline. If Stablebonds are securities, the regulated home for issuing/settling them on-chain in the EU is the DLT Pilot Regime — and France is where two-thirds of that entire regime's live infrastructure sits. France is not a generic EU beachhead; it is the specific jurisdiction where the *correct* regulatory rail is most developed.

## Etherfuse is already a French sovereign-debt issuer

The strategic-context section treats France as a greenfield prospect to be navigated. But Etherfuse already tokenizes French government paper today: the bond roadmap lists "Treasury Bill / EUR / France / Aa2 AA / EUROB / **Live**" [shared/bond-roadmap.md], and EUROB is backed by eight eurozone sovereigns explicitly including France [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. Etherfuse is not asking the AMF for permission to enter a market it has never touched — it is asking the regulator of a country whose sovereign bonds it already holds and tokenizes. That reframes the pre-application posture from cold outreach to "we already run a live product on your government's debt."

## The bifurcation the pages miss

France uniquely serves *both* of Etherfuse's EU regulatory needs at once, which no single page connects:

- **Stablebonds (securities)** → DLT Pilot Regime, where France leads 4/6 pilots [wiki/entities/companies/amf-france.md].
- **MXNe / Sovereign Coins (fiat-referenced stablecoins)** → likely EMT under MiCA, for which the AMF is France's competent authority and DASP registration is the on-ramp [wiki/concepts/MiCA.md, wiki/concepts/DASP.md].

So the AMF relationship is not one path but two parallel authorizations, and France is the rare jurisdiction advanced on both simultaneously.

## Decision-useful takeaway

Before chasing DASP registration as the EU unlock, get the classification question answered directly with the AMF's fintech sandbox: is a tokenized French T-bill a DLT-Pilot instrument or a MiCA instrument? The AMF page lists exactly this sandbox ("FinTech, Innovation and Competitiveness division") and the *Loi industrie verte* digital-asset sandbox as pre-application channels [wiki/entities/companies/amf-france.md]. Framing the first conversation as "we already issue a live EUROB backed by French OATs — which regime applies?" is faster and more credible than a generic DASP application, and it routes Etherfuse toward the DLT Pilot Regime France actually leads.
