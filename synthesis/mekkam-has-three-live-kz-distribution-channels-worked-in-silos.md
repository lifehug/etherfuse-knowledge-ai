# One MEKKAM product, three Kazakhstan distribution channels — sequence them, don't pick one

> The single planned MEKKAM Stablebond already has three distinct, confirmed-or-warm Kazakhstan distribution channels — Freedom's 7M-user Super App, AIX's TabaDo retail exchange, and KASE's digital-asset venue — each reaching a different segment, yet they are being worked as unrelated threads.

The Kazakhstan sources, read together, show Etherfuse has accidentally assembled a complete three-layer distribution stack for one product (the tokenized MEKKAM treasury bill), but no page treats it as a stack:

- **Mass-market super-app:** Freedom Bank's Super App — 7M+ customers, ~10,000 downloads/day — is a confirmed distribution partner for MEKKAM, modeling 10–50% adoption scenarios worth $36M–$180M in yield generated [shared/partners/freedom-etherfuse-case-study.md; shared/partners/2026-05-21-kazakhstan-partnerships.md].
- **TradFi retail exchange:** AIX offers conventional retail access from $1 and age 16 via TabaDo [wiki/entities/companies/aix.md] — reaching self-directed retail investors who don't use the Freedom app.
- **Digital-asset exchange:** KASE explicitly welcomed Etherfuse as a launch partner for its June-2026 Solana-native venue [shared/partners/2026-05-22-kase-digital-assets.md] — reaching crypto-native participants and providing on-chain composability.

These segments barely overlap: a Freedom banking customer, a TabaDo retail trader, and a KASE crypto user are three different people. Treating the channels as competing (e.g., the existing "KASE faster than AIX" framing [kase-wants-etherfuse-as-a-launch-partner-on-solana-in-june]) leaves two of three segments unserved.

**Decision:** Sequence a layered launch off a single MEKKAM issuance — Freedom Super App for reach and balance capture, KASE for the regulated-exchange / DeFi-composability credential, AIX/TabaDo for the TradFi retail tail. All three depend on the same upstream gate (the NBK letter for the Meka launch, expected June 2026 [shared/meetings/2026-05-26-nic-nbk.md]), so the distribution side can be pre-wired in parallel rather than worked deal-by-deal. AIX in particular sits at zero commercial engagement despite the warm AIFC-meeting introduction [wiki/entities/companies/aix.md].
