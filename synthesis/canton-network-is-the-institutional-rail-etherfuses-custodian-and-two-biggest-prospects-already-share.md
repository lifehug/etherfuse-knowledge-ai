# Canton is the shared institutional rail Etherfuse's own custodian already sits on

> Etherfuse's GILTS custodian (Archax), its landmark TradFi prospect (DTCC), and its largest Korean institutional prospect (Shinhan Asset Management) are all live on Digital Asset's Canton Network — and Etherfuse already claims Canton mint/burn capability — making Canton the single highest-leverage chain to prioritize even though it appears nowhere on the official five-chain roster.

Three independent, high-value relationships in this neighborhood have converged on the same permissioned settlement layer, yet Etherfuse's published network list (Stellar, Base, Polygon, Solana, Monad) omits it entirely [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

- **Archax** — Etherfuse's own GILTS custodian — went live on Digital Asset's Canton Network in January 2026 and completed the UK's first public-blockchain tokenised-deposit Gilt purchase with Lloyds on Canton [wiki/entities/companies/archax.md].
- **DTCC** — the largest TradFi infrastructure play in tokenization ($100T custodied) — is tokenizing DTC-custodied U.S. Treasuries on Canton with a July 2026 pilot and October 2026 commercial launch, alongside BlackRock, Goldman, JPMorgan, Anchorage, and Circle [shared/partners/2026-06-02-dtcc.md].
- **Shinhan Asset Management** — Korea's #2 asset manager, building a ~$100B derivatives-collateral tokenization business with Etherfuse — became the first Canton super validator in Korea, and explicitly framed its regulatory sequencing as "Canton (permissioned) → Etherfuse (public chain)" [shared/partners/2026-06-04-shinhan-asset-management-clevel.md].

Crucially, Etherfuse has already told Shinhan it "can mint and burn Stablebond assets directly on Canton," positioning that capability as the bridge between Shinhan's permissioned environment and Etherfuse's public-chain infrastructure [shared/partners/2026-06-05-shinhan-cover-letter.md]. That means the capability is not hypothetical.

**Why this matters:** Canton is quietly emerging as the institutional interoperability layer where the biggest sovereign-bond and MMF tokenizers already meet. Etherfuse's GILTS collateral is custodied by a firm (Archax) already operating there; DTCC's Canton→Stellar roadmap [shared/partners/2026-06-02-dtcc.md] overlaps directly with Etherfuse's existing Stellar deployment; and Shinhan's regulator-approved go-live is gated on the Canton step. Formally adding Canton to the Stablebond roster — and marketing GILTS as Canton-native via Archax — would simultaneously unblock the Shinhan launch, create DTCC collateral compatibility, and let Etherfuse's already-live UK product ride its own custodian's newest rail. This is a chain-prioritization decision that three separate deals are independently pulling toward, invisible on any single page.
