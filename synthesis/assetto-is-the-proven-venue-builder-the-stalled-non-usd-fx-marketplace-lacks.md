# Assetto is the proven venue-builder the stalled non-USD FX marketplace lacks

> Assetto is a fourth Andres-Salcedo-owned, Solana-based non-USD stablecoin liquidity hub, but unlike the stale Agora/Noble/Plume legs it is run by the team that already built the #1 DEX and #1 money market on another ecosystem — making it the one lead that can actually build both the FX-swap venue and the Stablebond lending venue Etherfuse's roadmap keeps assuming exists.

## The overlooked overlap

Assetto (formerly Precept Labs) is building "a non-USD stablecoin liquidity hub on Solana featuring swaps and yield backed by Stablebonds," with the plan to back wrapped MXN/GBP/EUR/BRL/KRW tokens using CETES, TESOURO, KTB, GILTS and EUROB as treasury [wiki/entities/companies/assetto.md]. Its assignee is Andres Salcedo [wiki/entities/companies/assetto.md] — the same owner the existing synthesis flags for the Agora (AUSD USD leg + "joint FX marketplace"), Noble (non-USD FX DEX) and Plume (non-USD vaults) cluster. Assetto is therefore not a standalone Discovery lead; it is a **fourth member of the same non-USD FX-marketplace package**, and specifically the one that proposes to *be the venue itself* rather than supply a leg.

## Why Assetto is the strongest node in that cluster

The others are stale and pre-product. Assetto's team, by contrast, "previously built Meridian (the largest DEX on Movement) and Echelon (the largest money market on Aptos and Movement)" and has done "a full pivot to 100% Solana focus" for stablecoin ecosystem access [wiki/entities/companies/assetto.md]. That is the exact skill set — building deep swap liquidity and a money market — that the Agora/Noble/Plume legs need someone to execute. Bundling Assetto with that cluster turns four disconnected Nurture/Discovery leads into one package with a builder attached.

## Two Etherfuse gaps, one lead

**FX/swap side:** Assetto's swap hub is the on-Solana marketplace for Etherfuse's whole non-USD roster — directly serving the $1.55B non-USD stablecoin market (up 260% YoY) that Etherfuse positions itself to own [shared/ecosystems/stablecoins.md].

**Lending side:** Assetto's Phase 2 is "Echelon money market integration and revenue share" [wiki/entities/companies/assetto.md] — i.e., re-deploying the largest money market its team ever built as a Stablebond lending venue on Solana. That replicates the model in Case Study 5, where Stablebonds become yield-bearing collateral enabling non-USD (MXN/BRL) lending markets at effective borrowing costs below zero [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md], and it directly extends the only live precedent — the Loopscale × TESOURO BRL credit market, the first BRL credit market on Solana [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. On-chain lending markets for Stablebonds already run 15–20% APY on Solana [shared/bd/why-stablebonds-vs-direct-bonds.md], and Solana RWA TVL is at an all-time high [shared/master-knowledge-base.md] — so a second, battle-tested money-market operator for non-USD Stablebonds is high-value, not speculative.

## The cheap bootstrap

The gating blocker is Assetto's capital raise and engineering hires, and the near-zero-cost lever is already identified: Dave offered to support a Solana omnibus grant application ($50–100K) to seed the initial liquidity pools [wiki/entities/companies/assetto.md]. That grant, not a direct Etherfuse spend, is what stands up the FX-marketplace venue the cluster has lacked.

## Recommendation

Stop working Assetto as an isolated July-9 circle-back. Re-scope it as the venue anchor of the Andy-owned non-USD FX-marketplace package, prioritize the Solana grant application, and treat Phase 2 Echelon as a named second Solana lending venue alongside Loopscale rather than a vague "phase 2."
