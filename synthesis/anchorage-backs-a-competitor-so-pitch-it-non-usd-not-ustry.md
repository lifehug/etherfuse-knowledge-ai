# Anchorage already backs OpenEden — so the winning Anchorage pitch is non-USD coverage, not USD Treasuries

> Anchorage Digital financially backs OpenEden's TBILL, a direct competitor to Etherfuse's USTRY, which means any Etherfuse pitch built on US Treasuries walks into a conflict — the durable wedge is the 6-currency non-USD sovereign roster (MXN/BRL/KRW/GBP/EUR + 50 planned) that OpenEden structurally cannot offer.

Two sources in this slice collide in a way neither page notices. Etherfuse's competitor and sovereign-debt intelligence both record that **OpenEden (TBILL, tokenized US T-Bills) is backed by Anchorage Digital** [shared/ecosystems/sovereign-debt.md, shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. Etherfuse's own USTRY is a tokenized US Treasury Stablebond (~3.2% APY, Surus Trust/BNY Mellon custody) [shared/master-knowledge-base.md] — i.e. USTRY and Anchorage-backed TBILL are the *same product category*.

That reframes the Anchorage relationship entirely:

1. **Don't lead with USTRY.** Anchorage has already placed its bet on the USD tokenized-treasury layer via OpenEden; pitching USTRY invites a conflict and offers Anchorage nothing it doesn't already have exposure to.
2. **Lead with the non-USD moat.** Etherfuse's stated differentiator is that '95%+ of tokenized treasuries market is USD-only' and it uniquely covers **6 non-USD sovereign currencies with 50+ planned** and holds CNBV authorization no competitor has [shared/ecosystems/competitors.md, shared/ecosystems/sovereign-debt.md]. OpenEden cannot serve a bank that needs MXN, BRL, KRW, or KZT exposure — Etherfuse can.
3. **Anchorage's LATAM expansion needs exactly this.** Anchorage is reportedly expanding into Mexico/LATAM [wiki/entities/companies/anchorage-digital-bank.md], a region where the demanded instrument is local-currency yield (CETES, TESOURO), not more USD T-bills — precisely Etherfuse's live inventory.

The fact that Anchorage already underwrote a tokenized-treasury issuer is a *positive* signal (it understands and believes the model) — but it should steer the commercial conversation toward the non-USD sovereign coverage where Etherfuse is complementary rather than competitive. Framing the offer as 'the non-USD half of the tokenized-sovereign market Anchorage doesn't reach through OpenEden' is the credible, conflict-free wedge.
