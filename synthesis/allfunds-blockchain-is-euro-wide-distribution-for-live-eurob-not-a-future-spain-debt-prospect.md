# Allfunds Blockchain is EU-wide distribution for the already-live EUROB

> The Allfunds Blockchain page scopes it as a 'potential' channel for some future Spanish tokenized government debt, but Etherfuse already ships EUROB — a live EUR Stablebond backed by eight eurozone sovereigns including Spain — so the real, immediate play is distributing an existing product through Europe's largest fund network, not waiting on a Spain-specific instrument.

The Allfunds Blockchain wiki page frames the opportunity narrowly and speculatively: it is 'identified as a potential institutional distribution channel for tokenized government debt in Spain' with 'no formal engagement initiated to date' [wiki/entities/companies/allfunds-blockchain.md]. That framing badly undersells the fit, because it treats the product as hypothetical when a matching product is already live.

Etherfuse's EUROB is a live EUR-denominated Stablebond backed 1:1 by short-term treasury bills from eight eurozone sovereign issuers — and Spain is explicitly one of them — custodied by Spiko [wiki/markets/spain.md]. The authoritative bond roadmap confirms EUROB is 'Live' for Spain plus Austria, Belgium, Finland, France, Germany, Italy and Poland [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. So there is no need for a bespoke 'Spanish tokenized government debt' instrument to give Allfunds something to distribute; EUROB already contains Spanish sovereign exposure and ships today.

The strategic value of Allfunds is that it is 'one of Europe's largest fund distribution platforms' with 'a broad network of asset managers and institutional investors across Europe' [wiki/entities/companies/allfunds-blockchain.md] — a pan-European rail, not a Spain-only one. Pairing that with a live product turns this from a research note into a near-term distribution deal: EUROB (and GILTS for GBP) become the SKUs, Allfunds Blockchain the institutional channel.

The custody groundwork is also already moving in-country: Etherfuse is standing up a BBVA Spain depository account and BBVA Spain uniquely supports DocuSign-based corporate authorization (unlike BBVA Mexico), reducing the operational friction of a Spanish institutional launch [wiki/markets/spain.md]. Net: the highest-leverage first motion is to open engagement around EUROB distribution across Allfunds' European institutional base, rather than logging Allfunds as a future Spain-debt prospect.
