# A Kazakhstan Stablebond at 17–18% would be Etherfuse's single fattest-margin asset

> Kazakhstan government bonds yield ~17–18% and are classified non-security, which puts a KZT Stablebond above every current product on yield and lands it in the top partner-revenue and management-fee tiers — making Kazakhstan not just strategic but the most lucrative Stablebond per dollar of AUM.

The WhiteBIT partnership record notes Kazakhstan government bonds carry a yield of ~17–18% as of April 2026 and are classified as non-security tokens per Kazakhstan government guidance, which simplifies listing by avoiding securities-law overhead [wiki/entities/companies/whitebit.md]. That yield is materially above every currently live Stablebond — TESOURO (BRL) at ~13% is today's highest [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

Yield is not just a marketing figure here; it is the direct driver of Etherfuse's own economics. The partner revenue-share schedule pays 60 bps of AUM (the top tier) for any bond yielding >10%, versus 10–20 bps for the sub-6% products, and the management fee scales to 1.5% at the high-yield end versus 0.25% at the low end [shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md]. A KZT product at 17–18% pins both dials at their maximum. In per-dollar-of-AUM terms, one dollar in a KZT Stablebond earns Etherfuse roughly 3–6x what a dollar in CETES, USTRY, GILTS, or EUROB earns.

The same high yield also makes it the most attractive DeFi collateral / carry-trade asset in the roster (deposit 17–18% collateral, borrow lower-yield USDC), amplifying usage on the collateral-playbook and lending fronts.

Implication for prioritization: the extensive Kazakhstan pipeline (Halyk custody, NBK letter, AIFC SPC, KASE/AIX listing, Freedom/NIC distribution) is usually ranked on strategic-beachhead grounds. This adds a pure-margin argument — the product being launched there is structurally the most profitable Stablebond Etherfuse can ship — which should push the KZT launch up the queue independent of the geopolitical thesis.
