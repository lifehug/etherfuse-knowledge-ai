# The 36% Mexican interest tax is an unsold offshore-treasury wedge

> The same 36% Mexican interest-withholding tax that forced 9 Dots to hold its CETES/USTRY yield in a Japan entity recurs across deals as an unnamed deal-closer, meaning Etherfuse's headline ~6–7% CETES treasury yield is materially overstated for onshore holders and the offshore-yield/onshore-spend structure should be packaged as a product.

**The recurring fact.** 9 Dots couldn't run Mexican-side treasury at all because "a 36% interest tax on yields makes MX-side treasury management unviable through traditional channels" — so the structure Etherfuse agreed routes all yield-bearing instruments (CETES/USTRY) to the *Japan* entity, with the Mexican entity only ever receiving Stablebonds, liquidating to MXN, and paying local providers [wiki/entities/companies/9-dots.md]. The Japanese parent isn't incidental; it is the tax shelter that makes the yield worth holding.

**It is not a one-off.** The exact same 36% figure surfaces independently in the Reap card-collateral work, where Etherfuse's own treasury sources note an offshore holding structure delivering "tax efficiency up to 36% savings on yield" [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md]. Two unrelated deals, same number, same fix: hold the yield-bearing token in an offshore entity, spend locally.

**Why this matters for the pitch.** Every Mexican-treasury case study Etherfuse ships advertises gross CETES yield — e.g. the corporate-treasury study claims "~$600,000–$700,000 per year" on $10M at ~6–7% APY with no mention of withholding [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md], and the partner-revenue tiers pay bps off gross AUM yield [shared/bd/partner-revenue-tiers.md]. For a Mexican-resident corporate holder that headline is roughly a third too high after the interest tax; for an *offshore* holder of the same token it is accurate. That asymmetry is a selling point Etherfuse is leaving on the table: the dual-entity "offshore-holds-the-yield, onshore-spends-the-fiat" template is the thing that makes the corporate-treasury yield case actually true, and it generalizes to the stalled Amazon/Aeromexico-style idle-MXN pitches.

**The connective insight.** This is the same tax logic Etherfuse already deploys upmarket — the AIFC SPC pitch beats Goldman on "35% Mexican corporate tax vs. tax-deferred," and the AirTM/1Money Bermuda issuance route exists partly for tax/regulatory arbitrage [shared/partners/2026-05-13-airtm-jv-proposal.md]. 9 Dots shows the *operating-company* version of that same wedge, one tier down from institutional wrappers, and it should be written up as a repeatable structuring template rather than rediscovered deal by deal.
