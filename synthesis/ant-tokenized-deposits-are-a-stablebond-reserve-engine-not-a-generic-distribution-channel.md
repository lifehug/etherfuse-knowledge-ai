# Ant's tokenized-deposit push is a Stablebond reserve-and-float engine across the SC corridors

> Ant International's own stated top priority — expanding its Bettr Treasury platform and tokenized deposits — makes it a sovereign-yield reserve and settlement-float customer at 2B-transaction scale across exactly the Southeast Asian currencies Etherfuse is about to launch via Standard Chartered, a far larger and more specific wedge than the wiki's generic 'yield layer for partner banks' hook.

**The reframe.** The Ant International page hooks the deal as '[[concepts/stablebonds]] as yield layer for partner banks and wallets' and lists Ant's own top priority as 'Expanding Bettr Treasury platform and tokenized deposits' [wiki/entities/companies/ant-international.md]. Those two facts point at a much sharper motion than a generic distribution pitch: Ant is *itself building a treasury/tokenized-deposit product*, and Etherfuse ships the reserve leg and float-yield rails that sit underneath one.

**Why this is a reserve play.** Etherfuse's own materials show the exact structure: a tokenized-deposit or stablecoin issuer holds sovereign Stablebonds (USTRY/GILTS/EUROB) as its productive, auditable reserve instead of zero-yield cash or opaque USDT — the model pitched to the International Bank of Tajikistan ('the stablebond *is* the reserve — productive, sovereign-grade, auditable') [shared/partners/2026-05-26-international-bank-of-tajikistan.md]. Ant's 'tokenized deposits' are the same problem shape: they need a reserve and a yield source, which is what Stablebonds are.

**Why the corridor match matters.** Ant powers 2B+ transactions in emerging markets [wiki/entities/companies/ant-international.md], and the PSP float case study is built on precisely Ant's footprint — PHP, IDR, THB, VND settlement float parked in USTRY between 48–72h batch windows, corridors run at institutional mid instead of retail correspondent rates [shared/bd/case-study-psp-hongkong.md]. Those same currencies (Philippines, Malaysia, Indonesia, Vietnam) are the ones Etherfuse's 2026 roadmap schedules for Stablebond issuance via Standard Chartered FSS [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. So the pitch to Ant is not 'add a savings feature' — it's 'be the local-currency Stablebond reserve and float-yield engine for your treasury product across the exact markets we're issuing in next.'

**Why it's an easy structural fit.** The institutional-platform overview already ships tokenized deposits' full back-end: multi-chain issuance, KYB, proof-of-reserves, partner API, embedded wallets — 'a production-ready tokenization platform AND the compliance, reporting, fiat rails' [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md]. Ant would not have to build the reserve/attestation layer; it plugs in.

**Action.** Re-scope the Money20/20 Asia meeting around Bettr Treasury / tokenized deposits specifically: lead with the USTRY reserve-leg + SE-Asia float-yield case study, and tie it to the Standard Chartered issuance roadmap so Ant sees local-currency Stablebonds landing in its own corridors. This is a much larger revenue surface (reserve AUM + float + FX) than the current 'not set' priority and undefined contract value the page carries [wiki/entities/companies/ant-international.md].
