# Spiko is the warm bridge to Amundi, not a cold institutional prospect

> Etherfuse's own confirmed EUROB custodian is the same firm that just launched a $100M tokenized UCITS fund with Amundi (€2.3T AUM) on the same chain Etherfuse runs on — making Spiko the fastest warm path to Europe's largest asset manager, a link the Amundi page raises as an open question but never resolves.

## The overlooked connection

The Amundi page treats Amundi as a dormant, no-contact strategic-watch entity and explicitly asks in its Open Questions: *"Is there a direct engagement opportunity, or is Amundi best approached via Spiko or BNP Paribas?"* [wiki/entities/companies/amundi.md]. It never answers this — and the answer is already sitting in the neighborhood.

Spiko is not a neutral third party to Etherfuse. It is the **confirmed, authoritative-source-verified institutional custodian for Etherfuse's live EUROB Stablebond**, holding EU sovereign debt from eight countries [wiki/entities/companies/Spiko.md]. This is corroborated across the BMA business plan, the Tala DDQ, the Kaia overview, and the meta-briefing [wiki/entities/companies/Spiko.md]. EUROB is a live product with Spiko named as custodian on the public overview [shared/overview.md] and master KB [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

The same Spiko simultaneously partnered with **Amundi (€2.3T AUM)** to launch the SAFO tokenized UCITS fund — $100M, on Stellar and Ethereum, powered by Chainlink NAV feeds with BNP collateral [wiki/entities/companies/amundi.md][wiki/entities/companies/Spiko.md].

## Why this is high-leverage

1. **Institutional partner target of the highest order.** Company goals prioritize "institutional partners who launch their stablecoin strategies on us" and "the biggest markets." Amundi is Europe's largest asset manager. Winning it as an allocator, distributor, or UCITS-wrapper partner for Stablebonds is a landmark.

2. **The intro path is already operational, not cold.** Etherfuse works with Spiko daily on EUROB custody. Rather than cold-outreach to Amundi, Etherfuse can ask its own custodian for a warm introduction to the team Spiko just executed a $100M fund launch with.

3. **Shared infrastructure lowers the technical friction.** Spiko holds ~$495M on Stellar, ranks #2 on the Stellar RWA leaderboard, and is a RedStone oracle partner — exactly Etherfuse's stack (Stellar + RedStone) [wiki/entities/companies/Spiko.md][shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. Etherfuse's CMO already drafted a reply positioning CETES as a "natural complement to overnight funds" like SAFO [wiki/entities/companies/Spiko.md]. That angle generalizes: EUROB and the non-USD Stablebond roster are complementary sovereign-yield legs to Amundi's overnight-liquidity UCITS product.

4. **Amundi has proven it will tokenize on Stellar via a UCITS wrapper.** That is precisely the regulated European retail vehicle the neighborhood repeatedly reaches for (SPVs, ETNs, Allfunds distribution). Amundi is the one entity that has already shipped a tokenized UCITS fund on Etherfuse's chain — a distinct, retail-legal wrapper Etherfuse cannot easily build itself.

## The action

Upgrade the Amundi page from passive watch to an active Spiko-mediated intro track: use the existing EUROB custody relationship to request a warm introduction to Amundi's tokenization team, and lead with EUROB/CETES as sovereign-yield complements to the SAFO overnight structure. Trust caveat: the Amundi–Spiko SAFO details are sourced only to reported xscout briefings [wiki/entities/companies/amundi.md], so confirm the partnership specifics through Spiko directly before pitching.
