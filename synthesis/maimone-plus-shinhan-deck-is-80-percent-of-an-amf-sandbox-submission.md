# The Maimone Legal Response + Shinhan Compliance Deck = 80% of an AMF Sandbox Package

> The 20-question legal response approved for external distribution to Maimone & Associados and the compliance infrastructure presentation delivered to Shinhan together contain every substantive element an AMF fintech sandbox submission would require — making the actual preparation cost for AMF engagement far lower than the wiki pages imply.

## What the AMF Would Ask

The AMF's fintech sandbox ("FinTech, Innovation and Competitiveness" division) requires firms to demonstrate: product structure and legal mechanics, investor protection and ownership rights, custody arrangements, AML/KYC compliance architecture, cross-jurisdiction regulatory treatment, bankruptcy remoteness, and ongoing reporting/attestation [wiki/entities/companies/autorite-des-marches-financiers.md] [wiki/markets/France.md].

## What Already Exists

**The Maimone response** [shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md] covers all 20 of these topics in externally-approved language: token ownership mechanics (Q2), asset segregation and bankruptcy remoteness under both Mexican and Swiss law (Q3, Q15, Q16), CNBV resolution scope including non-Mexican assets (Q5, Q12), AML architecture with specific FIU credentials (Q11), custody model per jurisdiction (Q8, Q13, Q14), private offering structure (Q7), and open-end redemption mechanics (Q20). Critically, Q6 specifically addresses how EUROB tokens relate to EU/national law — the exact jurisdiction the AMF cares about.

**The Shinhan compliance presentation** [shared/partners/2026-06-05-shinhan-compliance-presentation.md] documents the operational compliance stack in visual, regulator-friendly format: KYB risk scoring (40% client profile / 20% geography / 20% products / 20% channel), biometric KYC with on-chain wallet mapping, institutional and user tax reporting with monthly PDFs, multi-chain transaction monitoring, AML screening against OFAC/EU/UN/UK lists plus local jurisdiction lists, DeFi wallet scanning, and continuous proof-of-reserves attestation.

## The Gap

The missing 20% is France-specific: translating the framework into French regulatory terminology, mapping to specific AMF sandbox application requirements, and engaging French legal counsel to confirm EUROB's classification under MiCA (EMT vs. ART vs. DLT Pilot Regime — an open question flagged in the MiCA concept page [wiki/concepts/MiCA.md]). But the substantive compliance architecture and legal reasoning already exist in externally-distributable form.

## Implication

The AMF wiki page and France market page frame AMF engagement as a major future workstream. In reality, the compliance documentation has already been produced for other purposes. The marginal effort to adapt it for AMF is a legal translation exercise, not a compliance architecture build.
