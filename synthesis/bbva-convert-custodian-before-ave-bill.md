# BBVA: convert our custodian into a white-label partner before the Murat AVE bill lets them build a peso stablecoin in-house

> BBVA is simultaneously our CETES custodian, a $100M/$7M top pipeline deal, and a direct participant in the BIS tokenization architecture — but Mexico's new AVE stablecoin framework gives BBVA a legal path to launch its own peso product, so the window to lock them in as a partner on yield (which AVEs structurally cannot offer) is now, not later.

## Three BBVA facts that only matter when combined

1. **BBVA is already our infrastructure-deep custodian** for CETES and FX, a Category I institution with 19.57% capitalization [shared/overview.md][shared/regulatory-overview.md].
2. **BBVA is the single largest pipeline opportunity** at $100M TVL and $7.0M annual revenue, in Negotiation [shared/bd/pipeline-valuation-snapshot-2026-06.md].
3. **BBVA is not exploring tokenization abstractly — it was a direct participant in BIS Project Agorá,** which demonstrated atomic settlement across MXN, USD, EUR, GBP, CHF, JPY and KRW using tokenized reserves and commercial-bank deposits [shared/research/2026-06-01-tokenization-stablecoin-fx-tam.md].

## The risk the AVE bill introduces

Mexico's Murat/AVE bill creates the first legal path for a compliant peso stablecoin and explicitly warns: "BBVA Mexico, Banorte, or Citibanamex could use this framework to launch their own peso stablecoins. They'd have distribution advantages on payments" [shared/products/2026-05-12-murat-bill-stablebond-mxne-positioning.md]. A bank that already sits inside the BIS tokenization stack (Agorá) and already custodies our CETES has, in principle, much of what it needs to disintermediate us on a peso payment token.

## Why we still hold the high ground — and must act on it

The same bill codifies that **yield-bearing tokenized securities (Stablebonds) sit on the LMV securities track, while AVEs are zero-yield payment tokens** — "Etherfuse's moat is yield — which AVEs explicitly cannot offer" [shared/products/2026-05-12-murat-bill-stablebond-mxne-positioning.md]. BBVA can build a peso payment rail; it cannot legally bolt yield onto an AVE. So the durable partnership is to position Etherfuse as BBVA's yield layer — the CETES Stablebond / MXNe revenue-share engine — rather than letting BBVA conclude it can do the whole thing itself.

The revenue-share economics are already built and scale with their distribution: at $50M+ MXNe market cap, customers receive 60% of CETES yield while Etherfuse keeps 30%, and EF margin improves with scale [shared/products/mxne-customer-revenue-share.md]. For a bank with BBVA's distribution, that is a co-branded yield product, not a competitive threat.

## Timing

Nothing in the AVE bill forces operational change before mid-2027, and the comfortable IFPE-application start is September 2026 [shared/products/2026-05-12-murat-bill-stablebond-mxne-positioning.md]. That 18-month window is precisely when BBVA will decide build-vs-partner. Closing the $100M BBVA deal as a yield/custody partnership before BBVA scopes an in-house AVE is the single highest-dollar, time-boxed move in the Mexico book.
