# Named yield-bearing business accounts for China-trade SMEs is a wedge Bitso can't match

> bmoni and BeKey independently surfaced the same unmet Mexican need — foreign SMEs (heavily China-trade) who cannot get bank accounts and want named virtual business accounts that also earn yield — a product Etherfuse can uniquely deliver because IFPAs like Bitso legally cannot pay yield and cannot issue accounts in the client's own name.

The same demand pattern shows up from two separate partners in the same neighborhood, which turns an anecdote into a vertical:

- BeKey's on-the-ground lead Natasha: 'there's a lot of people here who are not from Mexico, who do a lot of trade with China... it's really difficult to get a bank account in Mexico if you're a foreigner,' and crucially they need the account in the client's own name, not BeKey's, because 'their invoices would say BeKey and not their company name' [shared/partners/bekey.md].
- The BMONI onboarding meeting logs the same request as an explicit Etherfuse action: 'Dave to investigate Mexico virtual business account structure (legal-representative model) for foreign SMEs' [shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bmoni-etherfuse-kyb-business-onboarding.md].

The defensibility is spelled out in the transcript itself: to issue virtual accounts you must be an IFPA ('mini bank'), and 'one of the limitations of an IFPA is you can't offer yield' — which is exactly why Bitso (an IFPA) routes to Etherfuse for yield, and why Bitso still cannot open accounts in the customer's own name [shared/partners/bekey.md]. Etherfuse's edge is the combination competitors can't stack: a legal-representative + KYB flow that produces a named business account, paired with CETES/MXNe yield on the idle balance — the same treasury-and-ramp mechanics already productized for Baubap [shared/partners/baubap-integration.md].

This is not a technical build ('it's more of a product solution,' Dave notes) but a packaged legal-rep-plus-KYB workflow monetizable with a hard onboarding fee, riding the free-KYB API. It converts the highest-friction problem in the Mexico corridor into a differentiated, repeatable SME product with guaranteed China-trade transaction volume behind it.
