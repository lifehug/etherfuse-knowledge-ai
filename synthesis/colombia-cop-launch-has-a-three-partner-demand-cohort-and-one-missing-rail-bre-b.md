# Colombia COP: a three-partner launch cohort waiting on a Bre-B off-ramp

> The in-flight COP Stablebond already has at least three independent partners pulling on Colombia (Addi, Tempo's enterprise contractor marketplace, and Cobre), but the operational gap that none of those deals can close on its own is a COP off-ramp — Tempo names exactly which rail it needs: Bre-B.

An existing finding ties Addi alone to the COP Stablebond Etherfuse is building with BBVA for next quarter. The neighborhood shows that is understating it — COP demand is stacking from at least three independent directions, the same pattern flagged for Argentina and Bolivia, except here the product is already in flight.

**The demand cohort:**
- **Addi** — Colombian BNPL, $300M raised, home currency COP, surfaced at Fit 9/10 [wiki/entities/companies/addi.md].
- **Tempo** — names a **Colombian Peso (COP) Marketplace** as its *primary* partnership opportunity: a "super large enterprise" that wants to pay contractors in a COP stablecoin, and explicitly notes "Etherfuse is working on COP with BBVA, targeting next quarter" [shared/partners/tempo.md].
- **Cobre** — "runs significant daily cash flows across Colombia, Mexico, and the US and is actively looking for treasury/liquidity solutions," already moved Nurture→Negotiation [shared/meetings/2026-04-30-cobre-meet-the-team.md].

**The missing rail.** Tempo spells out the operational requirement no other source states: the COP marketplace needs to "off-ramp via **Bre-B** ('brebbe') to contractor bank accounts" [shared/partners/tempo.md]. Bre-B is Colombia's instant-payment system — the COP analogue to the PIX rail Etherfuse already runs for TESOURO/Brazil [shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bloque-etherfuse-onboarding-kickoff.md] and the SPEI rail it runs for MXNe/Mexico [shared/partners/tala.md]. A COP Stablebond with no COP off-ramp is exactly the MXNe-without-SPEI failure mode; the BBVA collateral leg is being built but the fiat egress rail is the unbuilt piece.

**Why it matters now.** This converts "COP is on the roadmap" into "COP has a ready launch cohort and a single named integration blocker." BBVA is the shared thread — Etherfuse is building COP collateral with BBVA, and Tempo separately notes it is "very close to BBVA" [shared/partners/tempo.md] — so the same relationship can plausibly carry both the collateral and the Bre-B banking connection. The fastest revenue path is to sequence the COP launch around Bre-B integration and line up Addi (consumer savings/BNPL float), Tempo's enterprise (contractor payouts), and Cobre (treasury) as three distinct go-live customers rather than treating COP as a single-partner pilot.
