# The regulatory pack ARQ's compliance team requested already exists — reactivation is near-zero-cost

> ARQ's stall is a documentation gap Etherfuse has already closed: the exact items its compliance team asked for (CNBV no-action letter, Swiss-law structure, proof-of-reserves) are fully answered in an existing, externally-distributable 20-question legal response prepared for another LATAM law firm.

ARQ's Florencia Abal requested a written brief plus a regulatory/audit pack — specifically the CNBV no-action letter, the Swiss-law structure, and proof-of-reserves — for internal compliance validation, and the deal then went dark [wiki/entities/companies/arq-finance.md]. An open question on the ARQ page is even whether that pack 'has been formally prepared and sent, or only requested' [wiki/entities/companies/arq-finance.md]. It has effectively already been prepared — for someone else.

The Maimone & Associados response (a formal answer to a Brazilian law firm's 20-question inquiry, marked approved for external distribution) covers each of ARQ's three asks in institutional detail: CNBV Resolution P090/2024 and its certified *fe de hechos* with public links (Q4, Q9, Q12) [shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md]; the Swiss DLT Act Art. 973d / 242a–242b bankruptcy-remoteness structure and its interaction with Mexican insolvency law (Q15, Q16) [shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md]; and the weekly/monthly proof-of-reserves attestations by BHR ENW with hosted links (Q10, Q18) [shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md]. It even directly addresses foreign-asset custody and Brazil-specific concerns (usury caps, Q19) — relevant since Brazil is one of ARQ's four markets.

Because ARQ is a stablecoin-native neobank whose compliance ask is precisely what this document answers, re-engagement costs almost nothing: send the Brazil-tailored Maimone Q&A alongside the standard proof-of-reserves and CNBV links [shared/master-knowledge-base.md]. The deal moved to Nurture on an unanswered proposal, not a lost one — closing a documentation gap that is already closed is the fastest available path to reactivating a 2M-customer, $10B-volume account.
