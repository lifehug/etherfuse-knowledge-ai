# Argentina: stacked partner demand, zero roadmap coverage

> At least four independent partners/prospects (Adyen, Airtm, Alfred Pay, Meta) all touch Argentina, yet ARS appears nowhere on Etherfuse's official Stablebond roadmap — a scheduling blind spot even sharper than Colombia, because partners are already penciling Argentina launches for Q2/Q3 2026.

Adyen — the focal entity — is flagged as a high-value settlement-float prospect specifically because its operations span **Mexico, Argentina, and the USA** [wiki/entities/companies/adyen.md]. Argentina recurs across the neighborhood as a market partners actively want:

- **Airtm** lists Argentina as a **priority country with a Q2 2026 target**, and even pre-names the product (`AirARS`) backed by new Etherfuse issuance — Argentina/Mexico are explicitly its "first deployment targets" [shared/partners/airtm-partnership-proposal.md][shared/partners/airtm.md].
- **Alfred Pay** already operates in Argentina under a **VASP license** and wants a replicable local-balance/yield model "across all their markets" [shared/partners/alfred-pay-2026-04-22.md].
- **Meta** flagged **Argentina as a high-interest market** alongside Brazil and Nigeria in its treasury/repatriation pilot discussion [shared/partners/meetings/2026-04-02-meta-meeting.md].

The blind spot: **Argentina (ARS) does not appear anywhere on the authoritative Stablebond roadmap** — not in the live set, not in 2026 Q3, not in the long 2026 Q4 list that includes dozens of smaller and less-liquid sovereigns (Afghanistan, Belarus, Turkmenistan, etc.) [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. So multiple partners are scheduling Argentina products for dates the company has no corresponding issuance plan for.

This mirrors the Colombia finding structurally, but is **worse on roadmap coverage** (Colombia at least sits on Stellar's Q4 2026 roadmap; Argentina sits on no roadmap at all) and **better on partner readiness** (Airtm and Alfred already have live or licensed Argentine operations to distribute through).

**Caveat / why it's medium not high:** Argentina's capital controls and 20–50%+ inflation make a clean short-duration T-bill Stablebond harder than CETES/TESOURO, which is likely why it was skipped. But the partner pull suggests the right first move may not be a sovereign bond at all — it's the **Brale/MXNe-style peso-stablecoin pattern** (Airtm's `AirARS`, an Alfred-branded ARS balance) backed by whatever local instrument is viable, the same wrap-first approach Etherfuse already runs for the Mexican peso [shared/partners/2023-12-14-brale-mxne-proposal.md][shared/partners/airtm-partnership-proposal.md]. **Decision: put ARS on the roadmap explicitly and let Airtm/Alfred volume commitments set its priority, rather than leaving four partners pointed at a product that isn't scheduled.**
