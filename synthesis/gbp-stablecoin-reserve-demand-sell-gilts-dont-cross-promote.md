# GBP stablecoin demand is stacking — sell GILTS reserves, not cross-promotion

> Agant and Bloque's TGBP are two independent GBP-stablecoin issuers Etherfuse is touching right now, and both map onto the exact reserve-backing model Etherfuse already runs for MXNe/AirTM — meaning Agant should be reframed from a 'complementary peer' into a GILTS reserve customer issuing under Etherfuse's Bermuda license.

Agant is logged as a *complementary, non-competitive* relationship — "they tokenize GBP/UK Gilts while Etherfuse focuses on MXN/CETES" — with the offering reduced to "grant support" and "mutual cross-promotion" [wiki/entities/companies/agant.md]. That framing leaves money on the table. Agant is building "tokenized products around UK Gilts and government debt" [wiki/entities/companies/agant.md] — which is precisely the asset Etherfuse already ships live as GILTS [shared/bond-roadmap.md]. A GBP stablecoin issuer holding GILTS as reserves is the identical structure Etherfuse documents for stablecoin collateral: issuer mints local-currency coin, parks reserves in the matching Stablebond, reserves earn sovereign yield instead of sitting idle [wiki/concepts/stablecoin-collateral.md]. The collateral page even lists "GILTS (UK) ~3.4% → GBP stablecoin reserves" as an explicit use case [wiki/concepts/stablecoin-collateral.md]. The proven template is Brale/MXNe (CETES-backed peso coin, 10–90% tiered rev-share) [shared/partners/2023-12-14-brale-mxne-proposal.md] and the AirTM JV (Etherfuse owns issuance/custody, partner owns distribution) [shared/partners/2026-05-13-airtm-jv-proposal.md].

This is not a one-off. In the same neighborhood, Bloque asked for "UK expansion via TGBP starting at ~$1M/month" with a "stablecoin ramp + yield product," and Dave committed to intro the TGBP team [shared/meetings/2026-04-15-bloque-etherfuse-onboarding-kickoff.md]. So Etherfuse is independently in front of at least two GBP-stablecoin programs (Agant, TGBP) that all need the same thing — a yield-bearing GBP reserve asset Etherfuse already issues.

The Bermuda piece closes the loop. Agant *itself* introduced Etherfuse to Christos at the BMA, and the DABA framework was confirmed as a strong fit for tokenized sovereign debt with Section 8 notification-only new-product launches [wiki/entities/companies/bma.md]. AirTM and others are already routing branded local-currency issuance through that same Bermuda license [shared/partners/2026-05-13-airtm-jv-proposal.md]. The cleanest structure for Agant is therefore: issue its GBP coin under Etherfuse's in-flight Bermuda license, backed by GILTS reserves, on the Brale/AirTM rev-share model — rather than letting Agant stand up its own GBP product and stack of relationships. That converts a 'discovery / cross-promo' lead into recurring reserve-yield revenue and another GILTS distribution channel.
