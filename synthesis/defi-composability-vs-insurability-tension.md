# DeFi composability quietly undermines the KYC/insurability story

> Etherfuse markets DeFi secondary-market composability as a core differentiator, but the same circulating-in-uncontrolled-pools behavior is precisely what complicates crypto-crime underwriting and weakens the "fully KYC'd, tracked-wallet" compliance narrative — a tension no single page reconciles.

On the Marsh call, Etherfuse stressed that assets are "fully KYC'd; tokens cannot be used for payments and can only be purchased through Etherfuse's app into tracked wallets" — then immediately conceded that "DeFi pools exist where assets can be traded secondarily" [01KP6A31XS2VFZRADFHHPPPQ1V]. Marsh logged this as an open underwriting concern: tokens "may circulate beyond the primary KYC'd purchase channel" [teams/cfo/legal/mexico/2026-04-14-etherfuse-mma-crypto-crime-policy.md].

**That secondary circulation is not an edge case — it's the dominant behavior, and we actively promote it.** The Etherfuse profile lists DeFi composability as a headline achievement: Loopscale (BRL credit), Templar (Stellar lending), Orca (KTB LP), plus Jupiter/Whirlpool/SDEX/Kuru swap routing [wiki/entities/companies/Etherfuse.md], and the overview adds Curve and Uniswap [shared/overview.md]. Most tellingly, the formal legal answer to Brazilian counsel states that "the majority of users engage with their Stablebonds through DeFi secondary markets — including liquidity pools on Uniswap and Curve Finance — rather than holding to redemption" [shared/partners/2026-06-01-maimone-associados-response.md].

So the differentiator (open, composable, tradeable everywhere) and the institutional comfort story (closed, KYC'd, tracked, insurable) are in direct conflict. This surfaces a concrete strategic choice the company has not yet made explicit: either (a) define an insured/compliance perimeter that covers only the primary-minted, identity-mapped supply and price insurance/attestations to that subset, or (b) accept that the moment a token hits a public pool the KYC-to-wallet mapping and any token-theft coverage lapse. Decision-useful because both the Marsh policy scope and the regulator-facing compliance pitch (institutional-platform-overview's identity-to-wallet mapping [shared/institutional-platform-overview.md]) depend on resolving it the same way. Recommendation: explicitly bifurcate the narrative — "insured & compliance-tracked primary supply" vs. "freely composable secondary supply" — so DeFi reach stops eroding the institutional risk story.
