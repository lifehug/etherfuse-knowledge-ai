# Amazon Mexico: sell the seller float, not Amazon corporate

> Amazon Mexico's wishlist entry is stalled on 'enterprise BD is complex,' but its two largest idle-MXN liabilities — marketplace seller-disbursement float and Amazon Pay consumer balances — map onto three products Etherfuse already ships, and the seller side is reachable as an SMB-treasury play without ever closing Amazon corporate.

The Amazon Mexico page lists the entity as Wishlist with **zero outreach** and a single self-imposed blocker: "Amazon's enterprise structure makes BD complex" [wiki/entities/companies/amazon-mexico.md]. That blocker assumes the only deal is a top-down corporate one. It isn't.

**Amazon Mexico holds two distinct idle-MXN pools, each matching a shipped product:**

1. **Seller-disbursement float** — proceeds held pending payout to marketplace sellers. This is structurally identical to the SMB-lender reserve case study (reserves that must be held but earn ~0% in bank deposits, converted to ~7% CETES while remaining same-day redeemable) and to the issuer settlement-float mechanic where idle balances "earn the local sovereign yield" instead of sitting at 0% [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md][shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md].
2. **Amazon Pay consumer stored value** — the exact "auto-yield on float" / yield-bearing-savings feature from the digital-wallet case study, where idle balances are allocated to the highest-yield Stablebond and the platform earns a 10–60 bps revenue share [shared/bd/generic-case-studies.md].

**The non-obvious route around the BD blocker:** Amazon Mexico marketplace sellers *are* the SMB population the corporate-treasury and SMB-lender case studies were written for. Etherfuse can offer CETES-backed reserve yield directly to those sellers — or wrap it into a yield-bearing card via the Visa local-issuer collateral model, where any in-country Visa BIN holder (e.g. Efeevo, ~200K-card cap before a local entity is required) issues against CETES collateral with no Amazon corporate sign-off needed [shared/bd/visa-local-issuer-card-collateral.md]. This reaches the same economic flows without the multi-quarter enterprise sale, in a market with 1,104 fintech startups and 70M users already in motion [shared/master-knowledge-base.md].

**Why now:** the easy first move is not "research Amazon's fintech team" (the page's current next step) but packaging the seller-treasury + Amazon Pay auto-yield case studies into a one-pager and routing it through an existing Mexican BaaS/issuer partner already onboarding with Etherfuse, then using seller adoption as the proof point to open the corporate conversation later — the same land-then-expand pattern used elsewhere in the pipeline.
