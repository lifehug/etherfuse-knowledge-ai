# Uzbekistan is a shovel-ready sovereign-bond market with a government that already tokenized its bonds — and we have no one on it

> Uzbekistan has already done government-bond tokenization (HUMO, July 2025), is legislating stablecoin and tokenized-bond rules right now, sits on our 2026 Q4 roadmap, and matches our exact playbook — yet appears nowhere in the pipeline, making it the single clearest unworked easy win.

## The convergence

Three independent sources line up on Uzbekistan:

1. **The government already tokenized sovereign bonds.** HUMO Digital, a subsidiary of Uzbekistan's national interbank payment system, issued a state-registered token backed by government bonds in July 2025, logged in the national crypto-asset register [shared/research/raw/2026-06-04-western-mena-government-bond-signals.md]. This is effectively sovereign-bond tokenization done by a state entity — proof of political will, not just openness.

2. **The legal rails are being built on a timeline that favors a first mover.** Stablecoins became legally recognized as a payment method on Jan 1, 2026; a framework for tokenized equities and bonds is in progress for early 2026; the 2026–2030 FinTech Strategy is approved [shared/research/uzbekistan-sovereign-bond-pitch-stats.md]. A partnership now "would align with the 2026–2030 FinTech Strategy, giving the pitch a regulatory tailwind rather than headwind" [shared/research/uzbekistan-sovereign-bond-pitch-stats.md].

3. **The demand profile is textbook Stablebond.** 40% unbanked, only 10% save formally despite a 32% savings rate ("the issue is channel, not behavior"), $16.1B remittances (18% of GDP), 82% mobile penetration, median age 27.9, and a domestic bond market that doubled issuance 2023–2025 [shared/research/uzbekistan-sovereign-bond-pitch-stats.md]. This is the same "informal savings, no formal rail" gap Stablebonds were designed to close [shared/products/stablebond-thesis-neon.md].
